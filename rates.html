Why No Insurance?

Do we take insurance?

Short answer: No. Longer answer:

We chose private-pay because we prioritize your privacy and your goals over insurance company rules.

When insurance pays for therapy, they require a diagnosis code — something that goes on your permanent medical record. Even if you're just working through a life transition or wanting to understand yourself better, insurance wants a label. We don't think you need one to deserve support.

Insurers also decide how many sessions you "need" and what treatment modalities are "covered." That often means shorter sessions, stricter timelines, and a case manager who doesn't know you making decisions about your care. We don't play that game.

Going private-pay means:

  • ✓ Privacy — Your records stay between us
  • ✓ Flexibility — We decide session length together
  • ✓ Control — You choose how often to meet
  • ✓ Focus — We treat you, not a diagnosis code

Have out-of-network benefits? We can provide a superbill for you to submit yourself. Just know that sending it to your insurer means they get access to your diagnosis and treatment details — that's your call.

We also keep a few sliding scale spots available. Support shouldn't be a luxury, and we don't think privacy should cost you either.

Under the No Surprises Act, you have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate explaining how much your medical care will cost. We are happy to provide this upon request.
