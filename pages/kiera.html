My Story

I've always believed in the healing power of creativity. Before I became a therapist, I saw how art helped me process my own experiences — things I couldn't put into words yet, but could express through color, shape, and form.

That belief led me to become an art therapist. Now I get to help other women use creativity as a pathway to healing. You don't need to be "artistic" — you just need to be open to expressing yourself in new ways.

I specialize in working with adult women because I understand the unique pressures we face — the expectations, the juggling acts, the way we often put ourselves last. Whether you're dealing with anxiety, navigating a major life change, or grieving a loss, I'm here to help you find your way through.

"You don't have to be an artist to benefit from art therapy. You just have to be human. Sometimes the things we can't say in words come through in color, texture, and form. That's where healing begins."

— Kiera

What Is Art Therapy?

It's not about making pretty pictures. It's about expressing what words can't capture.

Art therapy combines traditional talk therapy with creative expression. Sometimes our feelings are too big, too complex, or too buried for words alone. Art gives them another pathway out.

How It Works

  • 🎨 No art skills required — This isn't an art class. It's about the process, not the product.
  • 🧠 Accesses different parts of the brain — Art activates areas that verbal processing doesn't reach.
  • 💭 Reveals patterns — What you create often shows what you can't yet say.
  • ✨ Creates distance — Looking at your experience outside yourself can bring clarity.
