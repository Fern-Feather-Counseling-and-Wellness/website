### 1. ABOUT OUR PRACTICE

**Our Approach**

Fern & Feather Wellness Center provides trauma-informed, integrative psychotherapy services. We combine evidence-based clinical approaches with somatic (body-based) interventions, rooted in an understanding of nervous system regulation, attachment theory, and neurobiology.

Our practice is:
- **LGBTQ+ affirming** — We honor all gender identities, sexual orientations, and relationship configurations
- **Anti-oppressive** — We recognize how systems of power, marginalization, and intergenerational trauma impact mental health
- **Neuroscience-informed** — We integrate current research on trauma, the brain, and the body
- **Collaborative** — You are the expert on your own experience; we work together toward your goals

### 2. THE THERAPEUTIC PROCESS

**What to Expect**

Psychotherapy is a collaborative process through which we explore your concerns, develop insight, build skills, and work toward your goals. Therapy may involve:
- Talking about thoughts, feelings, and experiences
- Learning about how your nervous system responds to stress and trauma
- Developing coping and regulation skills
- Processing difficult memories (when you're ready)
- Identifying patterns and exploring new ways of relating to yourself and others

**Therapy Can Be Challenging**

It is normal and expected that therapy will sometimes bring up difficult emotions. Discussing painful experiences, examining long-held patterns, or making changes in your life can feel uncomfortable. This is often part of the healing process. We will work together to ensure you have the support and skills to navigate this safely.

**No Guaranteed Outcomes**

While research shows psychotherapy is effective for many people, we cannot guarantee specific results. The success of therapy depends on many factors, including your active participation, the quality of our working relationship, and factors outside our control.

### 3. YOUR RIGHTS AS A CLIENT

You have the right to:

- **Be treated with dignity and respect** regardless of your identity, background, or circumstances
- **Ask questions** about your treatment, our qualifications, or any aspect of our work together
- **Decline to answer** any question or discuss any topic
- **Request a different approach** if something isn't working for you
- **Receive culturally responsive care** that honors your identities and experiences
- **End treatment** at any time (though we recommend discussing this first)
- **Access your records** as permitted by law
- **File a complaint** if you believe your rights have been violated (see Section 9)
- **Have a support person** present during sessions if clinically appropriate

### 4. CONFIDENTIALITY & PRIVACY

**Your Information Is Protected**

What you share in therapy is confidential. We will not disclose your participation in therapy or the content of our sessions without your written permission, except in the following circumstances:

**Limits to Confidentiality (When We Are Required to Disclose):**

1. **Imminent danger to yourself:** If you communicate a specific, imminent threat to harm yourself and appear to have the intent and means to carry it out, we may need to take action to protect you, which could include contacting emergency services or designated family members.

2. **Imminent danger to others:** If you communicate a specific, imminent threat to harm an identifiable person, we may be required to warn that person and/or contact law enforcement (this is known as the "duty to warn").

3. **Abuse of vulnerable persons:** If we have reasonable cause to suspect abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a child, elderly person, or person with a disability who is unable to protect themselves, we are required by law to report this to the appropriate authorities.

4. **Court orders:** If we are ordered by a court to release records or testify, we may be required to comply.

5. **Medical emergency:** In a medical emergency during a session, we may share necessary information with emergency medical personnel.

**Additional Privacy Considerations:**

- **Minors:** If you are under 18, parents/guardians generally have legal rights to information about your treatment. We will discuss with you and your family how information will be shared.

- **Couples/Relationship therapy:** In couples or relationship therapy, we do not keep secrets between partners. We ask that you share important information with your partner(s) in session, or we may need to discuss together how to handle sensitive information.

- **Group therapy:** Confidentiality in groups depends on all members agreeing to maintain privacy. We cannot guarantee other group members will keep information confidential.

**Technology & Telehealth:**

We use secure, HIPAA-compliant platforms for telehealth sessions and communication. However, no electronic communication is 100% secure. Please ensure you are in a private location for telehealth sessions.
