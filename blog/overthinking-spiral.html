Overthinking and Mental Spirals: How to Break the Loop

Overthinking • 6 min read

If your brain runs twenty tabs at once and still feels like it has not solved anything, you are not alone. Overthinking often starts as an attempt to feel prepared and safe, but it can quickly become a loop of rumination, second-guessing, and exhaustion.

What Overthinking Looks Like

  • Replaying conversations and decisions for hours.
  • Needing certainty before taking action.
  • Getting stuck between options and doing neither.
  • Trouble sleeping because your mind will not downshift.

Why It Keeps Going

Overthinking promises control. If you think hard enough, maybe you can prevent mistakes, rejection, or regret. But the mind rarely finds final certainty. Instead, each thought creates three new ones, and your nervous system stays on alert.

How to Interrupt a Spiral

  • Name the pattern: “I am in a rumination loop.”
  • Shift from analysis to action with one small next step.
  • Set a “decision window” so thoughts cannot run forever.
  • Use grounding (temperature, movement, breath) to settle your body first.

When Therapy Helps

Therapy can help you understand what overthinking is protecting you from and build real tolerance for uncertainty. You can learn to make values-based choices without perfect certainty and feel more present in your own life.
