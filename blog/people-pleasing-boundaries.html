People Pleasing and Boundaries: How to Stop Self-Abandoning

People Pleasing • 6 min read

People pleasing usually starts as a safety strategy: keep everyone happy, avoid conflict, stay connected. Over time, it can become self-abandonment. You may look “easygoing” while privately feeling resentful, exhausted, or invisible.

Common Signs

  • You say yes before checking if you actually can.
  • You feel guilty when you set limits.
  • You over-explain simple boundaries.
  • You lose track of what you want.

Boundary Language That Helps

  • “I can't commit to that this week.”
  • “I need some time before I decide.”
  • “I'm not available for that, but I can do this instead.”

Expect Discomfort, Not Danger

When you start setting boundaries, discomfort is normal. Your nervous system may interpret “new” as unsafe. That does not mean the boundary is wrong. Practice small, repeatable limits and let your system learn that you can stay connected without overextending.

Therapy Can Make This Easier

Therapy can help you build boundary skills, challenge guilt patterns, and strengthen your sense of self. If people pleasing overlaps with anxiety or compulsive reassurance, our anxiety therapy and OCD therapy approaches may help.
