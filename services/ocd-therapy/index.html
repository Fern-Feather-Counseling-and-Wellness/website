OCD Therapy in Cumming, Georgia

OCD is often exhausting, confusing, and far more consuming than other people realize. Intrusive thoughts and compulsions can take up hours of your day, while shame and secrecy make it even harder to ask for help. Therapy can help you understand the OCD cycle and begin stepping out of it.

Request an Appointment View All Services

OCD can look different than people expect

  • You get intrusive thoughts that feel disturbing, unacceptable, or impossible to ignore.
  • You spend time checking, reviewing, researching, confessing, counting, praying, or seeking reassurance.
  • You feel trapped trying to get certainty or relief, even when you know the cycle is making things worse.
  • You avoid people, places, objects, or situations because they trigger obsessional fear or mental spiraling.
  • You wonder whether it is “really OCD” because many of your compulsions happen silently in your head.

If the broader picture feels more like constant worry, panic, or perfectionism, take a look at Anxiety Therapy. If trauma or nervous-system overwhelm is closely tied to your symptoms, Trauma Therapy may also be relevant.
