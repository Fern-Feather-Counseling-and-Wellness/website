# Online Course Outline: Nervous System Reset (Weeks 1-2)

## Course Goal:
To provide participants with a foundational understanding of the polyvagal theory and nervous system regulation, equipping them with practical tools to identify and shift their nervous system states towards greater calm, resilience, and well-being.

---

## Week 1: Understanding Your Nervous System

### Module 1.1: Welcome & Introduction
-   **Welcome Video:** Introduce Ronda, Nicole, and the course goals. Emphasize a "science-based, not woo-woo" approach.
-   **Course Overview:** What participants will learn, how the course works, what to expect.
-   **Setting Intentions:** Guide participants to set personal goals for the course.

### Module 1.2: The Biology of Stress & Safety
-   **Lesson:** Your Nervous System 101: The basics of the autonomic nervous system (ANS).
    -   Sympathetic (Fight/Flight) vs. Parasympathetic (Rest/Digest)
    -   The role of the brainstem and amygdala in threat detection.
-   **Lesson:** What is "Trauma"?
    -   Defining trauma beyond single incidents (developmental/complex trauma).
    -   How experiences shape our nervous system's baseline.
-   **Activity:** Body Scan & Sensation Mapping
    -   Guided practice to notice physical sensations related to stress and safety.

### Module 1.3: Introduction to Polyvagal Theory
-   **Lesson:** The Three States of the Nervous System (Simplified):
    -   **Ventral Vagal:** Social engagement, safety, connection (The "green zone")
    -   **Sympathetic:** Mobilization, energy, fight/flight (The "red zone")
    -   **Dorsal Vagal:** Immobilization, shutdown, dissociation (The "blue zone")
-   **Lesson:** Why We Get Stuck: Understanding trauma's impact on ANS regulation.
-   **Resource:** Interactive "Window of Tolerance" visual.

### Module 1.4: Your First Regulation Tools
-   **Lesson:** The Power of Breath
    -   Diaphragmatic breathing basics.
    -   Simple breath-to-calm techniques.
-   **Lesson:** Grounding Techniques
    -   5-4-3-2-1 senses technique.
    -   Physical grounding (feet on floor, hand on chest).
-   **Activity:** Daily Practice Log - Start tracking your states and using 1-2 tools.

---

## Week 2: Deepening Regulation & Shifting States

### Module 2.1: Navigating the Red Zone (Sympathetic Activation)
-   **Lesson:** Recognizing Fight/Flight
    -   Physical and emotional signs of sympathetic dominance.
    -   Common triggers for anxiety and overwhelm.
-   **Lesson:** Discharge and Release
    -   Why movement helps: shaking, stretching, walking.
    -   The role of safe anger expression.
-   **Activity:** "Moving Through It" - Practical exercises for physical release.

### Module 2.2: Understanding the Blue Zone (Dorsal Vagal Shutdown)
-   **Lesson:** Recognizing Freeze/Fawn/Shutdown
    -   Signs of hypoarousal: dissociation, numbness, fatigue.
    -   When connection feels too much.
-   **Lesson:** Gentle Re-engagement
    -   Sensory input for gentle activation (light, sound, taste).
    -   The importance of co-regulation (if applicable).
-   **Activity:** Sensory Comfort Check-in - Identifying safe sensory inputs.

### Module 2.3: Cultivating Ventral Vagal Safety
-   **Lesson:** The Science of Social Connection
    -   How face, voice, and social cues regulate our systems.
    -   The importance of attuned relationships.
-   **Lesson:** Playfulness and Curiosity
    -   Reigniting joy and exploration as markers of safety.
    -   Activities for gentle curiosity.
-   **Activity:** Resource Building - Identifying people, places, and things that evoke safety.

### Module 2.4: Integrating Our Practice
-   **Lesson:** Putting It All Together
    -   Recognizing your personal patterns and triggers.
    -   Creating a personalized "Nervous System Reset" toolkit.
-   **Activity:** Week 2 Reflection & Plan
    -   Reviewing progress, identifying key takeaways, and planning for continued practice.
