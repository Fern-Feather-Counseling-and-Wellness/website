**Format:** Self-paced online course  
**Duration:** 5 modules, ~2 hours total content

**Target Audience:** Trauma survivors, therapists-in-training, wellness practitioners, anyone wanting to understand their stress responses

---

## Course Philosophy

This isn't another "just breathe" course. This is a science-backed introduction to your autonomic nervous system—what it is, how it works, and why understanding it changes everything about how you approach healing, stress, and relationships.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand the basic biology of the autonomic nervous system
- Identify your personal stress response patterns
- Build a toolkit of regulation techniques that actually work
- Recognize when you're in different nervous system states
- Develop self-compassion for your survival adaptations

## Module 1: The Biology of Stress (20 minutes)

### Lesson 1.1 — Meet Your Autonomic Nervous System (8 min)
- What "autonomic" actually means (involuntary, not automatic)
- The two branches: sympathetic and parasympathetic
- Why this matters for your daily life
- Quick quiz: What's your default state?

### Lesson 1.2 — The Sympathetic System: Your Internal Alarm (7 min)
- Fight/flight/freeze: more than metaphors
- What happens in your body during activation
- Why your heart races, muscles tense, digestion stops
- The evolutionary purpose (it's not broken, it's adaptive)

### Lesson 1.3 — The Parasympathetic System: Rest and Digest (5 min)
- The ventral vagal branch: social connection and safety
- The dorsal vagal branch: shutdown and collapse
- Why "just relax" doesn't work (and what does)

**Module 1 Practice:** Body scan baseline assessment

---

## Module 2: Your Window of Tolerance (25 minutes)

### Lesson 2.1 — What Is the Window? (8 min)
- The concept introduced
- Hyperarousal vs hypoarousal
- How trauma narrows the window
- The goal: expand, not eliminate

### Lesson 2.2 — Mapping Your Personal Window (10 min)
- Downloadable worksheet: My Window of Tolerance
- Identifying your hyperarousal signs
- Identifying your hypoarousal signs
- Tracking your baseline over time

### Lesson 2.3 — Triggers and Glimmers (7 min)
- Triggers: what pushes you out of your window
- Glimmers: what brings you back (or keeps you there)
- Building awareness without judgment
- Exercise: Your trigger/glimmer list

**Module 2 Practice:** Daily window tracking for one week

---

## Module 3: The Three States (30 minutes)

### Lesson 3.1 — Ventral Vagal: Social Engagement (10 min)
- What safety feels like in the body
- Facial expressions, vocal tone, breath
- Co-regulation: why connection matters
- Signs you're in this state

### Lesson 3.2 — Sympathetic: Mobilization (10 min)
- Fight, flight, and the less-discussed freeze
- The mobilization cascade
- Healthy vs stuck activation
- When mobilization is appropriate

### Lesson 3.3 — Dorsal Vagal: Shutdown (10 min)
- Collapse, dissociation, numbness
- Why shutdown is protective
- The biology of "playing dead"
- Recognizing shutdown vs relaxation

**Module 3 Practice:** State identification exercise with video examples

---

## Module 4: Regulation Techniques (35 minutes)

### Lesson 4.1 — Grounding: Coming Back to Now (8 min)
- Orienting: using your senses
- 5-4-3-2-1 technique (with variations)
- Physical grounding: feet, seat, support
- When to use grounding (and when not to)

### Lesson 4.2 — Breathwork for Regulation (10 min)
- The physiological sigh (Stanford research)
- Extended exhale technique
- Why "deep breathing" can backfire
- Matching breath to state (upregulation vs downregulation)

### Lesson 4.3 — Movement and Somatic Tools (10 min)
- Shaking and tremoring (TRE basics)
- Self-havening touch
- Bilateral stimulation (butterfly hug, tapping)
- Gentle movement for different states

### Lesson 4.4 — Creating Your Regulation Toolkit (7 min)
- Building a personalized menu
- Categorizing by speed and accessibility
- Practice when calm, use when dysregulated
- Downloadable: Build Your Toolkit worksheet

**Module 4 Practice:** Testing 3 techniques, tracking what works

---

## Module 5: Integration and Next Steps (20 minutes)

### Lesson 5.1 — Common Myths Debunked (7 min)
- "You can think your way out of trauma"
- "Calm is the goal"
- "Nervous system work is a quick fix"
- "You should be able to regulate alone"

### Lesson 5.2 — Building Sustainable Practice (8 min)
- Micro-practices: 30 seconds counts
- Habit stacking with existing routines
- Tracking progress without obsession
- Self-compassion when you "fail"

### Lesson 5.3 — When to Seek Professional Help (5 min)
- Signs you need more support
- Types of practitioners who can help
- What to look for in a trauma therapist
- Resources and crisis lines

**Module 5 Practice:** Creating your 30-day integration plan

## Platform Recommendations

**Option 1: Teachable**
- Best for: Professional appearance, easy setup
- Cost: $39-119/month
- Pros: Built-in email, certificates, affiliate program
- Cons: Monthly fee, limited customization

**Option 2: Kajabi**
- Best for: All-in-one (email + website + course)
- Cost: $149-399/month
- Pros: Everything integrated, great support
- Cons: Expensive for single course

**Option 3: Gumroad**
- Best for: Simple, pay-as-you-earn
- Cost: 10% fee per sale
- Pros: No monthly fee, easy setup
- Cons: Basic features, less professional

**Option 4: Self-hosted (WordPress + LifterLMS)**
- Best for: Full control, lowest long-term cost
- Cost: Hosting + plugin (~$200/year)
- Pros: Own your platform, customizable
- Cons: Technical setup required

**Recommendation:** Start with Gumroad for MVP, migrate to Teachable if successful.
