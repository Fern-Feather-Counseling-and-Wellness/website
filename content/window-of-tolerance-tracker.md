# Window of Tolerance Tracker
## Understand Your Nervous System States

**What is the Window of Tolerance?**

Your "window of tolerance" is the zone of arousal where you can function effectively. When you're within this window, you can:
- Think clearly and make decisions
- Process information and learn
- Connect with others
- Manage emotions without becoming overwhelmed

When stress pushes you outside this window, you enter either:
- **Hyperarousal** (fight/flight) — anxious, panicked, reactive
- **Hypoarousal** (freeze/shutdown) — numb, disconnected, depressed

## Quick Reference: Regulation Techniques

### For Hyperarousal (Calming Down):
- **Physiological sigh:** Two sharp inhales through nose, long exhale through mouth
- **Cold water:** Splash face or hold ice cube
- **Grounding:** 5-4-3-2-1 senses technique
- **Movement:** Shake out limbs, brisk walk
- **Orienting:** Look around, notice surroundings
- **Box breathing:** Inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4

### For Hypoarousal (Waking Up):
- **Movement:** Jumping jacks, dancing, stretching
- **Cold water:** Splash face with cold water
- **Strong sensations:** Sour candy, peppermint, spicy food
- **Music:** Upbeat, energizing songs
- **Social connection:** Call a friend, make eye contact
- **Posture:** Sit up straight, open body position

## Understanding the Science

Your nervous system has two branches that work like a seesaw:

**Sympathetic Nervous System (SNS)** — "Gas pedal"
- Activates for action, energy, alertness
- Too much = hyperarousal (anxiety, panic, reactivity)

**Parasympathetic Nervous System (PNS)** — "Brake pedal"
- Activates for rest, digestion, recovery
- Too much = hypoarousal (numbness, shutdown, depression)

**The Goal:** Flexible movement between these states, with most of your time in the balanced middle — your window of tolerance.

---

## Tips for Expanding Your Window

1. **Practice daily regulation** — even when you feel fine
2. **Track patterns** — notice what triggers what
3. **Start small** — tiny practices build capacity over time
4. **Be patient** — windows expand gradually, not overnight
5. **Get support** — therapy can help process what pushes you out
