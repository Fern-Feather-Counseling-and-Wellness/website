## 💡 Referral Best Practices

### When to Refer Out
1. **Medical stabilization needed** (acute suicidal ideation, psychosis, severe eating disorder)
2. **Specialty required** (OCD needing ERP, addiction needing IOP, eating disorder needing higher level of care)
3. **Not a fit** (clinical style mismatch, client requests different approach)
4. **Client request** (even if you think you're a good fit, honor client choice)

### How to Make Referrals
1. **Warm handoff** — call/email provider with client summary (if client consents)
2. **Provide 2-3 options** — never just one
3. **Follow up** — check if client connected, re-refer if needed
4. **Coordinate care** — with client consent, communicate with other providers
5. **Don't take it personally** — sometimes referrals don't stick, that's okay

### Building Referral Relationships
1. **Lead with generosity** — give referrals before asking
2. **Know their work** — understand their approach before referring
3. **Meet in person** — coffee builds trust
4. **Be responsive** — return calls/emails within 24 hours
5. **Show your work** — when asking for referrals, demonstrate your competence
6. **Reciprocate** — referral relationships are two-way
