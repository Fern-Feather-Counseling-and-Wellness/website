## 1. Somatic Therapy Efficacy

### Key Finding: Somatic Therapy Shows Strong Acceptability for PTSD
**Source:** PMC Study (2025) — "The Acceptability of Somatic Therapy for PTSD Among Patients at an Urban Safety Net Primary Care Clinic"

**What they found:**
> "This study confirms that ST, and a mind and body approach to PTSD more broadly, is acceptable and perhaps culturally relevant to diverse patients with PTSD in an urban safety net setting."

**Why it matters:** Somatic approaches aren't just effective — they're accessible across diverse populations, which aligns with Fern & Feather's inclusive mission.

### Key Finding: Somatic Experiencing RCT Shows Positive Results
**Source:** PMC Randomized Controlled Trial — "Somatic Experiencing for Posttraumatic Stress Disorder: A Randomized Controlled Outcome Study"

**What they found:**
> "This randomized controlled study of SE shows positive results indicating SE may be an effective therapy method for PTSD."

**Why it matters:** This is one of the few RCTs on Somatic Experiencing — solid evidence base for trauma work.

### Key Finding: 2025 Pilot Study on Group Somatic Experiencing
**Source:** PLOS One (December 2025) — Group-based somatic experiencing for Indonesian women survivors of sexual assault

**What they found:** Pilot feasibility study showing promise for group-based SE delivery in trauma populations.

**Why it matters:** Supports Fern & Feather's group therapy model with emerging evidence.

## 2. Attachment & Neuroscience

### Key Finding: Maternal and Paternal Sensitivity Meta-Analysis (2024)
**Source:** Open Journal of Psychiatry, 2024 — Meta-analysis on attachment determinants

**What they found:**
> "Maternal and Paternal Sensitivity: Key Determinants of Child Attachment Security Examined through Meta-Analysis"

**Why it matters:** Both parents matter for attachment — useful for couples work and parenting-focused content.

### Key Finding: EEG-Based Attachment Style Prediction (2025)
**Source:** Frontiers in Human Neuroscience (September 2025)

**What they found:** Enhanced EEG-based attachment style prediction through feature domain analysis.

**Why it matters:** Attachment styles have measurable neural signatures — "biology meets being human" positioning gold.

## 3. Intergenerational Trauma & Epigenetics

### Key Finding: Syrian Refugees Study (February 2025)
**Source:** Scientific Reports — "Epigenetic signatures of intergenerational exposure to violence in three generations of Syrian refugees"

**What they found:**
> "Our study responds to this call with support for the intergenerational epigenetic transmission of trauma in humans through the identification of novel DNAm sites and evidence for epigenetic age acceleration."

**Why it matters:** Direct human evidence of epigenetic trauma transmission — powerful for "your trauma isn't just in your head, it's in your biology" messaging.

### Key Finding: Holocaust Survivors — Third Generation Resilience (July 2025)
**Source:** Scientific Reports — "From trauma to resilience: psychological and epigenetic adaptations in the third generation of holocaust survivors"

**What they found:**
> "The transmission of trauma across generations, particularly among descendants of Holocaust survivors, presents a complex interplay of psychological and epigenetic adaptations."

**Why it matters:** Third-generation effects are real — trauma work isn't just about the individual, it's about family systems across time.

### Key Finding: Paternal Transmission Mechanisms (2024)
**Source:** OxJournal Review — "Epigenetic Inheritance of Trauma Across Generations"

**What they found:**
> "A growing body of literature has focused on the transmission of paternal preconception trauma to offspring through sperm... Mechanisms that have been implicated in this process... are DNA methylation, histone modifications and ncRNAs."

**Why it matters:** Fathers pass trauma through epigenetic mechanisms too — not just maternal line.

## 4. Nervous System Regulation & HRV

### Key Finding: Brain-Heart Interactions Under Stress (August 2025)
**Source:** Scientific Reports — "Brain activation and heart rate variability as markers of autonomic function under stress"

**What they found:**
> "Efficient brain–heart interactions, mediated by the central autonomic network (CAN), are crucial in regulating physiological and psychological stress."

**Why it matters:** HRV isn't just a wellness metric — it's a marker of brain-body integration and stress resilience.

### Key Finding: HRV Biofeedback & Safe and Sound Protocol Review (2025)
**Source:** PMC Review — "Harnessing non-invasive vagal neuromodulation: HRV biofeedback and SSP for cardiovascular and autonomic regulation"

**What they found:**
> "This intervention enhances the vagal tone and restores autonomic nervous system regulation, addressing the dysregulation often seen in PTSD."

**Why it matters:** HRV biofeedback has solid evidence for trauma recovery — supports Fern & Feather's somatic approach.

### Key Finding: TRE and HRV (2021, cited 2025)
**Source:** TRE Global Research Compilation

**What they found:**
> "Tension Trauma Releasing Exercises (TRE) regulates the autonomic nervous system, increases heart rate variability, and improves psychophysiological stress in university students."

**Why it matters:** Body-based tremoring (TRE) measurably improves autonomic regulation — another tool for the toolkit.
