## Quiz Questions (15 total)

### Section 1: Close Relationships

1. **When a romantic partner gets distant, I tend to:**
   a) Give them space and focus on my own life
   b) Feel anxious and try harder to connect
   c) Feel hurt but don't say anything
   d) See it as a sign the relationship isn't working

2. **In arguments with loved ones, I usually:**
   a) Try to understand their perspective and find compromise
   b) Worry they'll leave me if I express my needs
   c) Shut down or withdraw to avoid conflict
   d) Focus on solving the problem logically

3. **My ideal level of closeness in a relationship is:**
   a) Balanced — togetherness and independence
   b) Very close, with lots of time together
   c) More independent, with clear boundaries
   d) Practical partnership focused on shared goals

### Section 2: Self-Perception & Emotions

4. **When I'm upset, I typically:**
   a) Reach out to someone I trust for support
   b) Worry people will judge me for being "needy"
   c) Keep it to myself until I calm down
   d) Analyze what caused the problem and find solutions

5. **My feelings about myself in relationships are generally:**
   a) Mostly positive — I'm worthy of love
   b) Insecure — I worry I'm not enough
   c) Neutral — relationships don't define me
   d) Confident — I know what I bring to the table

6. **When someone compliments me, I tend to:**
   a) Appreciate it and believe them
   b) Feel awkward and doubt their sincerity
   c) Brush it off or downplay it
   d) Thank them but focus on areas to improve

### Section 3: Conflict & Independence

7. **If a friend cancels plans last minute, I usually think:**
   a) "They must have a good reason"
   b) "They don't really want to spend time with me"
   c) "That's fine, I enjoy my alone time"
   d) "I'll make other plans or use the time productively"

8. **My need for personal space is:**
   a) Moderate — I value both connection and independence
   b) Low — I prefer being with people most of the time
   c) High — I need lots of alone time to recharge
   d) Variable — depends on my goals and schedule

9. **When facing a personal challenge, I'm more likely to:**
   a) Talk it through with someone supportive
   b) Seek reassurance from multiple people
   c) Handle it alone until I figure it out
   d) Research solutions and create a plan

### Section 4: Childhood & Patterns

10. **Looking back at my childhood caregivers, I'd describe them as:**
    a) Generally available and responsive
    b) Sometimes there, sometimes not
    c) More distant or self-focused
    d) Practical and focused on achievement

11. **The emotional tone in my childhood home was mostly:**
    a) Warm and secure
    b) Unpredictable or anxious
    c) Cool or detached
    d) Structured and task-oriented

12. **As a child, when I was upset, my caregivers typically:**
    a) Comforted me and helped me regulate
    b) Reacted with their own anxiety or frustration
    c) Told me to "toughen up" or left me alone
    d) Focused on solving whatever caused the upset

### Section 5: Current Relationship Patterns

13. **In my closest current relationship, I notice I:**
    a) Feel secure and can express needs openly
    b) Often worry about being "too much" or "not enough"
    c) Keep some emotional distance to protect myself
    d) Focus on practical partnership over emotional intimacy

14. **When someone gets really close to me emotionally, I tend to:**
    a) Welcome it and reciprocate
    b) Feel excited but also scared they'll leave
    c) Pull back slightly to maintain boundaries
    d) Assess whether this benefits both of us

15. **My friends would probably describe me as:**
    a) Reliable and emotionally available
    b) Caring but sometimes anxious in relationships
    c) Independent and self-sufficient
    d) Practical and goal-oriented

## Scoring & Results

### Scoring Key:
- **Mostly A's: Secure Attachment**
- **Mostly B's: Anxious Attachment**
- **Mostly C's: Avoidant Attachment**
- **Mostly D's: Dismissive-Avoidant Attachment**
- **Mixed patterns:** Disorganized or earned-secure patterns

### Result Descriptions:

#### **Secure Attachment**
**Core Pattern:** Comfortable with intimacy and independence.
**Strengths:** Can express needs clearly, handle conflict constructively, maintain healthy boundaries.
**Growth Areas:** May need to recognize when partners have different attachment styles.
**Tip:** Your secure foundation helps create stability in relationships. Keep practicing open communication.

#### **Anxious Attachment**
**Core Pattern:** Craves closeness but fears abandonment.
**Strengths:** Highly attuned to relationship dynamics, deeply caring, committed.
**Growth Areas:** Learning self-soothing, distinguishing real threats from anxiety, expressing needs without panic.
**Tip:** Practice noticing when anxiety is talking vs. actual relationship issues. Build your "secure base" within yourself.

#### **Avoidant Attachment**
**Core Pattern:** Values independence, uncomfortable with too much closeness.
**Strengths:** Self-sufficient, good boundaries, doesn't get overwhelmed by others' emotions.
**Growth Areas:** Learning to tolerate vulnerability, recognizing emotional needs as valid.
**Tip:** Small steps toward vulnerability can feel safer than big emotional leaps. Start with low-risk sharing.

#### **Dismissive-Avoidant Attachment**
**Core Pattern:** Prioritizes logic and achievement over emotional connection.
**Strengths:** Practical, goal-oriented, emotionally regulated.
**Growth Areas:** Recognizing that emotions contain valuable information, allowing softness without seeing it as weakness.
**Tip:** Emotions aren't the opposite of logic — they're different data. Practice naming feelings without judgment.

## Personalized Recommendations

### For **Secure** Individuals:
1. **Continue nurturing relationships** — Your secure base helps others feel safe
2. **Notice when partners have different attachment styles** — Adjust communication accordingly
3. **Practice teaching secure patterns** to loved ones who didn't learn them early
4. **Consider therapy** to process any relationship history that feels unresolved

### For **Anxious** Individuals:
1. **Develop a self-soothing toolkit** — Breathing, grounding, self-talk
2. **Practice "pause before pursuing"** — Wait 10 minutes before texting/calling when anxious
3. **Build evidence of your worth** outside relationships — hobbies, skills, achievements
4. **Therapy can help:** Explore childhood patterns, build internal security

### For **Avoidant** Individuals:
1. **Practice small vulnerability** — Share one feeling per day with someone safe
2. **Notice physical sensations** when emotions arise — body awareness builds emotional literacy
3. **Schedule connection time** rather than waiting to "feel like it"
4. **Therapy can help:** Explore protective strategies, build comfort with intimacy

### For **Dismissive-Avoidant** Individuals:
1. **Practice naming emotions** daily — use an emotion wheel for vocabulary
2. **Connect feelings to needs** — "I feel X because I need Y"
3. **Allow small moments of softness** — a gentle touch, kind word to self
4. **Therapy can help:** Bridge mind-body connection, explore emotional intelligence
