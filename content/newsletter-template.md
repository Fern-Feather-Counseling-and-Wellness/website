**Example (March):**

### The Difference Between Rest and Regulation

You've slept. You've taken weekends off. You've binge-watched Netflix. So why are you still exhausted?

Because rest isn't the same as regulation.

Rest is the absence of activity. Regulation is the presence of safety in your nervous system.

When you're stressed, your sympathetic nervous system (fight/flight) activates. Your body pumps cortisol and adrenaline. These chemicals prepare you to run from a lion — or meet a deadline.

Here's what most people miss: Those stress hormones don't just disappear when the stressor ends. They need to be metabolized. Processed. Completed.

Rest without regulation is like pausing a movie. The stress is still there, waiting.

Regulation means actively completing the stress cycle:
- Movement (shaking, walking, dancing)
- Breathwork (physiological sigh, box breathing)
- Connection (hugs, talking, eye contact)
- Sensory grounding (cold water, touch)

This is why somatic therapy works. We don't just talk about your stress. We help your body process it.

**This month's challenge:** Try one regulation technique daily for a week. Notice the difference between resting and actually regulating.

## Section 2: Quick Tips (Bulleted List)

**Format:** 3-5 actionable tips

**Example:**

### This Month's Micro-Practices

⚡ **The 30-second reset:** Two sharp inhales, long exhale. Do this before checking email.

⚡ **Grounding through feet:** While waiting in line, press your feet into the floor. Notice the support.

⚡ **Cold water splash:** When overwhelmed, splash cold water on your face. Activates the dive reflex.

⚡ **Orienting:** Let your head turn slowly, let your eyes look around. Let your body find safety.
