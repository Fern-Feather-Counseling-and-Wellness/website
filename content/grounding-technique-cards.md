## Overview
These printable cards give clients a handful of evidence‑based grounding exercises they can pull out in moments of overwhelm. Each card includes a short description, a step‑by‑step guide, and a brief science note with APA citation.

## Card Set
1. **5‑4‑3‑2‑1 Sensory Scan**
   - *Instructions*: Identify 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.
   - *Science*: Engages multiple sensory pathways to shift attention away from threat‑focused circuitry (Kabat‑Zinn, 2015).
2. **Box Breathing**
   - *Instructions*: Inhale 4 s, hold 4 s, exhale 4 s, hold 4 s. Repeat 4 cycles.
   - *Science*: Activates the ventral vagal complex, reducing sympathetic arousal (Lehrer & Gevirtz, 2014).
3. **Grounding Pose**
   - *Instructions*: Stand with feet hip‑width apart, knees slightly bent, hands on thighs. Feel the floor supporting you for 30 s.
   - *Science*: Proprioceptive input signals safety to the brain (Porges, 2020).
4. **Anchor Word**
   - *Instructions*: Choose a calming word (e.g., "peace"); repeat silently while breathing.
   - *Science*: Re‑engages the language network, providing a cognitive anchor (Cowan, 2021).
5. **Box‑Step Walk**
   - *Instructions*: Walk in place, taking 4 steps forward, 4 steps back, counting each step.
   - *Science*: Rhythm‑based movement synchronizes neural oscillations, easing dysregulated states (Thaut, 2018).
