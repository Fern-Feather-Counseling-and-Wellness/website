# Grounding Technique Cards
## Portable Tools for Nervous System Regulation

**Instructions:** Print this document on cardstock, cut along the lines, and keep these cards with you. Use them when you feel anxious, overwhelmed, or disconnected.

---

## Card 1: 5-4-3-2-1 Senses Technique

**When to use:** Anxiety, panic, racing thoughts, feeling overwhelmed

**How to do it:**
1. **5** things you can SEE — Look around, name them
2. **4** things you can TOUCH — Feel textures around you
3. **3** things you can HEAR — Listen for sounds near and far
4. **2** things you can SMELL — Notice scents in the air
5. **1** thing you can TASTE — Take a sip of water or notice your mouth

**Why it works:** Engages your prefrontal cortex, pulling you out of threat response and into present-moment awareness.

**Time needed:** 1-2 minutes

---

## Card 2: Physiological Sigh

**When to use:** Stress, shallow breathing, tight chest, before sleep

**How to do it:**
1. Take a deep breath IN through your nose
2. Take another quick, sharp inhale (top up the lungs)
3. Exhale slowly through your mouth with a sigh
4. Repeat 1-3 times

**Why it works:** Double inhale reinflates collapsed alveoli in the lungs. Slow exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest).

**Time needed:** 30 seconds

*Research: Huberman Lab, Stanford University*

---

## Card 3: Cold Water Reset

**When to use:** Panic, dissociation, emotional flooding, can't calm down

**How to do it:**
1. Fill a bowl with cold water (or use sink)
2. Hold your breath
3. Submerge your face for 10-30 seconds
4. OR splash cold water on your face
5. OR hold an ice cube

**Why it works:** Triggers the mammalian dive reflex — slows heart rate, activates the vagus nerve, shifts you from sympathetic (fight/flight) to parasympathetic (calm).

**Time needed:** 30-60 seconds

---

## Card 4: Box Breathing

**When to use:** Before stressful events, public speaking, tests, difficult conversations

**How to do it:**
1. Inhale for **4** counts
2. Hold for **4** counts
3. Exhale for **4** counts
4. Hold for **4** counts
5. Repeat 4-5 cycles

**Why it works:** Structured breathing pattern regulates CO2 and O2 balance, calms the nervous system, and gives your mind a simple focus.

**Time needed:** 2-3 minutes

*Used by Navy SEALs for stress management*

---

## Card 5: Grounding Through Feet

**When to use:** Dissociation, feeling "floaty," disconnected from body

**How to do it:**
1. Stand or sit with feet flat on the floor
2. Press your feet firmly into the ground
3. Notice the sensation of contact — heels, balls of feet, toes
4. Wiggle your toes inside your shoes
5. Feel the weight of your body being supported

**Why it works:** Reconnects you to physical reality, activates proprioception (body position awareness), brings attention out of your head and into your body.

**Time needed:** 1 minute

---

## Card 6: Shaking/Trauma Release

**When to use:** After stress, tension in body, feeling "stuck," before bed

**How to do it:**
1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
2. Begin gently shaking your hands
3. Let the shaking travel up your arms
4. Add leg shaking, bouncing
5. Let your whole body shake gently for 1-2 minutes
6. Stop and notice the sensations

**Why it works:** Animals shake after trauma to discharge stress hormones. Humans suppress this. Shaking completes the stress cycle and releases tension.

**Time needed:** 2-3 minutes

*Based on TRE (Tension/Trauma Releasing Exercises)*

---

## Card 7: Orienting

**When to use:** Hypervigilance, scanning for threats, feeling unsafe

**How to do it:**
1. Let your head turn slowly to the right
2. Let your eyes look around — what's there?
3. Let your head turn slowly to the left
4. Let your eyes look around — what's there?
5. Notice what draws your attention
6. Let your body move toward what feels safe/pleasant

**Why it works:** Orienting is a natural mammalian behavior to assess safety. It activates the social engagement system and helps your nervous system determine "am I safe?"

**Time needed:** 1-2 minutes

---

## Card 8: Hand on Heart

**When to use:** Self-criticism, shame, loneliness, needing comfort

**How to do it:**
1. Place one hand on your heart
2. Place the other hand on your belly
3. Feel the warmth of your hands
4. Notice your heartbeat
5. Breathe slowly and deeply
6. Offer yourself kindness silently

**Why it works:** Self-touch releases oxytocin (bonding hormone). Hand on heart activates the caregiving system, soothing the nervous system.

**Time needed:** 1-2 minutes

---

## Card 9: 4-7-8 Breathing

**When to use:** Can't fall asleep, racing thoughts at night, anxiety

**How to do it:**
1. Exhale completely through your mouth
2. Inhale through nose for **4** counts
3. Hold breath for **7** counts
4. Exhale through mouth for **8** counts
5. Repeat 3-4 cycles

**Why it works:** Longer exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system. The counting gives your mind something to focus on instead of anxious thoughts.

**Time needed:** 2-3 minutes

*Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil*

---

## Card 10: Body Scan

**When to use:** Tension, stress, difficulty relaxing, before sleep

**How to do it:**
1. Start at the top of your head
2. Slowly move attention down through your body
3. Notice each area: forehead → jaw → neck → shoulders → arms → chest → belly → hips → legs → feet
4. Don't try to change anything, just notice
5. If you find tension, breathe into that area

**Why it works:** Builds interoceptive awareness (sensing internal states), helps identify where you hold stress, promotes relaxation through attention.

**Time needed:** 3-5 minutes

## Quick Reference: Which Technique When?

| State | Try This |
|-------|----------|
| Panic/Racing heart | Physiological sigh, Cold water |
| Can't think clearly | 5-4-3-2-1 senses, Orienting |
| Feeling "floaty"/dissociated | Grounding through feet, Cold water |
| Can't sleep | 4-7-8 breathing, Body scan |
| Tension/Stress | Shaking, Body scan |
| Before stressful event | Box breathing |
| Self-criticism/Shame | Hand on heart |
| Hypervigilance | Orienting, 5-4-3-2-1 |

## How to Use These Cards

1. **Print and cut** — Use cardstock for durability
2. **Laminate** — Optional, for longevity
3. **Carry with you** — Keep in purse, wallet, car, desk
4. **Practice when calm** — Learn the techniques before you need them
5. **Use at first sign** — Don't wait until you're overwhelmed
6. **Notice what works** — Everyone's nervous system is different

---

## Remember

✓ **These are tools, not cures.** They regulate your nervous system in the moment.

✓ **Consistency matters.** Practice daily, not just in crisis.

✓ **Be patient.** It takes time to retrain your nervous system.

✓ **Get support.** If you're struggling frequently, consider working with a trauma-informed therapist.
