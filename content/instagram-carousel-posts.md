## Carousel 2: "Signs Your Nervous System Is Dysregulated"

**Caption:**
Your body is constantly sending you signals. Are you listening? 🚨

Nervous system dysregulation isn't just "feeling stressed"—it shows up in surprising ways throughout your whole body.

Swipe to see the signs. How many do you recognize? ➡️

#NervousSystemHealth #TraumaRecovery #SomaticHealing #AnxietyRelief #MindBodyConnection #MentalHealthMatters #FernAndFeather

**Slide Content:**

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
- Title: "8 SIGNS YOUR NERVOUS SYSTEM IS DYSREGULATED"
- Subtitle: "(That have nothing to do with 'just being stressed')"

**Slide 2:**
- 1. CHRONIC TENSION
- Shoulders glued to your ears? Jaw clenched 24/7?
- Your body is holding stress it's not processing. Chronic muscle tension is a classic sign your nervous system is stuck in high alert.

**Slide 3:**
- 2. DIGESTIVE ISSUES
- IBS, nausea, appetite changes...
- Your gut has its own nervous system (the enteric nervous system). When your main nervous system is dysregulated, your gut feels it first. The gut-brain connection is real.

**Slide 4:**
- 3. SLEEP DISTURBANCES
- Trouble falling asleep? Waking up at 3am wired? Never feeling rested?
- A dysregulated nervous system doesn't know when to shift into rest mode. Your body thinks it's still in danger—even in bed.

**Slide 5:**
- 4. EMOTIONAL NUMBNESS
- Not feeling much of anything? Going through the motions?
- This is hypoarousal—your nervous system shut down to protect you. It's a survival response, not a character flaw. You can learn to feel again.

**Slide 6:**
- 5. STARTLING EASILY
- Jump at every noise? Heart races when someone enters the room?
- Your threat detection system is on overdrive. It's scanning for danger constantly—even when you're safe.

**Slide 7:**
- 6. BRAIN FOG & POOR CONCENTRATION
- Can't focus? Keep forgetting things? Feel like you're walking through molasses?
- When your nervous system is overwhelmed, cognitive resources get diverted to survival. Your brain is doing its best.

**Slide 8:**
- 7. CHRONIC FATIGUE
- Tired no matter how much you sleep? Everything feels like too much?
- Living in a dysregulated state is exhausting. Your body is working overtime trying to maintain safety. No wonder you're tired.

**Slide 9:**
- 8. PEOPLE-PLEASING & BOUNDARIES
- Say yes when you want to say no? Feel responsible for others' emotions?
- This is a nervous system adaptation. When safety felt conditional, you learned to accommodate. Your system is still trying to keep you safe through pleasing.

**Slide 10 (CTA):**
- RECOGNIZE YOURSELF?
- Your nervous system can heal. You can feel safe in your body.
- We specialize in somatic, trauma-informed therapy to help you regulate and thrive.

## Carousel 4: "Attachment Styles in Relationships"

**Caption:**
Your attachment style isn't a life sentence—it's information. 💡

Understanding how you attach helps you understand your patterns in relationships, and that's where change begins.

Swipe through the 4 attachment styles. Which one resonates most? ➡️

#AttachmentTheory #RelationshipTherapy #AttachmentStyles #CouplesTherapy #TraumaHealing #MentalHealthAwareness #FernAndFeather

**Slide Content:**

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
- Title: "ATTACHMENT STYLES IN RELATIONSHIPS"
- Subtitle: "Understanding Your Patterns"

**Slide 2:**
- Header: WHAT IS ATTACHMENT?
- Attachment theory explains how our early relationships with caregivers shape the way we connect with others throughout life.
- It's not about blame—it's about understanding patterns so you can make conscious choices.
- The good news: attachment can change through safe relationships and therapy.

**Slide 3:**
- SECURE ATTACHMENT 🌿
- Traits: Comfortable with intimacy, Can depend on others & be depended on, Manages emotions well, Communicates needs directly, Trusts others appropriately
- Core belief: "I'm worthy of love and others are reliable."
- Population: ~50-60%

**Slide 4:**
- ANXIOUS ATTACHMENT 💭
- Traits: Craves closeness but worries about abandonment, Seeks reassurance frequently, Sensitive to relationship cues, Fear of being "too much", Emotional ups and downs
- Core belief: "I need connection, but I'm not sure I can count on it."
- Needs: Consistency, reassurance, clear communication

**Slide 5:**
- AVOIDANT ATTACHMENT 🛡️
- Traits: Values independence highly, Uncomfortable with too much closeness, Difficulty identifying emotions, Withdraws when conflict arises, Fear of being "trapped"
- Core belief: "I want connection, but it feels unsafe to need others."
- Needs: Space, patience, gradual intimacy building

**Slide 6:**
- DISORGANIZED ATTACHMENT 🌪️
- Traits: Wants closeness but fears it, Unpredictable relationship patterns, Difficulty trusting, Often stems from trauma, Push-pull dynamics
- Core belief: "I need you, but I'm scared of what might happen if I trust you."
- Needs: Safety, consistency, trauma-informed therapy

**Slide 7:**
- YOU CAN EARN SECURE ATTACHMENT
- Attachment is not destiny. You can develop earned secure attachment through:
  - Therapy with a secure therapist
  - Relationships with secure people
  - Self-awareness & inner work
  - Understanding your patterns
  - Learning new relationship skills
- Change is possible at any age.

## Carousel 6: "Self-Regulation Techniques You Can Use Anywhere"

**Caption:**
Your nervous system doesn't care that you're in a meeting, on the train, or at a family dinner. When dysregulation hits, you need tools that work in real life.

Here are 6 techniques you can use anywhere—no one will even know. ➡️

Save this for when you need it. 💾

#SelfRegulation #AnxietyRelief #NervousSystemRegulation #GroundingTechniques #MentalHealthTips #SomaticHealing #FernAndFeather

**Slide Content:**

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
- Title: "6 SELF-REGULATION TECHNIQUES YOU CAN USE ANYWHERE"
- Subtitle: "(No one will even know)"

**Slide 2:**
- 1. THE 5-5-5 GROUNDING
- Perfect for: Anxiety, racing thoughts
- How to do it: Name 5 things you see, 5 things you can touch/feel, Take 5 deep breaths
- Why it works: Redirects your brain to the present moment.
- 🕒 Takes: 60 seconds

**Slide 3:**
- 2. BOX BREATHING (Silent)
- Perfect for: Before speaking, presentations, difficult conversations
- How to do it: Inhale for 4 counts, Hold for 4 counts, Exhale for 4 counts, Hold for 4 counts
- Why it works: Activates your parasympathetic nervous system.
- 🕒 Takes: 2-3 minutes

**Slide 4:**
- 3. COLD WATER ON WRISTS
- Perfect for: Panic, dissociation, overwhelming emotions
- How to do it: Go to the bathroom, Run cold water over your wrists or hold cold water in your hands, Focus on the temperature sensation
- Why it works: Triggers the mammalian dive reflex—rapidly calms your heart.
- 🕒 Takes: 30-60 seconds

**Slide 5:**
- 4. FEET ON THE FLOOR
- Perfect for: Dissociation, feeling "floaty," disconnected
- How to do it: Press feet firmly into ground, Wiggle toes inside shoes, Notice texture of socks/shoes, Feel the solid ground beneath you
- Why it works: Grounds you physically when you feel mentally scattered.
- 🕒 Takes: 30 seconds

**Slide 6:**
- 5. THE HALF-SMILE
- Perfect for: Low mood, frustration, stuck in negative loop
- How to do it: Relax your face, Turn up corners of mouth slightly (not forced), Hold for 10-20 seconds, Notice any internal shifts
- Why it works: Facial feedback loop—your brain reads your face as "things are okay."
- 🕒 Takes: 20 seconds

**Slide 7:**
- 6. PROGRESSIVE MUSCLE TENSE-RELEASE
- Perfect for: Muscle tension, sitting at desk all day, anxiety
- How to do it: Tense all muscles for 5 seconds (fists, shoulders, face, stomach, legs), Release all at once, Notice the wave of relaxation
- Why it works: Teaches your body the difference between tension and relaxation.
- 🕒 Takes: 1 minute

  - Accent: Terracotta (#C67C5C) - grounding, earthy, energetic

- **Headlines:** Clean sans-serif, bold weight
- **Body text:** Same family, regular weight, high readability
- **Accent text:** Italic for quotes or emphasis

### Image Recommendations
- Use authentic, diverse representation
- Nature elements (ferns, feathers, plants) as subtle background textures
- Avoid generic stock photos of "sad people"
