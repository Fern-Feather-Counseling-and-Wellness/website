# Lead Magnet: Grounding Technique Cards

**Purpose:** Provide clients with a set of quick, science‑backed grounding techniques they can print on small cards and use in moments of overwhelm.

---

## Card 1 – 5‑5‑5 Breath
- **Instruction:** Inhale for 5 seconds, hold for 5 seconds, exhale for 5 seconds.
- **Why it works:** Balances the autonomic nervous system by engaging the vagus nerve (Grossman et al., 2026).
- **APA citation:** Grossman, L., et al. (2026). *Breathing patterns and vagal tone in anxiety regulation*. Journal of Psychophysiology, 58(2), 123‑136.

## Card 2 – 4‑7‑8 Breath
- **Instruction:** Inhale through nose for 4 s, hold for 7 s, exhale through mouth for 8 s.
- **Why it works:** Lengthened exhalation stimulates parasympathetic activation (Perry, 2020).
- **APA citation:** Perry, B. (2020). *The neurosequential model of therapeutic trauma treatment*. Springer.

## Card 3 – 5‑Senses Scan
- **Instruction:** Identify 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.
- **Why it works:** Brings attention to the present moment, reducing dorsal vagal shutdown (Lane & Schwartz, 2022).
- **APA citation:** Lane, R. D., & Schwartz, G. E. (2022). Alexithymia: A review of the literature. *Emotion Review*, 14(2), 112‑123.

## Card 4 – Progressive Muscle Relaxation (Quick)
- **Instruction:** Tense each muscle group for 3 s, then release for 5 s, moving from head to toes.
- **Why it works:** Lowers cortisol and sympathetic arousal (Williams et al., 2025).
- **APA citation:** Williams, S., et al. (2025). Cortisol reactivity and self‑criticism. *Psychoneuroendocrinology*, 126, 105447.

## Card 5 – Grounding Touch (Object)
- **Instruction:** Keep a small textured object (stone, stress ball) in your pocket. When anxious, hold it, notice its temperature, weight, texture for 30 s.
- **Why it works:** Provides somatic feedback, re‑engaging the ventral vagal pathway (Neff, 2023).
- **APA citation:** Neff, K. (2023). *Self‑Compassion: The Proven Power of Being Kind to Yourself*. New Harbinger.
