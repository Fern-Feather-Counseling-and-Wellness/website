# Grounding Technique Cards – Quick Tools for Immediate Calm

**Format:** Printable 3" x 5" cards (8.5" x 11" sheet, double‑sided) – each card features a simple grounding exercise with a brief scientific note and QR code linking to a short audio guide (ElevenLabs TTS).

---

## Card 1 – 5‑4‑3‑2‑1 Senses (APA: Kirsch, 2021)
- **Instruction:** Look around and name **5** things you see, **4** you can touch, **3** you hear, **2** you smell, **1** you taste.
- **Science:** Engages the prefrontal cortex to shift attention from threat‑focused limbic activity (Kirsch, 2021).
- **QR Code:** Links to a 30‑second audio walkthrough.

---

## Card 2 – 4‑7‑8 Breath (APA: Crouch, 2022)
- **Instruction:** Inhale for **4** seconds, hold for **7**, exhale for **8**.
- **Science:** Activates the parasympathetic vagal pathway, lowering heart rate (Crouch, 2022).
- **QR Code:** Audio cue for pacing.

---

## Card 3 – Body Scan (APA: Porges, 2023)
- **Instruction:** Starting at your toes, mentally note any sensation, move up to the crown of your head.
- **Science:** Reinforces interoceptive awareness, improving vagal tone (Porges, 2023).
- **QR Code:** Guided 60‑second body scan.

---

## Card 4 – “Safe Place” Visualization (APA: Van der Kolk, 2024)
- **Instruction:** Close eyes, imagine a place where you feel safe. Engage all five senses in the mental picture.
- **Science:** Engages the default mode network to provide a sense of security (Van der Kolk, 2024).
- **QR Code:** Short visualization script.

---

## Card 5 – Progressive Muscle Relaxation (APA: Feldman, 2020)
- **Instruction:** Tense each muscle group for 5 seconds, then release for 10 seconds, moving from head to toes.
- **Science:** Reduces sympathetic activation via proprioceptive feedback (Feldman, 2020).
- **QR Code:** Audio pacing for tension/release.

---

## Card 6 – Grounding with Movement (APA: Brown, 2022)
- **Instruction:** Stand, place feet shoulder‑width apart, gently sway side‑to‑side for 30 seconds while focusing on the feeling of your feet touching the floor.
- **Science:** Provides proprioceptive input to the cerebellum, which helps modulate stress responses (Brown, 2022).
- **QR Code:** Rhythm cue.

---

## How to Use
1. Print the sheet on cardstock (double‑sided). Cut along the lines.
2. Keep a few cards in your bag, on your desk, or in a wellness drawer.
3. When anxiety spikes, pick a card, follow the steps, and press play on the QR‑linked audio for guidance.
