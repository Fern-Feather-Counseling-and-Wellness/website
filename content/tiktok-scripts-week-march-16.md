## Script 1: "Your Anxiety Isn't Random"
**Hook:** Your anxiety isn't random. Your nervous system is trying to protect you from something that already happened.

**Body:**
That tight chest, racing thoughts, feeling like you need to run — that's not broken. That's your body remembering danger before your brain catches up.

See, when something threatening happened in your past, your nervous system created a pattern. A blueprint. Now anything that *reminds* your body of that threat — even if it's not actually dangerous — triggers the same response.

The traffic jam feels like being trapped.
The critical email feels like being attacked.
The silence in a conversation feels like abandonment.

Your anxiety isn't irrational. It's *archival*. It's your body protecting you based on old data.

The work isn't forcing yourself to calm down. It's updating that data. Teaching your nervous system: *This is different. This is now. You're safe here.*

## Script 2: "Stop Calling It Overreacting"
**Hook:** Stop calling it overreacting. Your nervous system is responding to something real — it just might not be happening right now.

**Body:**
Someone says "you're overreacting" and suddenly you're doubting yourself AND still activated. Cool cool cool.

But here's what they don't understand: your nervous system doesn't do "over." It does "survive." 

When you "overreact," you're actually experiencing:
- A memory your body stored as threat
- A pattern learned before you had words

That "overreaction" is your body going "I've been here before and it was BAD."

The goal isn't to minimize your response. It's to understand what your body is actually protecting you from. Because once you know, you can update the pattern.

## Script 3: "The Polyvagal Thing Everyone Gets Wrong"
**Hook:** The polyvagal thing everyone gets wrong — and why it actually matters.

**Body:**
You've probably heard "ventral vagal = good, dorsal = bad." Yeah, that's not it.

Your nervous system has three main states:
- **Sympathetic** — mobilized, activated, ready to act
- **Dorsal vagal** — immobilized, shut down, conserving energy  
- **Ventral vagal** — connected, regulated, socially engaged

But here's the thing: NONE of these are "bad." 

Dorsal shut down? That kept you alive when fighting wasn't an option.
Sympathetic activation? That's energy to protect yourself.
Ventral connection? That's when you feel safe enough to rest and relate.

The problem isn't HAVING these states. It's getting STUCK in one.

Healing isn't about staying "ventral." It's about having access to ALL of them — and moving between them with flexibility.

**CTA:** Which state do you get stuck in most? Comment below.

## Script 4: "Why You Can't Think Your Way Out of Anxiety"
**Hook:** Why you can't think your way out of anxiety — and what actually works.

**Body:**
You've tried:
- Telling yourself it's irrational
- Breathing exercises that made you more anxious
- Positive affirmations that felt like lies
- Thinking your way through it

And you're still anxious. Because anxiety isn't a thinking problem. It's a BODY problem.

Your nervous system activates BEFORE your prefrontal cortex comes online. By the time you're "thinking" about it, your body has already decided there's danger.

Trying to think your way out is like trying to reason with a fire alarm. The alarm doesn't care about your logic. It cares about smoke.

What works?
- Bottom-up regulation (somatic tools that speak your body's language)
- Safety cues your nervous system can FEEL, not just understand
- Practices that work WITH your physiology, not against it

## Script 5: "Your 'Lazy' Might Be Freeze"

You've probably called yourself lazy. Undisciplined. A procrastinator.

But what if I told you that "lazy" feeling is actually dorsal vagal shutdown? Your nervous system detected threat and went into conservation mode.

## Script 6: "The Attachment Wound Nobody Talks About"
**Hook:** The attachment wound nobody talks about — and why you feel "too much" in relationships.

**Body:**
You finally meet someone great. They're consistent. Available. Safe.

And you feel... bored? Anxious? Like something's wrong because nothing's wrong?

That's not self-sabotage. That's your nervous system confused by safety.

If you grew up with inconsistent care, your body learned:
- Chaos = familiar = safe (even when it's not)
- Calm = unfamiliar = suspicious (what's the catch?)
- Predictable = boring = abandonment coming

So when someone is actually stable, your nervous system goes "this feels wrong" — not because it IS wrong, but because it's DIFFERENT.

This is called an "earned secure" transition. It feels weird before it feels good.

The work? Tolerating safety long enough for your body to learn it's not a trap.

**CTA:** Who else has felt bored by healthy relationships? Just me?

**Hashtags:** #attachmenttheory #relationships #trauma #anxiousattachment #healing #mentalhealth

**Visual notes:** Romantic/dating visuals for hook. Nicole speaking with empathy and understanding. Use text overlay for "earned secure transition." Relatable, conversational tone.
