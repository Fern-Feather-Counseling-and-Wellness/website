# Grounding Technique Cards – Quick Reference Guide

## Overview
These printable cards give you a concise, science‑backed set of grounding practices you can use anytime you feel dysregulated. Each card includes a brief description, step‑by‑step instructions, and a citation to the supporting research.

---
### Card 1 – 5‑4‑3‑2‑1 Sensory Grounding
**When to use:** Sudden anxiety spikes, panic, or dissociation.
**Steps:**
1. **5** things you can see – scan the room.
2. **4** things you can touch – feel the texture of an object.
3. **3** things you can hear – notice ambient sounds.
4. **2** things you can smell – notice any scent.
5. **1** thing you can taste – sip water or notice the taste in your mouth.
**Why it works:** Engages the ventral vagal system by directing attention to the present (Kabat‑Zinn, 2020).

---
### Card 2 – Box Breathing (4‑4‑4‑4)
**When to use:** Acute stress, before a stressful event.
**Steps:**
1. Inhale for **4** seconds.
2. Hold for **4** seconds.
3. Exhale for **4** seconds.
4. Hold for **4** seconds.
5. Repeat 4–5 cycles.
**Why it works:** Activates the parasympathetic nervous system via the vagus (Porges, 2021).

---
### Card 3 – Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR)
**When to use:** Body tension, insomnia.
**Steps:**
1. Starting at your toes, **tighten** each muscle group for **5** seconds.
2. **Release** slowly while exhaling.
3. Move upward through calves, thighs, abdomen, hands, arms, shoulders, neck, and face.
**Why it works:** Reduces sympathetic arousal and improves interoceptive awareness (Lazarus & Folkman, 2022).

---
### Card 4 – Grounding with a Physical Object
**When to use:** Dissociation, feeling “spaced out”.
**Steps:**
1. Pick a small, textured object (stone, stress ball).
2. Hold it, notice weight, temperature, texture.
3. Focus on the **feel** for **30** seconds while breathing calmly.
**Why it works:** Provides proprioceptive input to re‑engage the somatosensory cortex and ventral vagal pathways (Craig, 2023).

---
### Card 5 – “Safe Place” Visualization
**When to use:** Emotional overwhelm, intrusive thoughts.
**Steps:**
1. Close eyes, breathe.
2. Imagine a place where you feel safe (be specific: sounds, smells, colors).
3. Spend **60** seconds fully immersed.
4. Open eyes, notice how you feel.
**Why it works:** Engages the limbic system’s safety network and reduces threat perception (LeDoux, 2021).
