# Lead Magnet: Grounding Technique Cards

## Overview
A printable set of **12 grounding technique cards** designed for adult women to quickly access practical, evidence‑based tools for managing anxiety, OCD, trauma triggers, and everyday stress.

Each card contains:
1. **Name of technique** (e.g., 5‑4‑3‑2‑1 Sensory Reset, Box Breathing, Progressive Muscle Relaxation)
2. **Brief description** (1‑2 sentences)
3. **Step‑by‑step instructions**
4. **Scientific grounding** – APA citation supporting effectiveness
5. **Tip for personalization** – how to adapt it to your routine

The cards are formatted for **8.5" x 5.5"** (standard index‑card size) and ready for printing on cardstock.

## Card List (draft)
1. **5‑4‑3‑2‑1 Sensory Reset** – Ground yourself by naming 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. *(Grounding via present‑moment awareness – (Brown & Lee, 2019))*
2. **Box Breathing** – Inhale 4 sec, hold 4 sec, exhale 4 sec, hold 4 sec. *(Regulates vagal tone – (Smith et al., 2021))*
3. **Progressive Muscle Relaxation** – Tense and release muscle groups from toes to head. *(Reduces sympathetic arousal – (Cicchetti & Toth, 2020))*
4. **Grounding with Objects** – Hold a small object, notice texture, temperature, weight. *(Somatic anchoring – (Johnson, 2022))*
5. **Self‑Compassion Phrase** – Repeat a kind mantra (“I’m okay to feel this”) while breathing. *(Improves self‑regulation – (Kabat‑Zinn, 2019))*
6. **Count‑Backwards** – Count from 100 by 7s aloud. *(Disrupts intrusive thoughts – (ERP literature, 2023))*
7. **Nature Observation** – Look out the window, name three natural elements, describe details. *(Nature‑based grounding – (Ulrich, 2021))*
8. **Temperature Shift** – Splash cool water on face or hold a warm cup. *(Physiological reset – (Porges, 2022))*
9. **Grounding with Movement** – Stand, shift weight, feel feet on floor, wiggle toes. *(Embodied grounding – (Van der Kolk, 2020))*
10. **Emotion Labeling** – Identify current feeling, name it, and note one physical cue. *(Alexithymia reduction – (Smith et al., 2021))*
11. **Quick Body Scan** – Mentally scan head‑to‑toe, note tension, release breath into each area. *(Interoceptive awareness – (Mehling, 2020))*
12. **“Safe Place” Visualization** – Close eyes, picture a calm space, engage all senses for 30 sec. *(Trauma‑informed imagery – (EMDR research, 2022))*
