## Carousel Caption

**EMDR Therapy Explained: Your Questions Answered**

If you've heard about EMDR but aren't sure what it actually involves, this guide breaks it down. 🧠

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is one of the most researched trauma treatments available today. Unlike traditional talk therapy, EMDR uses bilateral stimulation (like eye movements) to help your brain reprocess traumatic memories that feel "stuck."

**Key things to know:**
- EMDR follows a structured 8-phase approach
- You don't have to verbally describe traumatic details at length
- It often works faster than traditional therapy for single-event trauma
- You remain present and aware throughout — not "reliving" the trauma
- Recommended by WHO, APA, and VA for PTSD treatment

**Who it can help:**
- PTSD (combat, assault, accident survivors)
- Complex trauma from childhood
- Anxiety, panic attacks, phobias
- Grief and loss that feels "stuck"
- First responders and healthcare workers
