## Workshop Philosophy

This isn't "yoga for trauma survivors." This is **nervous system education through embodied practice.** Participants learn the biology of their stress responses while experiencing tools that actually work—not because they're mystical, but because they're physiological.

**Core Principles:**
- No hands-on adjustments without explicit consent
- All poses are invitational, not prescriptive
- Choice and agency are prioritized over "correct" form
- Education is science-based, not spiritual (unless requested)
- Somatic experiencing principles integrated throughout

## Day 1: Understanding Your Nervous System

- Safety planning: exits, breaks, opting out

**10:30-11:30 — Nervous System 101 Lecture**
- Autonomic nervous system basics
- Sympathetic (fight/flight) vs parasympathetic (rest/digest)

### Afternoon Session (2:00pm - 5:00pm)

- Individual exercise: mapping your personal window

## Facilitator Notes

### Required Training
- 200-hour yoga teacher training minimum
- Trauma-informed yoga certification (recommend: Trauma Center, YogaFit, or similar)
- Somatic Experiencing or Sensorimotor Psychotherapy training preferred
- Group facilitation experience

### Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Room setup: mats in circle, no mirrors if possible
- [ ] Props: blocks, blankets, bolsters available
- [ ] Music playlist: ambient, no lyrics, 60-80 BPM
- [ ] Handouts: nervous system diagrams, resource lists
- [ ] Emergency plan: local crisis resources, exit procedures
- [ ] Self-care: facilitator support plan

### Adaptations for Different Populations

**For survivors of physical/sexual violence:**
- Extra emphasis on choice and agency
- No hands-on adjustments
- Careful with hip openers and prone positions
- More grounding, less activation

**For those with complex trauma/C-PTSD:**
- Slower pacing, more repetition
- Emphasis on resourcing before exploration
- Shorter sessions with breaks
- Clear container and boundaries

**For activists/burned-out helpers:**
- Focus on parasympathetic activation
- Permission to receive, not just give
- Community and co-regulation emphasis
- Sustainability over intensity
