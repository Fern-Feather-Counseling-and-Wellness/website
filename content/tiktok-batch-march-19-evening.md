## Script 1: What is IFS Therapy? (Internal Family Systems)

**Visual Concept:**
- Split screen showing "parts" animation
- Simple diagram of Self vs. Protectors/Exiles
- Calm, reassuring visuals

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Do you ever feel like there are different parts of you fighting for control? That's actually a normal nervous system response."

**Main Content (3-30s):**
"IFS therapy sees your mind as a system of parts — like an internal family.
- Managers: Keep you safe through control
- Firefighters: Distract from pain (hello, binge-watching)
- Exiles: Carrying old wounds

The goal isn't to eliminate parts — it's to help your *Self* (the calm, compassionate core) lead the system.

It's not multiple personality disorder — it's recognizing that we all have conflicting impulses because our nervous system developed different strategies to survive."

## Script 2: Polyvagal Theory — Simplified

**Visual Concept:**
- Simple ladder animation (ventral → sympathetic → dorsal)
- Body scan showing where each state lives
- Before/after regulation visuals

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Polyvagal theory isn't just therapy jargon — it's your body's threat detection system explained."

**Main Content (3-30s):**
"Think of it as a 3-rung ladder:
- **Ventral Vagal (Top):** Safe & social. Connection feels possible.
- **Sympathetic (Middle):** Fight/flight. Everything feels urgent.
- **Dorsal Vagal (Bottom):** Shutdown. Numbness, dissociation.

Your body moves up and down this ladder based on perceived safety — not just actual danger.

The goal of trauma therapy isn't to 'stop' moving down the ladder — it's to build pathways back up faster."

**CTA (30-45s):**
"Wondering where you are on the ladder right now? My Window of Tolerance guide explains how to recognize your state (link in bio)."

## Script 3: Somatic Experiencing — Beyond Talk Therapy

**Visual Concept:**
- Animation of trauma "stuck" in body
- Simple body scanning technique
- Before/after posture comparison

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Trauma isn't just stored in memories — it lives in your body's tension patterns."

**Main Content (3-30s):**
"Somatic Experiencing works with the body's natural ability to complete stress cycles.

When trauma happens, your nervous system gets interrupted mid-response. SE helps complete those incomplete physiological responses.

It's not about reliving the trauma — it's about noticing subtle body sensations and allowing gentle completion.

Example: That tension in your shoulders might be your body still bracing for a threat that passed years ago."

## Script 4: Your Window of Tolerance — Daily Practice

**Visual Concept:**
- Simple graphic of window expanding/contracting
- Daily life examples (traffic, difficult conversations)
- Micro-practices shown visually

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Ever notice how some days you can handle anything, and other days the smallest thing overwhelms you? That's your Window of Tolerance."

**Main Content (3-30s):**
"Your Window is your capacity to handle stress without flipping into fight/flight or shutdown.

It's not fixed — it expands with regulation practices and contracts with stress, lack of sleep, or illness.

Daily micro-practices widen your Window:
- Morning: 2 minutes of shaking out tension
- Midday: 3 physiological sighs
- Evening: Hand on heart, feeling heartbeat

Wider Window = More resilience without burnout."
