# LPC Supervision Curriculum & Materials
## Fern & Feather Wellness Center

**For:** Post-masters clinicians pursuing LPC licensure in Georgia  
**Duration:** 24 months (minimum required supervision hours)  
**Structure:** Weekly individual (1 hour) + biweekly group (2 hours)  
**Rate:** $100/hour individual, $50/hour group

---

## Supervision Philosophy

This isn't compliance-focused box-checking. This is **professional identity development** with a dual lens: clinical excellence AND sustainable practice. Fern & Feather supervision prepares clinicians not just to get licensed, but to build careers that don't burn them out.

**Core Values:**
- Trauma-informed practice as foundation, not specialty
- Social justice and systemic awareness integrated throughout
- Somatic and body-based approaches alongside traditional methods
- Business literacy for private practice survival
- Self-of-the-therapist as primary intervention tool

## 24-Month Curriculum Overview

### Phase 1: Foundations (Months 1-6)
**Theme:** Building the Container

| Month | Focus | Key Topics |
|-------|-------|------------|
| 1 | Intake & Assessment | Diagnostic skills, differential diagnosis, risk assessment, documentation standards |
| 2 | Therapeutic Relationship | Alliance building, rupture and repair, transference/countertransference, self-awareness |
| 3 | Treatment Planning | Evidence-based practice, goal-setting, measuring outcomes, adapting plans |
| 4 | Crisis Management | Suicide assessment, safety planning, duty to warn, documentation under pressure |
| 5 | Case Conceptualization | Theoretical orientation development, formulation models, client systems |
| 6 | Ethics Core | Georgia ethics code, professional boundaries, scope of practice, consultation norms |

**Phase 1 Assessment:**
- Complete at least 2 comprehensive case presentations
- Pass ethics quiz (80% minimum)
- Demonstrate intake competency via role-play or recording review
- Submit one risk assessment documentation sample

### Phase 2: Deepening (Months 7-14)
**Theme:** Clinical Sophistication

| Month | Focus | Key Topics |
|-------|-------|------------|
| 7 | Trauma-Specific Work | Trauma-informed principles, stabilization vs. processing, safety vs. risk |
| 8 | Somatic Approaches | Body-based interventions, nervous system regulation, tracking body states |
| 9 | Attachment & Relationships | Attachment theory in practice, couples work basics, family systems introduction |
| 10 | Diverse Populations | Cultural humility, LGBTQ+ affirming practice, neurodiversity, anti-racist therapy |
| 11 | Advanced Ethics | Boundary crossings vs. violations, gifts, self-disclosure, dual relationships |
| 12 | Mid-Point Integration | Case presentations, theory-practice integration, emerging clinical identity |
| 13 | Complex Cases | Treatment-resistant clients, personality disorders, severe and persistent mental illness |
| 14 | Professional Development | Specialization exploration, continuing education planning, career mapping |

**Phase 2 Assessment:**
- Present one complex trauma case with formulation
- Complete cultural competency self-assessment and learning plan
- Demonstrate somatic technique competency
- Receive satisfactory mid-point evaluation

### Phase 3: Integration (Months 15-22)
**Theme:** Becoming a Colleague

| Month | Focus | Key Topics |
|-------|-------|------------|
| 15 | Group Facilitation | Group dynamics, facilitation skills, trauma-informed groups |
| 16 | Supervision of Supervision | Peer consultation skills, mentoring approach, recursive learning |
| 17 | Business & Practice | Private practice basics, insurance vs. private pay, marketing ethics |
| 18 | Documentation & Legal | Progress notes, treatment records, subpoenas, court testimony |
| 19 | Burnout Prevention | Self-care as ethical imperative, sustainable practice design, boundaries |
| 20 | Advanced Case Conceptualization | Multi-theoretical integration, client systems, intergenerational patterns |
| 21 | Leadership & Advocacy | Professional involvement, systems change, consultation skills |
| 22 | Licensure Prep | Exam preparation, application process, transition planning |

**Phase 3 Assessment:**
- Co-facilitate mock group supervision session
- Present private practice business plan (even if not pursuing)
- Complete mock licensure exam (70% minimum)
- Demonstrate competency in all core areas via final case presentation

### Phase 4: Launch (Months 23-24)
**Theme:** Independence with Support

| Month | Focus | Key Topics |
|-------|-------|------------|
| 23 | Independent Practice | Reduced supervision intensity, autonomous decision-making, peer consultation setup |
| 24 | Colleague Transition | Ending supervision relationship, ongoing consultation agreement, alumni community |

**Phase 4 Assessment:**
- Final comprehensive case presentation
- Self-evaluation of growth and learning
- Supervisee evaluation of supervision received
- Licensure application submitted or ready

## Group Supervision Structure

**Biweekly 2-Hour Sessions:**

**Format A: Case Presentation (Week 1)**
- 10 min: Check-in and grounding
- 30 min: Case presentation (rotating supervisee)
- 40 min: Group discussion and consultation
- 30 min: Didactic teaching on rotating topic
- 10 min: Closing and integration

**Format B: Skill-Building (Week 2)**
- 10 min: Check-in
- 20 min: Topic introduction and mini-lecture
- 60 min: Experiential exercise or role-play
- 20 min: Debrief and application discussion
- 10 min: Closing

**Group Topics Rotation:**
1. Risk assessment practice
2. Somatic technique experiential
3. Difficult conversation role-plays
4. Documentation review and feedback
5. Ethics case studies
6. Cultural consultation
7. Attachment-based interventions
8. Business Q&A panel

## Risk Management & Ethics Protocols

### When to Immediately Consult
- Client expresses imminent suicidal intent
- Suspected child/elder/vulnerable adult abuse
- Client threatens harm to specific others
- Boundary confusion or crossing
- Countertransference interfering with treatment
- Ethical dilemma without clear answer

### Documentation of Concerns
- Verbal feedback given immediately
- Written note in supervision record
- Plan for addressing concern documented
- Follow-up scheduled

### Remediation Process
1. Identify specific concern with clear examples
2. Develop written remediation plan with timeline
3. Increase supervision intensity if needed
4. Document progress regularly
5. Evaluate readiness for independent practice
6. If unsucessful: facilitate referral to alternative supervisor
