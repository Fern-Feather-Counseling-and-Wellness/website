Somatic therapy works directly with those physical patterns. We teach your body it's safe NOW, not then.

## Script 2: "The 3-Minute Nervous System Scan"

**Hook (0-3 sec):**  
*"Stop scrolling. I need 3 minutes of your time."*

**Script (45 sec):**

"Quick somatic check-in. Do this with me.

First: Notice your jaw. Is it clenched? Just notice. Don't change it yet.

Second: Check your shoulders. Are they up by your ears? Mine were.

Third: Place one hand on your chest. One on your belly. Which hand is moving more when you breathe?

If it's your chest hand, you're breathing in stress mode.

Here's the fix: Exhale twice as long as you inhale. I'll wait.

In for 4... out for 8.

Do that 3 times.

You just downregulated your nervous system. That's somatic therapy in 3 minutes.

Follow for more tools that actually work."

## Script 3: "Your Trauma Isn't 'Stored' in Your Body (And Why That Matters)"

**Hook (0-3 sec):**  
*"Your trauma isn't 'stored' in your hips. Let me explain."*

**Script (60 sec):**

"I need to clear something up because this metaphor has gotten out of hand.

You hear: 'Trauma is stored in the body.'

What's actually happening: Your nervous system learned protective patterns. And those patterns involve your body.

When you were unsafe, you tightened. You held your breath. You made yourself small.

Your body didn't 'store' the trauma like a filing cabinet. It created a survival strategy.

And here's the thing — that strategy was brilliant. It kept you alive. It kept you safe.

But now? That same tension, that same holding, is exhausting you.

Somatic work isn't about 'releasing stored trauma.' It's about teaching your nervous system that the danger is over.

Your body isn't a storage unit. It's a learning system. And it can learn safety.

Follow for trauma education that actually makes sense."

## Script 4: "What Happens in a Somatic Therapy Session"

**Hook (0-3 sec):**  
*"POV: Your first somatic therapy session."*

**Script (45 sec):**

"You're nervous. I get it. Here's exactly what happens.

We start with talking. What's bringing you in? What's not working?

Then I ask: 'Where do you feel that in your body?'

You might say: 'My chest feels tight.'

I say: 'Stay with that sensation. What's the quality? Heavy? Constricted? Buzzing?'

We track it together. We notice if it shifts. If it wants to move.

Sometimes you cry. Sometimes you yawn. Sometimes your body finally lets go.

We don't analyze it to death. We let your body process what it's been holding.

And yes, we talk too. But we talk *with* your body, not around it.

Questions? Drop them below."

## Script 5: "The Difference Between Anxiety and Nervous System Dysregulation"

**Hook (0-3 sec):**  
*"Your anxiety might not be anxiety. Here's how to tell."*

**Script (60 sec):**

"Anxiety is a thought: 'Something bad is going to happen.'

Nervous system dysregulation is a body state: racing heart, shallow breathing, can't sit still, mind spinning.

They often happen together. But treating them the same doesn't work.

If you only address the thoughts — CBT, challenging cognitions, journaling — you miss the body.

Your nervous system is still in survival mode, even when your brain knows you're safe.

Real example: Client knows her partner isn't going to leave her. Brain totally gets it. But her body? Panic every time they disagree.

We didn't need more thought work. We needed body-based regulation.

Breath. Grounding. Tracking sensations. Teaching her body that disagreement ≠ abandonment.

If CBT hasn't worked for you, this might be why.

Follow for more nervous system education."
