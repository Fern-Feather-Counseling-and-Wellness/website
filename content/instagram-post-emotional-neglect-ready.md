**Slide 2 – Sign 1**
"Lack of Emotional Validation"

*Copy:* Growing up, you might have felt your feelings were “wrong” or “unimportant.” In adulthood, this shows up as self‑doubt or difficulty trusting your emotions. (Cicchetti & Toth, 2020)

---

**Slide 3 – Sign 2**
"Difficulty Identifying Feelings (Alexithymia)"

*Copy:* Struggling to label emotions can lead to feeling “numb.” It often stems from a childhood where emotions weren’t named or discussed. (Smith et al., 2021)

---

**Slide 4 – Sign 3**
"People‑Pleasing & Over‑Responsibility"

*Copy:* When caregivers neglect emotional needs, children learn to prioritize others’ needs over their own. In adults, this appears as chronic people‑pleasing. (Brown & Lee, 2019)

---

**Slide 5 – Sign 4**
"Isolation & Fear of Intimacy"

*Copy:* Without early emotional connection, intimacy can feel unsafe. You might withdraw or fear closeness in relationships. (Johnson, 2022)
