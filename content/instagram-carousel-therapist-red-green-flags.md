## RED FLAGS (When to Consider Walking Away)

### Slide 3: Red Flag #1 — Lack of Boundaries

**Header:** 🚩 RED FLAG #1: POOR BOUNDARIES

**Body:**
- Shares excessive personal details
- Tries to be your friend outside sessions
- Texts/calls outside business hours (except for scheduling)
- Talks about other clients

**Why it's a problem:** Blurred boundaries prevent objective care and can lead to ethical violations.

**Alternative:** Professional distance with warmth and empathy.

---

### Slide 4: Red Flag #2 — Judgmental or Shaming

**Header:** 🚩 RED FLAG #2: JUDGMENT & SHAME

**Body:**
- Tells you what you "should" feel/do
- Invalidates your experiences ("That's not a big deal")
- Uses guilt or shame as motivation
- Blames you for lack of progress

**Why it's a problem:** Therapy should be a judgment-free zone. Shame blocks healing.

**Alternative:** Curiosity and validation, even when challenging you.

---

### Slide 5: Red Flag #3 — No Clear Treatment Plan

**Header:** 🚩 RED FLAG #3: NO DIRECTION

**Body:**
- Sessions feel aimless or repetitive
- No discussion of goals or progress
- Can't explain their approach or why they're doing what they're doing
- You leave wondering "What was the point?"

**Why it's a problem:** Therapy should have direction and measurable progress.

**Alternative:** Collaborative goal-setting and regular check-ins about progress.

---

### Slide 6: Red Flag #4 — Pushing Their Agenda

**Header:** 🚩 RED FLAG #4: THEIR AGENDA > YOURS

**Body:**
- Pushes spiritual/religious views you didn't ask for
- Insists their preferred modality is the only right way
- Dismisses your concerns about their approach
- More invested in being "right" than helping you

**Why it's a problem:** Therapy is about YOUR needs, not the therapist's beliefs.

**Alternative:** Respect for your values and collaborative approach selection.

---

### Slide 7: Red Flag #5 — Unprofessional Behavior

**Header:** 🚩 RED FLAG #5: UNPROFESSIONALISM

**Body:**
- Frequently late, cancels last-minute
- Forgets important details about you
- Disorganized, messy notes
- Poor confidentiality practices

**Why it's a problem:** You're paying for a professional service. Basic reliability matters.

**Alternative:** Consistent, prepared, respectful of your time and investment.

---

## GREEN FLAGS (Signs of a Good Therapist)

### Slide 8: Green Flag #1 — Clear Communication

**Header:** ✅ GREEN FLAG #1: TRANSPARENCY

**Body:**
- Explains their approach and why
- Clear about fees, policies, boundaries
- Answers questions honestly
- Admits when they don't know something

**Why it matters:** You should never wonder "What's happening here?" in therapy.

**Example:** "I use CBT because research shows it's effective for anxiety. Here's what that looks like..."

---

### Slide 9: Green Flag #2 — Collaborative Approach

**Header:** ✅ GREEN FLAG #2: COLLABORATION

**Body:**
- Asks for your feedback regularly
- Adjusts approach based on what works for YOU
- Views you as the expert on your experience
- "We're a team" mentality

**Why it matters:** You're an active participant in your healing, not a passive recipient.

**Example:** "How did that exercise feel? Should we try something different next time?"

---

### Slide 10: Green Flag #3 — Cultural Humility

**Header:** ✅ GREEN FLAG #3: CULTURAL COMPETENCE

**Body:**
- Asks about your cultural background/identity
- Acknowledges their own biases/limitations
- Adapts approach to your cultural context
- Doesn't assume their experience = universal

**Why it matters:** Your identity shapes your experience. Good therapy honors that.

**Example:** "As a white therapist, I recognize I might miss things. Please correct me if I make assumptions."

---

### Slide 11: Green Flag #4 — Focus on Your Strengths

**Header:** ✅ GREEN FLAG #4: STRENGTHS-BASED

**Body:**
- Notices what you're doing right
- Helps you build on existing skills
- Believes in your capacity to heal
- Celebrates progress (even small wins)

**Why it matters:** Healing comes from amplifying strengths, not just fixing deficits.

**Example:** "I notice you're really resilient when X happens. Let's build on that."

---

### Slide 12: Green Flag #5 — Professional Growth

**Header:** ✅ GREEN FLAG #5: COMMITTED TO LEARNING

**Body:**
- Pursues ongoing training/education
- Stays current with research
- Seeks consultation when stuck
- Open to feedback and correction

**Why it matters:** Therapy evolves. Your therapist should too.

**Example:** "I just attended a training on polyvagal theory. Would you be interested in trying some of those techniques?"
