## Slide 2: Beyond Symbols

**Visual:** Rainbow flag with question mark overlay
**Text:**
**Affirmation ≠ Just a Flag**
True affirmation goes beyond symbols:
• Using your correct name & pronouns without "slips"
• Understanding LGBTQ+ community history & trauma
• Knowing current terminology (nonbinary, ace, pan, etc.)
• Recognizing intersectionality (race, disability, neurodivergence)

**Key Point:** Symbols without substance = performative allyship

---

## Slide 3: Cultural Competence

**Visual:** Diverse group of people in supportive circle
**Text:**
**Cultural Competence Matters**
A therapist should understand:
• **Coming out dynamics** (different for everyone)
• **Minority stress** (daily microaggressions add up)
• **Family rejection** impact on mental health
• **Conversion therapy trauma** (still legal in many states)
• **Healthcare discrimination** fears

**Fact:** LGBTQ+ youth are 4x more likely to attempt suicide than cishet peers (Trevor Project, 2025)

---

## Slide 4: Gender-Affirming Care

**Visual:** Gender spectrum illustration
**Text:**
**Gender-Affirming ≠ Just Trans Care**
Affirming all gender identities:
• **Trans & nonbinary** — medical transition support letters, social transition guidance
• **Genderfluid** — honoring day-to-day identity shifts
• **Cisgender** — examining gender roles & expectations
• **Questioning** — space to explore without pressure

**Note:** WPATH Standards of Care (v8) knowledge required for competent trans care

---

## Slide 5: Sexual Orientation Affirmation

**Visual:** Diverse relationship configurations illustration
**Text:**
**All Sexualities Valid**
Competent therapists understand:
• **Asexuality spectrum** (ace, gray-ace, demi)
• **Pansexual vs. bisexual** distinctions
• **Compulsory heterosexuality** impact
• **Polyamory/ENM** — not pathology, valid relationship structure
• **Internalized homophobia** — unpacking societal messaging

**Remember:** Someone's identity isn't a "phase" to be "worked through"

---

## Slide 6: Intersectionality in Action

**Visual:** Intersecting circles showing race, disability, class, gender, sexuality
**Text:**
**You're More Than One Identity**
Intersectional affirmation means:
• **BIPOC LGBTQ+** — understanding racial + LGBTQ+ discrimination
• **Disabled LGBTQ+** — accessibility beyond physical (sensory, cognitive)
• **Neurodivergent LGBTQ+** — recognizing autism/ADHD + identity intersections
• **Immigrant LGBTQ+** — cultural + identity navigation
• **Aging LGBTQ+** — different generational experiences

**Key:** No "one size fits all" LGBTQ+ experience

---

## Slide 7: What to Look For

**Visual:** Checklist graphic
**Text:**
**Green Flags in an Affirming Therapist**
✅ **Asks:** "What pronouns should I use?"
✅ **Has LGBTQ+ specific training** (beyond one workshop)
✅ **Knows community resources** (support groups, legal aid)
✅ **Doesn't assume** relationship structures or family plans
✅ **Understands consensual non-monogamy** isn't pathology
✅ **Affirms chosen family** as valid support system
✅ **Acknowledges their own biases** regularly

**Red Flag:** "I treat everyone the same" (ignores unique oppression)
