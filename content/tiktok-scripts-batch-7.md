## Script 1: "Your Nervous System Isn't Broken"

**Hook (0-3 sec):**  
*[Text on screen: "Your nervous system isn't broken — it's PROTECTING you"]*

**Script:**
> "Your anxiety isn't a defect. Your overthinking isn't a character flaw. Your nervous system is doing exactly what it evolved to do — keep you alive.
>
> The problem? It's using old software to solve new problems.
>
> That presentation at work? Your brain reads it like a saber-toothed tiger. That text you haven't answered? Same threat response as physical danger.
>
> You're not broken. You're biologically loyal to a fault. And that loyalty? It can be updated."

**CTA:** "Follow for somatic tools that actually work 🧠"

**Visual notes:** Fast cuts between "threat" scenarios (work presentation, unread texts) and calm regulation. Use trending audio with build-up/drop.

**Hashtags:** #anxietyrelief #nervoussystemregulation #somatictherapy #traumainformed #mentalhealthmatters #therapytok #anxietytips

## Script 2: "The 'Just Breathe' Lie"

**Hook (0-3 sec):**  
*[Text on screen: "Why 'just breathe' makes your anxiety WORSE"]*

**Script:**
> "Someone tells you to 'just breathe' when you're panicking. You try. It doesn't work. Now you feel like a failure ON TOP OF being anxious.
>
> Here's why: When your nervous system is in fight-or-flight, deep breathing can actually feel threatening. Your body thinks you're trying to override its protection system.
>
> What works instead? Grounding through sensation. Cold water on your wrists. Feeling your feet on the floor. Naming 5 things you see.
>
> Don't bypass your body's alarm system — reassure it first."

**CTA:** "Save this for your next anxious moment 💙"

**Visual notes:** Split screen — "just breathe" advice failing vs. grounding techniques working. Show actual techniques being demonstrated.

**Hashtags:** #anxietyhack #groundingtechniques #panicattackhelp #somatichealing #mentalhealthtips #therapisttok #anxietyrecovery

## Script 3: "Therapists Have Therapists"

**Hook (0-3 sec):**  
*[Text on screen: "Confession: I see a therapist. Here's why."]*

**Script:**
> "Plot twist: Your therapist has a therapist.
>
> I sit in the client chair every week. Not because I'm broken. Because I'm human. Because holding space for other people's pain requires me to process my own.
>
> The best therapists aren't the ones who 'have it all figured out.' They're the ones who've done their own work. Who know what it's like to sit in vulnerability. Who don't ask clients to go anywhere they haven't gone themselves.
>
> If your therapist claims they don't need therapy... that's a red flag, not a credential."

**CTA:** "Therapists in the comments — am I wrong? 👇"

**Visual notes:** Authentic, talking-head style. Maybe show a glimpse of "therapy day" routine. Trending relatable audio.

**Hashtags:** #therapisttok #therapyworks #mentalhealthawareness #therapistsoftiktok #authenticity #behindthechair #therapymyths

## Script 4: "Window of Tolerance Explained in 60 Seconds"

**Hook (0-3 sec):**  
*[Text on screen: "The 'Window of Tolerance' will change how you understand anxiety"]*

**Script:**
> "Imagine your nervous system has a window. Inside that window? You can think clearly, handle stress, respond instead of react.
>
> When anxiety spikes, you get pushed UP out of the window — hyperarousal. Racing thoughts, panic, can't sit still.
>
> When trauma triggers, you might drop DOWN out of the window — hypoarousal. Numb, frozen, disconnected.
>
> Most coping skills fail because they don't match WHERE you are. Stuck in hyperarousal? Calming techniques. Stuck in hypoarousal? Energizing, grounding movement.
>
> The goal isn't to never leave your window. It's to recognize when you do — and know exactly how to get back."

**CTA:** "Which one do you experience more — hyper or hypo? 👇"

**Visual notes:** Animated window graphic showing hyperarousal (up) and hypoarousal (down). Use color coding (red for hyper, blue for hypo, green for window).

**Hashtags:** #windowoftolerance #traumaeducation #anxietyexplained #nervoussystem #somatictherapy #mentalhealthtok #polyvagaltheory

## Script 5: "The Attachment Style You Can't Find in a Quiz"

**Hook (0-3 sec):**  
*[Text on screen: "There's a 5th attachment style no one talks about"]*

**Script:**
> "Everyone knows anxious, avoidant, secure, disorganized. But there's a fifth — and it describes about 40% of the people I work with.
>
> I call it 'earned secure.'
>
> You grew up with inconsistent or overwhelmed caregivers. You developed anxious or avoidant patterns. But then you did the work. Therapy. Self-reflection. Choosing better relationships.
>
> Now? You're mostly secure. But under extreme stress, those old patterns whisper. You know the tools, but you still feel the pull.
>
> That's not backsliding. That's being human. Earned secure means you built safety from scratch — and that's something to be proud of."

**CTA:** "Tag someone who's done the work 💪"

**Visual notes:** Text overlays showing the 4 traditional styles, then revealing the 5th. Authentic, encouraging tone. Soft background music.

**Hashtags:** #attachmentstyles #earnedsecure #attachmenthealing #relationshiptok #traumarecovery #therapytok #emotionalhealing
