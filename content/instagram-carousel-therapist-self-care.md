## Slide 3: Strategy #1 — Structural Boundaries

**Header:** 🛡️ STRATEGY 1: STRUCTURAL BOUNDARIES

**Body:**
**What it is:** Building limits into your schedule, not just willpower.

**Examples:**
- Buffer time between clients (15+ minutes)
- No back-to-back trauma sessions
- Protected admin day weekly
- Hard stop on work hours

**Why it works:** Prevents cumulative exhaustion before it starts.

**Icon:** Calendar with shield

---

## Slide 4: Strategy #2 — Peer Consultation

**Header:** 👥 STRATEGY 2: PEER CONSULTATION

**Body:**
**What it is:** Regular, structured support with colleagues.

**Examples:**
- Weekly case consultation group
- Co-reflection partners
- "No advice, just witnessing" sessions
- Shared resource library

**Why it works:** Reduces isolation, provides reality-check, spreads emotional load.

**Icon:** Two people connecting

---

## Slide 5: Strategy #3 — Body-Based Resets

**Header:** 🧠 STRATEGY 3: BODY-BASED RESETS

**Body:**
**What it is:** Using physiology to regulate nervous system after intense sessions.

**Examples:**
- 5-minute somatic shake-out
- Temperature change (cold water on wrists)
- Breath pattern shifting (4-7-8 breathing)
- Grounding through feet

**Why it works:** Directly addresses fight/flight activation from client stories.

**Icon:** Body with arrows showing energy flow

---

## Slide 6: Strategy #4 — Deliberate Detachment

**Header:** 🚪 STRATEGY 4: DELIBERATE DETACHMENT

**Body:**
**What it is:** Intentional mental separation from work.

**Examples:**
- "Leaving work at work" ritual
- Physical transition (change clothes, commute route)
- Mental container visualization
- No client stories at home

**Why it works:** Prevents work thoughts from invading personal life.

**Icon:** Door closing between work and home

---

## Slide 7: Strategy #5 — Professional Development as Self-Care

**Header:** 📚 STRATEGY 5: PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT AS SELF-CARE

**Body:**
**What it is:** Learning that fills your cup, not drains it.

**Examples:**
- Training in modalities YOU enjoy
- Reading for pleasure (not just CEUs)
- Creative clinical projects
- Mentoring/supervision (giving and receiving)

**Why it works:** Combats stagnation, reconnects with why you entered the field.

**Icon:** Growing plant with books

---

## Slide 8: Warning Signs You're Approaching Burnout

**Header:** ⚠️ WARNING SIGNS

**Body:**
**Early signs:**
- Dreading sessions you used to enjoy
- Cynicism about client progress
- Physical tension that doesn't resolve
- Emotional numbness between sessions

**Advanced signs:**
- Making clinical errors
- Dreaming about clients
- Isolating from colleagues
- Considering leaving the field entirely

**Icon:** Warning triangle
