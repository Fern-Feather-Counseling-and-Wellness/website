**Visual:** Balanced scale or peaceful imagery in green

**Text:**
```
SECURE ATTACHMENT
(~50% of people)

COMFORTABLE WITH:
✓ Closeness
✓ Being alone
✓ Communicating needs
✓ Healthy conflict

IN RELATIONSHIPS:
"I can depend on you
and still be myself."

```

## Slide 4: Anxious Attachment

**Visual:** Heart with waves or stormy imagery in orange/yellow

**Text:**
```
ANXIOUS ATTACHMENT
(~20% of people)

THE FEAR:
"You'll leave me."

PATTERNS:
→ Seeking constant
  reassurance
→ Overthinking
→ Fear of abandonment
→ Emotional highs/lows
→ Difficulty being alone

UNDERNEATH:
Deep need for connection
and fear of rejection.
```

---

## Slide 5: Avoidant Attachment

**Visual:** Person with small wall or distant imagery in blue

**Text:**
```
AVOIDANT ATTACHMENT
(~25% of people)

THE FEAR:
"You'll trap me."

PATTERNS:
→ Pulling away when
  things get close
→ Difficulty with
  vulnerability
→ Valuing independence
  over connection
→ Emotional shutdown
→ "I need space"

UNDERNEATH:
Deep fear of being
controlled or hurt.
```

---

## Slide 6: Disorganized Attachment

**Visual:** Confused/intertwined lines or mixed imagery in purple

**Text:**
```
DISORGANIZED ATTACHMENT
(~5% of people)

THE FEAR:
"I need you AND
I'm terrified of you."

PATTERNS:
→ Push-pull dynamics
→ Intense but unstable
  relationships
→ Difficulty trusting
→ Often from childhood
  trauma/neglect

UNDERNEATH:
The person who was
supposed to keep you
safe was also scary.
```

## Design Notes

**Color Coding by Style:**
- Secure: Sage green (#8FA895)
- Anxious: Warm orange (#D4A373)
- Avoidant: Calm blue (#6B8E9F)
- Disorganized: Soft purple (#9B8AA5)

**Background:**
- Cream (#F5F1EB) for all slides
- Colored accents matching attachment style

**Fonts:**
- Headers: Bold sans-serif (Montserrat Bold)
- Body: Clean sans-serif (Inter)
- Size: 24-28px headers, 18-20px body

**Style:**
- Minimal, calming aesthetic
- Consistent branding
- Simple icons or line art
- High contrast for readability

**Accessibility:**
- Alt text for all visuals
- Color contrast meets WCAG standards
- Large enough text for mobile
