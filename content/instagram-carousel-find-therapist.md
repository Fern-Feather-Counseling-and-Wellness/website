## Slide 3: Tip #1 — Check the Basics

**Visual:** Checklist or credential verification imagery

**Text:**
```
1. CHECK THE BASICS

✓ Licensed in your state
✓ Trained in your concerns
  (anxiety, trauma, etc.)
✓ Accepts your insurance
  OR fits your budget
✓ Has availability that
  works for you

Red flag: Won't answer
basic questions before
you book.
```

---

## Slide 4: Tip #2 — The Consultation Test

**Visual:** Phone call or video chat imagery

**Text:**
```
2. DO A CONSULTATION

Most therapists offer
a free 15-min call.

Pay attention to:
→ Do they listen?
→ Do you feel heard?
→ Is their approach
  clear?
→ Do you feel safe
  (even a little)?

Trust your gut.
If it feels off,
keep looking.
```

---

## Slide 5: Tip #3 — Ask These Questions

**Visual:** Speech bubbles with questions

```
3. ASK THESE QUESTIONS

"What approach do

"How do you handle
crisis situations?"

Good therapists
welcome questions.
```

## Design Notes

**Color Palette:**
- Background: #F5F1EB (warm cream)
- Primary text: #2C3E33 (deep forest green)
- Accent: #8FA895 (sage green)
- Highlight: #D4A373 (warm terracotta)

**Fonts:**
- Headers: Bold sans-serif (Montserrat Bold)
- Body: Clean sans-serif (Inter)
- Size: 24-28px headers, 18-20px body

**Style:**
- Minimal, calming aesthetic
- Checkmarks and bullet points for readability
- Simple icons or line illustrations
- Consistent branding elements

**Accessibility:**
- High contrast text
- Large enough font for mobile
- Alt text for all visuals
