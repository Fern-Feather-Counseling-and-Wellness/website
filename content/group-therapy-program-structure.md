# Fern & Feather Group Therapy Program Structure

## Program Philosophy

**"Healing happens in connection."**

Group therapy at Fern & Feather is designed for deep nervous system work that individual therapy can't fully replicate. The power of co-regulation, witnessing, and shared experience creates unique opportunities for healing—especially for attachment wounds, shame, and isolation.

**Core Principles:**
- Small groups (6-8 members) for safety and intimacy
- Closed groups (same members for full duration) for trust-building
- Trauma-informed facilitation with somatic integration
- Justice-oriented: all groups are LGBTQ+ affirming, anti-racist, body-positive
- Science-backed approaches with measurable outcomes

## Program Offerings

**The Foundation**

- **Duration:** 8 weeks
- **Frequency:** Weekly, 90 minutes
- **Group Size:** 6-8 members
- **Format:** Closed group (intake required)
- **Price:** $85/session ($680 total) or $620 if paid upfront
- **Facilitator:** Lead therapist (Nicole or Kiera)

**Target Participants:**
- Anxiety, panic, or chronic stress
- Difficulty regulating emotions
- History of trauma (single incident or complex)

- Interested in somatic approaches

**Curriculum Overview:**
| Week | Theme | Focus |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Mapping Your Nervous System | Introduction, psychoeducation, baseline assessment |
| 2 | The Biology of Stress | Sympathetic activation, cortisol, stress response |
| 3 | Grounding Techniques | Practical tools for immediate regulation |
| 4 | Window of Tolerance | Hyperarousal, hypoarousal, personal patterns |
| 5 | Co-Regulation | The power of group connection, attachment repair |
| 6 | Triggers and Activation | Identifying triggers, titration, pendulation |
| 7 | Building Resilience | Resourcing, expanding capacity, self-compassion |

**Session Structure (90 min):**
- 10 min: Check-in and grounding

- 10 min: Closing and resource anchoring

**Outcomes Measured:**
- Pre/post: PCL-5 (PTSD symptoms), GAD-7 (anxiety), weekly nervous system tracking
- Qualitative: Self-reported regulation capacity, group cohesion scale

## Facilitation Approach

### Co-Facilitation Model
Most groups are co-facilitated by Nicole and Kiera, providing:
- Modeling of healthy professional relationship
- Different perspectives and styles
- Coverage if one facilitator is absent
- Reduced burnout for facilitators
- Richer group dynamics

### Trauma-Informed Group Principles
1. **Safety First:** Physical and emotional safety prioritized
2. **Transparency:** Clear structure, expectations, and process
3. **Peer Support:** Fostering connection between members
4. **Collaboration:** Members as partners in healing
5. **Empowerment:** Choice and voice centered
6. **Cultural Humility:** Acknowledging differences, avoiding assumptions
7. **Somatic Integration:** Body-based techniques throughout

### Managing Group Dynamics
- **The Monopolizer:** Gentle redirection, individual check-in after session
- **The Silent One:** Invitation without pressure, written options
- **Conflict:** Mediation using IFS or NVC principles
- **Emotional Flooding:** Grounding techniques, individual support
- **Secondary Trauma:** Processing difficult stories, facilitator self-care

## Outcome Measurement

### Standardized Assessments
- **Intake:** GAD-7, PHQ-9, PCL-5, ECR-R (attachment)
- **Midpoint:** Brief symptom check
- **Completion:** Same assessments + qualitative feedback
- **3-month follow-up:** Sustainability check

### Qualitative Measures
- Weekly mood/nervous system tracking (1-10 scale)
- Session satisfaction (brief 3-question survey)
- Group cohesion assessment (midpoint and end)
- Narrative feedback: "What changed for you?"

### Success Metrics
- 80% completion rate (group therapy standard)
- Average symptom reduction: 30%+
- 90% would recommend to friend
- 60% continue to another group or individual therapy

## Risk Management

### Contraindications for Group Therapy
- Active psychosis or severe dissociation
- Active substance use disorder (first 90 days of recovery)
- Current suicidal ideation with plan/intent
- History of violence or severe aggression in groups
- Inability to maintain confidentiality

**Alternative:** Individual therapy until stabilized, then group

### Crisis Protocol
1. Safety assessment during intake
2. Safety plan developed with each member
3. 24/7 crisis line provided (local resources)
4. Facilitators available by text for crises (with boundaries)
5. Clear scope: group is not crisis intervention

## Sample Session: Nervous System Regulation (Week 3)

**Theme:** Grounding Techniques

**Opening (10 min):**
- Grounding exercise: 5-4-3-2-1 senses technique
- Check-in: One word for how you're arriving today
- Review: "What's one thing you tried this week from our toolbox?"

**Psychoeducation (20 min):**
- The neuroscience of grounding (orienting, mammalian safety cues)
- Why grounding works (vagus nerve, parasympathetic activation)
- When to use grounding vs. other techniques
- Common mistakes: forcing relaxation, spiritual bypassing

**Experiential (30 min):**
- Guided orienting practice (eyes, ears, body)
- Weighted grounding (blankets, sandbags, body weight)
- Cold water vagus stimulation (hand washing ritual)
- Self-press (hand on heart, hand on belly)

**Group Process (20 min):**
- "What did you notice in your body during the exercises?"
- Sharing experiences, normalizing variety of responses
- Problem-solving: "What gets in the way of grounding?"
- Peer support and witnessing

**Closing (10 min):**
- Favorite technique from today identified
- Commitment to practice once before next session
- Grounding anchor established (word, gesture, image)
- Brief closing ritual (collective breath, intention)
