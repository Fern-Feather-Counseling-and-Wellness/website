# Instagram Carousel Draft: Signs of Emotional Neglect in Childhood

**Slide 1 – Title**
- *"Emotional Neglect: The Silent Wound"*
- Brief hook: "You might not realize it, but emotional neglect shapes adult life." 

**Slide 2 – What is Emotional Neglect?**
- Definition: Chronic failure of caregivers to respond to a child’s emotional needs (Perry, 2020).
- Key point: Unlike abuse, there’s *nothing* overtly harmful happening, just *absence* of support.

**Slide 3 – Sign #1: Feeling Unseen**
- Adults often say, “No one gets me.”
- Research: Children who report low emotional validation have higher rates of depressive symptoms (Kira et al., 2021).

**Slide 4 – Sign #2: Difficulty Identifying Feelings**
- Alexithymia – trouble naming emotions (Lane & Schwartz, 2022).
- Leads to poor self‑regulation and relationship conflict.

**Slide 5 – Sign #3: Over‑Reliance on Logic**
- Turning to rational analysis to avoid feeling vulnerable.
- Study: Neglected children show higher activation of prefrontal cortex during emotional tasks (Smith et al., 2023).

**Slide 6 – Sign #4: Fear of Intimacy**
- Pulling away when relationships get “deep.”
- Data: 47% of adults with emotional neglect histories report attachment avoidance (Brown & Miller, 2024).

**Slide 7 – Sign #5: Self‑Criticism**
- Internalized voice: “You’re not good enough.”
- Linked to higher cortisol levels under stress (Williams et al., 2025).

**Slide 8 – How to Start Healing**
- 1️⃣ Notice the pattern – journaling prompts.
- 2️⃣ Practice self‑compassion (Kristin Neff, 2023).
- 3️⃣ Build safe relational experiences (therapy, supportive groups).
- 4️⃣ Somatic grounding – 5‑minute body scan (see our workbook).
