## Slide-by-Slide Content

### Slide 1: Title Slide
**Headline:** The Window of Tolerance
**Subhead:** Your nervous system's comfort zone — explained
**Visual:** Simple diagram of three zones (window in middle)
**Caption Hook:** "Ever feel like your emotions are either too much or not enough? That's your Window of Tolerance talking."

### Slide 2: What Is the Window of Tolerance?
**Headline:** Imagine your nervous system has a "window"
**Body Text:** The Window of Tolerance is the optimal zone where you can:
- Think clearly
- Feel emotions without being overwhelmed
- Respond (not react) to situations
- Access creativity and connection
**Visual:** Person comfortably inside a window frame
**Icon:** 🪟

### Slide 3: Hyperarousal (Above the Window)
**Headline:** Hyperarousal → Fight or Flight
**Symptoms:**
- Racing thoughts
- Panic, anxiety
- Anger, irritability
- Restlessness
- Can't sit still
**Body:** "Your threat detection is stuck ON. Everything feels like danger."
**Visual:** Person above window, looking frantic/activated
**Color:** Red/orange tones

### Slide 4: Hypoarousal (Below the Window)
**Headline:** Hypoarousal → Freeze or Collapse
**Symptoms:**
- Numbness, disconnection
- Exhaustion, shutdown
- Brain fog, can't think
- Feeling "checked out"
- Can't feel emotions
**Body:** "Your system has shut down to protect you. Nothing feels real."
**Visual:** Person below window, looking collapsed/dissociated
**Color:** Blue/gray tones

### Slide 5: Inside the Window
**Headline:** Inside → You can function
**Characteristics:**
- Can think clearly
- Feel emotions without being overwhelmed
- Make thoughtful decisions
- Connect with others
- Handle stress without melting down or shutting down
**Body:** "This is where healing happens — not by avoiding emotions, but by building capacity to feel them."
**Visual:** Person centered in window, looking calm/balanced
**Color:** Green/calm tones

### Slide 6: How to Widen Your Window
**Headline:** 4 Ways to Build Capacity
1. **Regular grounding** — Daily practice, not just crisis
2. **Co-regulation** — Safe relationships rewire your nervous system
3. **Somatic therapy** — Process trauma at body level
4. **Mind-body practices** — Yoga, breathwork, mindfulness
**Visual:** Window widening animation or before/after
**Body:** "A wider window = more resilience, less time spent in dysregulation."

### Slide 7: CTA Slide
**Headline:** Therapy helps widen your window
**Body:** "If you spend more time outside your window than inside, somatic therapy can help. We work with your nervous system to build capacity so you can handle life's stresses without shutting down or melting down."
**Button/CTA:** "Learn about somatic therapy at Fern & Feather"
**Link:** fernfeatherwellness.com/therapy
**Visual:** Fern & Feather logo + calming image

## Caption Copy

### Option 1 (Educational):
"Your nervous system has a 'Window of Tolerance' — the zone where you can function optimally. 🪟

When you're INSIDE the window:
• You can think clearly
• Feel emotions without being overwhelmed  
• Respond instead of react
• Connect with others

When you go ABOVE the window (hyperarousal):
• Fight/flight activated
• Racing thoughts, panic, anger
• Everything feels like danger

When you drop BELOW the window (hypoarousal):
• Freeze/collapse response
• Numbness, disconnection, exhaustion
• Can't feel or think clearly

Trauma, chronic stress, or unmet needs can make your window narrow. You might ping-pong between hyper and hypo without much time in that comfortable middle zone.

The goal isn't to never leave the window — that's impossible. The goal is to:
1. Recognize when you've left it
2. Know how to get back
3. Gradually widen it so you can handle more stress

## Scientific Backing

### Key Concepts:
- **Polyvagal Theory** (Porges): Autonomic nervous system states
- **Window of Tolerance** (Siegel): Optimal arousal zone
- **Trauma & the body** (Van der Kolk): How trauma narrows the window

### Simplicity vs. Accuracy:
- **Keep simple** for social media
- **Note complexity** in comments if needed
- **Avoid over-simplifying** neurobiology
- **Cite sources** if questioned

### Updates:
- **Acknowledge debate** around polyvagal theory (2024-2026 criticisms)
- **Focus on clinical utility** rather than biological certainty
- **Stay current** with research
