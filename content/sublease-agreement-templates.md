# Fern & Feather Sublease Agreement Templates
## Workspace Rental for Aligned Wellness Practitioners

---

## Overview

Fern & Feather's subleasing strategy creates additional revenue while building a community of aligned practitioners. This document includes three agreement templates for different rental arrangements.

**Philosophy:**
- Subtenants must align with Fern & Feather's values (trauma-informed, LGBTQ+ affirming, inclusive)
- Clear boundaries protect both parties
- Professional autonomy within shared space
- Build community, not just income

---

**Entire Agreement:** This constitutes the entire agreement between parties. Amendments must be in writing.

## Implementation Notes

### Legal Review
**⚠️ IMPORTANT:** These templates require review by a Georgia-licensed attorney before use. Consider:
- Local landlord-tenant laws
- Zoning requirements for multi-practitioner wellness spaces
- HIPAA business associate considerations if sharing any client information
- Professional board regulations for shared spaces

### Insurance Consultation
- Consult with insurance broker about Fern & Feather's general liability coverage
- Determine if subtenants need to name Fern & Feather as additional insured
- Consider requiring tenants to carry their own general liability (not just professional)

### Pricing Strategy
**Suggested Starting Rates (Alpharetta market research):**
- Part-time office: $400-600/month (2 days/week)
- Hourly room rental: $35-50/hour (office), $50-75/hour (group room)
- Membership tiers: $200-500/month depending on hours

**Pricing should consider:**
- Mortgage/rent and operating expenses
- Utilities, WiFi, maintenance
- Insurance increases
- Desired profit margin
- Market rates in area

### Screening Process
**Application steps:**
1. Initial inquiry form (online)
2. Credentials verification
3. Values alignment interview
4. Reference checks (2-3 professional)
5. Space tour
6. Trial period (optional: 1 month)
7. Formal agreement

### Community Building
- Monthly email newsletter for subtenants
- Quarterly in-person gatherings
- Shared resource library (books, trainings)
- Referral network development
- Case consultation groups (peer-led or facilitated)
