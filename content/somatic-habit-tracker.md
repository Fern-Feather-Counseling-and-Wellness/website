# Somatic Self-Care Habit Tracker
## Build Nervous System Regulation Into Your Daily Life

**How to Use This Tracker:**

1. **Choose 3-5 habits** to track (suggestions below)
2. **Check off** each day you complete the habit
3. **Rate your overall wellbeing** weekly
4. **Reflect** on patterns at month end
5. **Adjust** habits based on what works

**Why Track Somatic Habits:**

Nervous system regulation isn't about one big intervention — it's about consistent micro-practices. Tracking helps you:
- Build awareness of what actually helps
- Create accountability for self-care
- See progress over time (even when it feels slow)
- Identify which practices fit your life

---

## Habit Suggestions

**Choose 3-5 that resonate with you:**

□ Morning grounding (2 min feet on floor)
□ Physiological sigh (3x daily)
□ Body scan before bed
□ 10-minute walk without phone
□ Cold water splash on face
□ Hand on heart self-soothing
□ Shaking/movement break
□ Orienting (looking around slowly)
□ Box breathing (5 cycles)
□ Journaling about sensations
□ No screens 30 min before bed
□ Eat one meal without distractions

## Somatic Practice Quick Reference

### 2-Minute Practices (Emergency Regulation)
- Physiological sigh (2 sharp inhales, long exhale)
- Hand on heart and belly
- Cold water splash
- Grounding through feet

### 5-Minute Practices (Daily Maintenance)
- Box breathing (5 cycles)
- Orienting (looking around slowly)
- Body scan (head to toe)
- Shaking out limbs

### 10-Minute Practices (Deep Regulation)
- Extended body scan
- Walking meditation
- Gentle stretching/yoga
- Journaling with somatic awareness

---

## Tips for Success

✓ **Start small** — 3 habits is better than 5 you can't maintain

✓ **Anchor to existing routines** — Do your practice right after brushing teeth or before coffee

✓ **Progress, not perfection** — 5/7 days is a win, not a failure

✓ **Notice the difference** — Pay attention to how you feel on days you practice vs. days you don't

✓ **Be flexible** — If a habit isn't working, swap it out

✓ **Track immediately** — Check the box right after you do the practice

✓ **Review weekly** — Don't wait until month end to adjust

---

## Remember

**Consistency beats intensity.** Five minutes of grounding every day is more effective than one hour once a week.

**Your nervous system learns through repetition.** Each time you practice regulation, you're teaching your body a new pattern.

**Be patient with yourself.** Change happens gradually. The tracker helps you see progress you might otherwise miss.

**This is self-care, not another obligation.** If tracking becomes stressful, simplify or take a break.
