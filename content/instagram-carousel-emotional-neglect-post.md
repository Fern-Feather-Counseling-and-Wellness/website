---

**Slide 1 – Title**
"When Love Is Missing: Spotting Emotional Neglect"

---

**Slide 2 – Sign 1**
"Lack of Emotional Validation"
"Growing up, you might have felt your feelings were ‘wrong’ or ‘unimportant.’ In adulthood, this shows up as self‑doubt or difficulty trusting your emotions. (Cicchetti & Toth, 2020)"

---

**Slide 3 – Sign 2**
"Difficulty Identifying Feelings (Alexithymia)"
"Struggling to label emotions can lead to feeling ‘numb.’ It often stems from a childhood where emotions weren’t named or discussed. (Smith et al., 2021)"

---

**Slide 4 – Sign 3**
"People‑Pleasing & Over‑Responsibility"
"When caregivers neglect emotional needs, children learn to prioritize others’ needs over their own. In adults, this appears as chronic people‑pleasing. (Brown & Lee, 2019)"

---

**Slide 5 – Sign 4**
"Isolation & Fear of Intimacy"
"Without early emotional connection, intimacy can feel unsafe. You might withdraw or fear closeness in relationships. (Johnson, 2022)"

---

**Slide 6 – Healing Path**
"Re‑Learning Emotional Safety"
"Therapy can help you recognize these patterns, practice self‑compassion, and build secure emotional bonds. You deserve to feel seen and heard."
