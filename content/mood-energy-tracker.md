# Daily Mood & Energy Tracker
## Build Awareness, Spot Patterns, Support Your Nervous System

**How to Use This Tracker:**

1. **Rate your mood** each day (1-10 scale)
2. **Track your energy** level (1-10 scale)
3. **Note your sleep** quality and duration
4. **Record significant events** or stressors
5. **Identify patterns** over time

**Why This Matters:**

Your mood and energy aren't random — they follow patterns influenced by sleep, stress, hormones, and life events. Tracking helps you:
- Identify triggers and what helps
- Spot cyclical patterns (weekly, monthly)
- Communicate more effectively with your therapist
- Make informed decisions about your health

## Rating Scales

### Mood Scale (1-10)

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Extremely low, hopeless, unable to function |
| 2 | Very low, significant distress |
| 3 | Low, struggling, everything feels hard |
| 4 | Below average, noticeable low mood |
| 5 | Neutral, okay, getting by |
| 6 | Above average, reasonably content |
| 7 | Good, positive, functioning well |
| 8 | Very good, happy, energized |
| 9 | Excellent, joyful, thriving |
| 10 | Peak wellbeing, couldn't be better |

### Energy Scale (1-10)

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Completely depleted, bedridden |
| 2 | Very low energy, minimal activity possible |
| 3 | Low energy, basic tasks only |
| 4 | Below average, tiring easily |
| 5 | Moderate, adequate for daily tasks |
| 6 | Good energy, comfortable activity level |
| 7 | High energy, productive and active |
| 8 | Very high energy, vigorous activity |
| 9 | Excellent energy, feels abundant |
| 10 | Peak energy, limitless feeling |

### Sleep Quality Scale

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Poor | Difficulty falling/staying asleep, not rested |
| Fair | Some sleep issues, somewhat rested |
| Good | Mostly slept well, reasonably rested |
| Excellent | Slept deeply, fully rested |

## For Therapy Sessions

**Bring this tracker to your therapy session to discuss:**

- Patterns your therapist might notice
- Correlations between events and mood/energy
- Sleep's impact on your mental health
- Progress over time
- Adjustments to your treatment plan

**Questions to ask your therapist:**
- "Do you notice any patterns I missed?"
- "Should we focus on sleep hygiene?"
- "How does this compare to clinical depression/anxiety patterns?"
- "What interventions might help my low days?"

---

## Tips for Accurate Tracking

✓ **Track at the same time each day** — evening works best for reflecting on the whole day

✓ **Be honest** — this is for you, not to impress anyone

✓ **Note the context** — a 3/10 mood after a funeral is different than a 3/10 on an average Tuesday

✓ **Look for trends, not single days** — one bad day isn't a pattern

✓ **Track alongside other factors** — exercise, social connection, caffeine, medication changes

✓ **Be patient** — patterns emerge over weeks, not days

---

## Remember

**Mood and energy fluctuate — that's normal.** This tracker isn't about achieving perfect 10s every day. It's about:

- Understanding your unique patterns
- Identifying what helps and what hurts
- Making informed decisions
- Tracking progress over time
- Communicating effectively with your care team

**If you notice consistently low scores (below 4) for more than two weeks, consider reaching out to a mental health professional.**
