## Carousel 3: "Grounding Techniques That Actually Work"

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
- Title: 5 Grounding Techniques
- Subtitle: For when your mind is racing and you can't think straight
- Visual: Calm, grounded imagery
- CTA: Save for your next anxious moment →

**Slide 2:**
- Title: 1. The Physiological Sigh
- Body: "Double inhale through nose, long exhale through mouth. Repeat 3 times."
- Why it works: "Rapidly offloads CO2, slows heart rate, signals safety to brain"
- Visual: Breathing diagram
- Best for: Acute stress, overwhelm, panic moments

**Slide 3:**
- Title: 2. 5-4-3-2-1 Senses
- Body: "Name 5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you feel, 2 you smell, 1 you taste"
- Why it works: "Brings you to present moment, activates prefrontal cortex"
- Visual: Icons for each sense
- Best for: Dissociation, racing thoughts, panic

**Slide 4:**
- Title: 3. Cold Water Reset
- Body: "Splash cold water on your face or hold an ice cube"
- Why it works: "Mammalian dive reflex slows heart rate, activates vagus nerve"
- Visual: Water/ice imagery
- Best for: Intense activation, can't calm down, anger

**Slide 5:**
- Title: 4. Feet on Floor
- Body: "Take off shoes. Feel soles of feet. Press down. Notice support."
- Why it works: "Somatic grounding, proprioceptive input, orients to safety"
- Visual: Feet on ground illustration
- Best for: Floating feeling, disconnection, mild anxiety

**Slide 6:**
- Title: 5. Orienting
- Body: "Soften gaze. Let eyes wander slowly. Name what draws attention."
- Why it works: "Releases freeze response, activates orienting reflex, finds safety"
- Visual: Eyes looking around
- Best for: Freeze, dissociation, feeling stuck

**Slide 7:**
- Title: The Key Insight
- Body: "Different states need different tools. Match the technique to your activation level."
- Visual: Matching diagram

**Slide 8:**
- Title: High Activation → Use:
- List: Physiological sigh, Cold water, Intense movement
- Visual: Red zone tools

**Slide 9:**
- Title: Medium Activation → Use:
- List: 5-4-3-2-1, Feet on floor, Box breathing
- Visual: Yellow zone tools

**Slide 10:**
- Title: Low Activation/Freeze → Use:
- List: Orienting, Temperature change, Gentle movement
- Visual: Blue zone tools

**Slide 11:**
- Title: Practice Before You Need It
- Body: "These work best when your nervous system already knows them. Try them when you're calm first."
- CTA: Which one will you try first? Comment below 👇
- Visual: Practice imagery

**Caption:**
Your anxiety toolkit is missing something. 🧰

You've probably tried:
❌ "Just breathe" (made you more anxious)
❌ "Think positive" (didn't touch the body)
❌ Meditation apps (couldn't focus enough to use them)

Here's the thing: Different nervous system states need different tools.

When you're mildly anxious → 5-4-3-2-1 works great
When you're in panic → You need physiological sigh or cold water
When you're frozen → Orienting and temperature change

The techniques in this carousel are somatic — they work through the body, not the mind. That's why they work when thinking your way out doesn't.

Save this post and try each technique when you're CALM first. Your nervous system learns through repetition. When you really need them, they'll be familiar.

## Carousel 5: "Questions to Ask Before Choosing a Therapist"

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
- Title: 5 Questions to Ask
- Subtitle: Before choosing a trauma-informed therapist
- Visual: Professional but warm imagery
- CTA: Save this for your therapist search →

**Slide 2:**
- Title: 1. "What is your approach to trauma?"
- Red flag: "We won't talk about it" or "Just exposure therapy"
- Green flag: "I use somatic approaches, EMDR, or IFS — we go at your pace"
- Why it matters: Trauma lives in the body, not just the mind

**Slide 3:**
- Title: 2. "How do you handle when I get overwhelmed?"
- Red flag: "Push through it" or "We need to process this now"
- Green flag: "We'll use grounding techniques and titrate the work"
- Why it matters: Good trauma therapy never floods you

**Slide 4:**
- Title: 3. "Do you work with the body or just talk?"
- Red flag: "Trauma is just thoughts" or dismissive of somatic work
- Green flag: "I incorporate body awareness, breathing, or movement"
- Why it matters: Trauma is stored somatically — talk alone often isn't enough

**Slide 5:**
- Title: 4. "How do you measure progress?"
- Red flag: Vague answers or symptom reduction only
- Green flag: "We track your window of tolerance, body awareness, and felt sense of safety"
- Why it matters: You need concrete ways to know you're healing

**Slide 6:**
- Title: 5. "What if we're not a good fit?"
- Red flag: Defensiveness or "You need to commit"
- Green flag: "Therapy is about fit — I'm happy to refer if needed"
- Why it matters: The therapeutic relationship IS the intervention

**Slide 7:**
- Title: Bonus: Trust Your Body
- Body: "Do you feel safer after the consultation? Does your body relax or tense? Your nervous system knows before your mind does."
- Visual: Intuition/gut feeling imagery

**Slide 8:**
- Title: At Fern & Feather...
- Body: "We specialize in somatic, trauma-informed therapy. We go slow. We work with your body, not against it."
- CTA: Link in bio for free consultation
- Visual: Fern & Feather branding

**Caption:**
Choosing a therapist is hard. Here's how to do it right. 🧭

When you've experienced trauma, the therapeutic relationship itself can be triggering. That's why fit matters SO much.

These questions help you find someone who:
✓ Understands trauma is somatic, not just cognitive
✓ Won't flood or retraumatize you
✓ Respects your pace and boundaries
✓ Has actual training in trauma modalities

Red flags to watch for:
🚩 "Just think positive"
🚩 "You need to forgive and move on"
🚩 Pushing you to talk about trauma before you're ready
🚩 Dismissing your body symptoms
🚩 Making you feel bad for having needs

Green flags to seek:
✅ Somatic or body-based training
✅ EMDR, IFS, Somatic Experiencing, or similar certifications
✅ Willingness to go slow
✅ Comfortable with silence and emotion
✅ You feel safer after talking to them

Your nervous system deserves care from someone who truly understands it.

## Design Notes for All Carousels

### Visual Style
- **Colors:** Fern & Feather brand palette (greens, earth tones, warm neutrals)
- **Fonts:** Clean, readable sans-serif for body; elegant serif for headers
- **Imagery:** Diverse representation, warm lighting, nature elements
- **Icons:** Simple line drawings, consistent style

### Slide Dimensions
- Instagram carousel: 1080 × 1350px (4:5 ratio)
- Safe zone for text: Keep within center 80%

### Text Guidelines
- Maximum 125 characters per slide
- Use bullet points for readability
- Bold key phrases
- Include white space

### Engagement Tactics
- End with question to drive comments
- Use "Save this" CTA on cover slide
- Include relevant hashtags (max 30)
- Post at optimal times (check insights)
