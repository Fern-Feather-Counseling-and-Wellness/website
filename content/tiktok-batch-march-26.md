## Fern & Feather TikTok Concepts (Professional, Educational)

### Concept 3: "The 4 F's of Trauma Response (Not Just Fight or Flight)"
**Hook:** "Trauma responses aren't just fight or flight. Here are all 4 F's..."
**Style:** Clean animated diagram with clear labels
**Timing:** 30 seconds
**Visual Concept:**
- Animation of four branches:
  1. **Fight** (aggression, anger)
  2. **Flight** (avoidance, running away)
  3. **Freeze** (shutdown, dissociation)
  4. **Fawn** (people-pleasing, compliance)
- Each F has simple icon and description
- Text: "Your body knows what it needs to survive"
- Text: "No response is 'wrong' — it's adaptive"
- Text: "Therapy helps you move from survival to thriving"
**Audio:** Clear, educational synth background
**Hashtags:** #TraumaResponse #TraumaRecovery #PolyvagalTheory #NervousSystem #FernAndFeather
**Caption:** The 4 F's of trauma response: Fight, Flight, Freeze, and Fawn. Your body chooses the response that kept you safest in past threatening situations. Healing isn't about eliminating these responses — it's about expanding your capacity to choose. 🕊️ #TraumaInformed #SomaticTherapy

### Concept 4: "What's the Difference Between Anxiety and Trauma?"
**Hook:** "Anxiety and trauma overlap, but they're not the same thing. Here's how to tell..."
**Style:** Side-by-side comparison with clear distinctions
**Timing:** 45 seconds
**Visual Concept:**
- Two columns: **Anxiety** (left) vs. **Trauma** (right)
- **Anxiety column:** "Future-focused worry", "Generalized fear", "What-if thinking"
- **Trauma column:** "Past-event response", "Specific triggers", "Body-based memories"
- Animation showing overlap in middle (panic attacks, avoidance)
- Text: "Anxiety: 'What might happen'"
- Text: "Trauma: 'What already happened'"
- Text: "Both need care, but different approaches"
**Audio:** Clear, explanatory background music
**Hashtags:** #AnxietyVsTrauma #TraumaTherapy #AnxietyHelp #MentalHealthEducation #FernAndFeather
**Caption:** Anxiety is often about future threats ("what might happen"), while trauma is about past events ("what already happened"). Both create distress, but they respond to different therapeutic approaches. Understanding which you're dealing with is the first step toward effective healing. 🧭 #TraumaInformedCare #AnxietySupport
