## What Is Couples Therapy (And What It Isn't)

**Couples therapy is:** A structured, evidence-based process where a trained therapist helps partners improve their relationship through communication skills, conflict resolution, emotional connection, and understanding each other's needs.

**Couples therapy isn't:** A referee who decides who's right or wrong. A magic fix that works without effort from both partners. A last-ditch effort before divorce (though it can help there too).

### Common Myths About Couples Therapy

**Myth:** "We should be able to fix this ourselves."
**Reality:** Just as you'd see a doctor for physical health or a trainer for fitness, relationship experts have specialized training you don't. They provide tools and perspectives you can't access on your own.

**Myth:** "Therapy is only for relationships on the brink of collapse."
**Reality:** Many couples come to therapy proactively — to strengthen communication before problems become crises, to navigate major transitions (marriage, parenthood, empty nesting), or to deepen an already good connection.

**Myth:** "The therapist will take sides."
**Reality:** A good couples therapist remains neutral, focusing on the relationship system rather than individual "fault." The goal isn't to prove who's right but to understand how the relationship dynamic works and how to improve it.

## Evidence-Based Approaches to Couples Therapy

At Fern & Feather, our therapists are trained in several research-backed approaches:

### Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)
**What it is:** EFT helps couples identify and change negative interaction patterns by accessing and expressing underlying emotions. Based on attachment theory, it views relationship distress as a disruption in the emotional bond.

**How it works:** The therapist helps partners express vulnerable emotions (fear, loneliness, need) rather than surface-level anger or criticism, creating new patterns of connection and responsiveness.

**Research:** EFT has demonstrated strong outcomes in clinical trials, with studies showing significant improvement in relationship satisfaction and attachment security (Johnson, 2019; Wiebe et al., 2020).

### The Gottman Method
**What it is:** Developed by Drs. John and Julie Gottman after 40+ years of research on what makes relationships succeed or fail.

**How it works:** Uses assessment tools to identify strengths and areas for growth, then teaches practical skills for managing conflict, building friendship, and creating shared meaning.

**Key concepts:** Love maps, fondness and admiration, turning toward bids for connection, positive sentiment override, the Four Horsemen (criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling) (Gottman, 1999; Gottman & Silver, 2015).

### Integrative Behavioral Couples Therapy (IBCT)
**What it is:** Combines traditional behavioral approaches with acceptance strategies.

**How it works:** Helps partners change what they can (behavior) while accepting what they can't (certain personality traits, past experiences). Focuses on emotional acceptance as a path to change.

### Narrative Couples Therapy
**What it is:** Views problems as separate from the couple — externalizing the issue so partners can unite against it rather than against each other.

**How it works:** The therapist helps the couple rewrite their relationship story, highlighting strengths and resilience they may have overlooked.

## What to Expect in Your First Session

### Before You Arrive
Most therapists (including Fern & Feather) will send intake forms electronically. These typically ask about:
- Basic information about each partner
- Relationship history
- Current concerns and goals
- Individual mental health history
- What you hope to achieve in therapy

### The Initial Assessment (1-3 Sessions)
The first few sessions are usually assessment-focused:

**Individual sessions:** Many therapists meet with each partner individually (1 session each) to hear their perspective privately and assess for safety concerns.

**Joint session:** The therapist observes how you interact, asks about your relationship history, and begins to identify patterns.

**Feedback session:** The therapist shares their assessment, proposes a treatment plan, and collaborates with you on goals.

### Ongoing Sessions (Typically Weekly or Biweekly)
Sessions usually last 50-90 minutes and follow a general structure:
1. **Check-in:** How has the week been? Any significant events or conflicts?
2. **Agenda setting:** What do you want to focus on today?
3. **Processing:** Exploring an issue with therapist guidance
4. **Skill building:** Learning and practicing new communication or conflict resolution tools
5. **Homework:** Practicing skills between sessions
6. **Wrap-up:** Summarizing insights and planning for the week ahead

## How to Know If You Need Couples Therapy

Consider therapy if you notice:
- **Communication patterns:** More criticism than appreciation, frequent misunderstandings, avoiding difficult topics
- **Conflict style:** Arguments that escalate quickly, no resolution, same issues recurring
- **Emotional distance:** Feeling lonely in the relationship, lack of intimacy, living parallel lives
- **Trust issues:** Betrayal, secrecy, or constant suspicion
- **Life transitions:** Struggling to adjust to major changes together
- **Proactive strengthening:** Wanting to build skills before problems arise

**Important:** If there's active abuse (physical, emotional, sexual), individual therapy is recommended first, with couples therapy only when safety is established and both partners are committed to change.

## FAQs About Couples Therapy in Alpharetta

### How long does couples therapy take?
Most couples see meaningful improvement in 12-20 sessions (3-5 months). Some continue longer for deeper work or maintenance.

### How much does it cost?
At Fern & Feather, couples therapy sessions are $180-220 per 50-minute session. We're a private-pay practice (no insurance) to protect your privacy and allow us to focus on your needs rather than insurance requirements.

### Do both partners need to want therapy?
It's ideal if both are willing, but one partner can start individually to work on their part of the relationship dynamic. Sometimes this motivates the other partner to join.

### What if my partner refuses to come?
Consider individual therapy to work on your responses and communication. Sometimes changes in one person can shift the entire relationship dynamic.

### Is everything we say confidential?
Yes, with standard limits (harm to self/others, child/elder abuse). We don't share information with each other from individual sessions without explicit permission.

### Can we do online sessions?
Yes, we offer secure telehealth options for couples throughout Georgia.

### What if we decide to separate during therapy?
A good therapist can help you navigate separation or divorce with more respect and less damage, especially if children are involved.
