### Slide 2: Technique 1 — 5-4-3-2-1 Sensory Grounding
**Visual:** Clean graphic showing numbers 5-4-3-2-1 with icons
**Text:**
**Technique 1: Sensory Inventory**
Engage your 5 senses to anchor in the present:

5️⃣ Look: Name 5 things you can see
4️⃣ Touch: Name 4 things you can feel
3️⃣ Listen: Name 3 things you can hear  
2️⃣ Smell: Name 2 things you can smell
1️⃣ Taste: Name 1 thing you can taste

**Why it works:** Redirects attention from anxious thoughts to sensory input, engaging the prefrontal cortex.

---

### Slide 3: Technique 2 — Temperature Shift
**Visual:** Ice cube or cold compress graphic
**Text:**
**Technique 2: Temperature Shift**
Use temperature to shock your system back to the present:

🧊 Hold an ice cube in your hand
🧊 Splash cold water on your face
🧊 Place a cold compress on your neck

**Science says:** Cold exposure activates the mammalian diving reflex, slowing heart rate and shifting you from panic to calm.

**Safety note:** Don't use ice directly on skin for more than 30 seconds.

---

### Slide 4: Technique 3 — Orienting
**Visual:** Person looking around room graphic
**Text:**
**Technique 3: Orienting**
Scan your environment like your nervous system was designed to:

👀 Slowly look around the room
🔍 Name 3 safe things you see
🎯 Identify exits, windows, doors
🧠 Mentally map your space

**Why it works:** Anxiety often comes from a "stuck" threat detection system. Orienting tells your brain "I'm safe here" and completes the scanning reflex.

---

### Slide 5: Technique 4 — Grounding Through Feet
**Visual:** Feet on ground graphic
**Text:**
**Technique 4: Plant Your Feet**
Connect with the literal ground beneath you:

👣 Feel your feet on the floor
⚖️ Shift weight side to side
🦶 Press down through heels and balls of feet
🌱 Imagine roots growing into the earth

**Pro tip:** Do this barefoot on grass or carpet for stronger effect. The pressure sensation sends "I'm grounded" signals up your vagus nerve.

---

### Slide 6: Technique 5 — Breathing With Movement
**Visual:** Breath + movement diagram
**Text:**
**Technique 5: Breath + Movement Combo**
Don't just breathe — breathe with intention:

🌀 **Box Breathing:** 4 sec in → 4 sec hold → 4 sec out → 4 sec hold
🔄 **With movement:** Add gentle shoulder rolls or hand presses

**Key insight:** Anxious breathing is shallow and fast. Controlled breathing with movement regulates both respiratory and muscular systems.

## Educational Notes for Comments

**Common questions to anticipate:**
- "What if grounding doesn't work for me?"
  *Response:* "Grounding works best when practiced regularly, not just in crisis. Try making one technique a daily habit, then notice if it's easier to access when anxious."

- "Is this different from mindfulness?"
  *Response:* "Yes! Mindfulness is awareness of thoughts/feelings. Grounding is specifically about connecting with your body/environment to regulate your nervous system. They complement each other."

- "How long should I practice these?"
  *Response:* "Start with 1-2 minutes daily. The goal isn't perfection — it's building a reliable toolkit your body remembers when stressed."
