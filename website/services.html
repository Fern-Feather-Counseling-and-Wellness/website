What to Expect

🧠 Individual Therapy

One-on-one support tailored to your unique needs and goals.

⏱️ 45 minutes
📍 In-person or telehealth
💰 $135–$180/session

Who it's for: Adults dealing with anxiety, depression, trauma, life transitions, relationship issues, or anyone seeking personal growth and deeper self-understanding.

What we address:

  • Anxiety and panic disorders
  • Trauma and PTSD
  • Depression and mood issues
  • Life transitions (career, relationships, identity)
  • Grief and loss
  • Self-esteem and identity
  • Stress and burnout
  • Perfectionism and people-pleasing

Our approach: We draw from multiple evidence-based modalities including Internal Family Systems (IFS), somatic therapy, EMDR, CBT, and attachment-based therapy. We tailor our approach to what works best for you.

How long will I need therapy?

It varies. Some people find significant relief in 8-12 sessions. Others prefer ongoing support for deeper work. We'll check in regularly about your progress and adjust as needed.

💕 Couples Therapy

Strengthen your relationship through better communication and deeper connection.

⏱️ 50–80 minutes
📍 In-person or telehealth
💰 $210–$235/session

Who it's for: Couples at any stage — dating, engaged, married, separating, or anything in between. All relationship structures welcome (monogamous, polyamorous, LGBTQ+, etc.).

What we address:

  • Communication breakdowns
  • Conflict resolution
  • Trust and betrayal
  • Intimacy and connection
  • Life transitions (moving, kids, career changes)
  • Navigating different needs and desires
  • Pre-marital preparation
  • Conscious uncoupling (if separation is the path)

Our approach: We use Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) and Gottman Method principles to help you understand your attachment patterns, communicate effectively, and rebuild connection.

Do we both need to want therapy?

Ideally, yes. But if one partner is hesitant, we can start with the willing partner and work toward joint sessions when ready.

Why We Don't Take Insurance

And why that's actually better for you.

We're a private-pay practice. No insurance billing, no third-party interference, no compromises. Here's what that means — and why we made this choice.

The Insurance Reality Check

Insurance companies exist to make money, not to support your growth. When insurance gets involved, here's what actually happens:

  • ✓ You get a permanent diagnosis. Insurance requires a mental health disorder code to pay for anything. That label follows you forever — even if it's just situational stress or you're working on personal growth.
  • ✓ They decide what "counts." Prevention, relationship work, self-understanding? Not "medically necessary." Insurance only covers crisis management, not actual thriving.
  • ✓ They limit your care. Some bureaucrat who's never met you decides how many sessions you "need" and what you're allowed to work on. Spoiler: their spreadsheet doesn't know your life.
  • ✓ Your privacy disappears. Insurance records create a permanent trail of your mental health history. Future employers, insurance companies, even government agencies can potentially access this.

Here's What You Get With Us

  • ✓ True privacy. Your records stay between us. No diagnosis required unless medically necessary. No insurance company reviewing your notes.
  • ✓ Care that fits you. We decide together what's best — not some insurer looking at their bottom line. Work on whatever you actually need to work on.
  • ✓ Quality over quantity. Insurance-based practices often require 25-30+ clients just to keep the lights on. We're mindful about our caseload so you get our full attention.

Superbills (Out-of-Network Reimbursement)

If you have out-of-network benefits, we can provide a superbill — a detailed receipt with diagnostic codes — that you submit to your insurance for partial reimbursement.

Important: Submitting a superbill means your insurance will have your diagnosis and treatment information on file. You're trading some privacy for potential reimbursement. Entirely your call — we'll provide superbills if you want them, and we won't judge either way.
