My Story

I've always been the person my friends come to when they need to talk through relationship stuff. Breakups, makeups, family drama, friendship conflicts — I've heard it all, and I've always been genuinely curious about what makes relationships work (and what makes them fall apart).

Becoming a therapist felt like making it official. I get to do what I've always done — listen deeply, ask the right questions, help people see patterns they can't see on their own — but now I have the training and tools to actually help create change.

I believe relationships are at the center of everything. The quality of our connections determines the quality of our lives. Whether you're struggling with your partner, your family, your friends, or yourself, I'm here to help you build relationships that feel safe, supportive, and authentic.

"The quality of our relationships determines the quality of our lives. My goal is to help you build connections that are authentic, supportive, and nourishing — starting with the relationship you have with yourself."

— Kiera

How I Work

Warm, direct, and focused on creating real change in your relationships.

💕 The Relationship Focus

I specialize in evidence-based approaches for relationships:

  • ✓ Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) — Creating secure attachment
  • ✓ Gottman Method — Research-based couples interventions
  • ✓ Attachment-Based Therapy — Understanding your patterns
  • ✓ Discernment Counseling — For couples on the brink
  • ✓ Trauma-Informed Care — Because the past affects the present

🤝 The Human Connection

I'm not a detached, clinical therapist. I bring:

  • ✓ Directness — I'll tell you what I'm seeing, with compassion
  • ✓ Humor — Therapy can be heavy; we need lightness too
  • ✓ Validation — Your feelings make sense, even when they're hard
  • ✓ Challenge — Growth happens when we stretch
  • ✓ Celebration — Progress deserves recognition

My Passion: Group Therapy

There's something magical about healing in community.

While I love working with couples and individuals, group therapy holds a special place in my heart. There's something incredibly powerful about realizing you're not alone — that other people are struggling with the same things you are.

In my groups, you'll find: A safe space to share, skills you can use immediately, feedback from people who get it, and connections that often last beyond the group.

Current & Upcoming Groups

  • Anxiety Support Group — Learn coping skills while connecting with others who understand
  • Relationship Skills Group — Practice communication and boundary-setting in a supportive environment
  • New Parents Group — Navigate the transition to parenthood with others going through the same thing
