## What to Expect in Therapy

### First Session (Intake)
- Review paperwork and policies
- Discuss what brings you to therapy
- Learn about your history, strengths, challenges
- Begin building therapeutic relationship
- Collaborate on initial goals

### Ongoing Sessions
- 50-minute meetings (typically weekly or biweekly)
- Check-in about past week
- Explore topics you bring
- Practice new skills or perspectives
- Process emotions and experiences
- Adjust approach as needed

### Common Therapy Experiences
- **Feeling worse before better** — digging into hard stuff can be temporarily destabilizing
- **Resistance** — parts of you may resist change (this is normal and welcome)
- **Breakthroughs** — moments of clarity or emotional release
- **Plateaus** — periods where progress feels slow
- **Termination** — planned ending when goals are met

**Therapy is not:** Advice-giving, friendship, crisis-only support, or a quick fix.

**Therapy is:** A collaborative process of self-discovery, skill-building, and healing.

## Confidentiality & Privacy

### What Is Confidential
- Everything you share in session (with exceptions below)
- Your records and progress notes
- Your contact information
- Payment information

### Legal Exceptions to Confidentiality
By law, I must break confidentiality if:
1. **You pose imminent danger to yourself or others** — I will take steps to ensure safety
2. **There is suspected abuse/neglect of a child, elderly person, or dependent adult** — I must report to authorities
3. **A court orders disclosure** — I will attempt to limit what is shared
4. **You are incapacitated and need medical care** — I will contact emergency services

### Other Considerations
- **Consultation:** I may discuss your case anonymously with clinical supervisors/peers
- **Electronic records:** Secured with encryption and password protection
- **Communication:** Email/text are not fully secure; use for scheduling only

**You have the right to:** Access your records, request corrections, and receive a copy of this policy.

## Getting the Most from Therapy

### Before Session
- Notice what's coming up for you
- Jot down topics you want to discuss
- Arrive 5-10 minutes early to settle in

### During Session
- Be honest (even when it's hard)
- Notice body sensations
- Ask questions if confused
- Give feedback about what's helpful/unhelpful

### After Session
- Allow time to process
- Notice any reactions (emotional, physical)
- Practice skills discussed
- Journal if helpful

### Common Pitfalls & Solutions
| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| "I don't know what to talk about" | Keep a running list between sessions |
| "I'm not making progress" | Discuss with me — we can adjust approach |
| "I'm afraid to tell you something" | Start small — we'll build trust over time |
| "I feel worse after session" | Normal — discuss how to manage integration |
| "I want to quit" | Discuss this feeling — may be important work |
