# Therapy Session Note Template
## Professional Documentation System for Fern & Feather

**Created:** March 20, 2026  
**For:** Nicole's clinical practice & Fern & Feather therapists  
**Purpose:** Standardized, compliant session documentation that supports clinical work, insurance requirements, and treatment outcomes tracking

---

## Core Principles

**This template ensures:**
- **Clinical integrity** — Notes reflect actual therapeutic work
- **Compliance** — Meets insurance, legal, and ethical standards
- **Efficiency** — Structured for quick completion (5-7 minutes max)
- **Continuity** — Clear treatment progression across sessions
- **Protection** — Risk management through thorough documentation

**Ethical/Legal Foundation:**
- HIPAA compliant
- Meets state board requirements (Georgia Composite Board)
- Aligns with ACA/APA ethical codes
- Supports insurance audit defense

---

## Template Sections

### 1. Session Header (Objective Data)

```
Client: [Full Name]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Session #: [X of planned Y]
Time: [Start - End] (Duration: 50 minutes)
Modality: [Individual/Couples/Group/Family]
Format: [In-person/Virtual/Phone]
Present: [Client only / Client + Partner / etc.]
```

### 2. Subjective Report (Client's Words/Experience)

**Prompt:** *What did the client report since last session?*
- Mood/affect: [describe]
- Life events/changes: [brief]
- Progress on homework/goals: [specific]
- Current stressors: [list]
- Physical health/symptoms: [note if relevant]
- Medication/substance use: [if applicable]
- Safety assessment: [SI/HI/self-harm, risk factors]

**Example language:**
- "Client reported increased anxiety this week following conflict with partner."
- "Noted improved sleep after implementing bedtime routine."
- "Denied SI/HI, contracts for safety, has support system in place."

### 3. Objective Observations (Therapist's Notes)

**Prompt:** *What did I observe during the session?*
- Appearance: [grooming, dress, posture]
- Affect/mood: [congruent with content? range?]
- Engagement: [collaborative, resistant, detached]
- Speech: [rate, volume, coherence]
- Thought process: [linear, tangential, organized]
- Insight/judgment: [level demonstrated]
- Therapeutic alliance: [strength, rapport, transference/countertransference noted]

**Example language:**
- "Client presented with congruent affect, made eye contact, engaged collaboratively."
- "Noted psychomotor agitation when discussing workplace stress."
- "Demonstrated increased insight compared to previous sessions."

### 4. Assessment & Analysis (Clinical Thinking)

**Prompt:** *What's my clinical understanding of this session?*
- Diagnosis consideration: [link symptoms to DSM-5/ICD-10 criteria]
- Progress toward treatment goals: [specific, measurable]
- Clinical formulation: [why is client presenting this way now?]
- Risk assessment: [current level, changes, protective factors]
- Strengths/resources: [client's internal/external supports]

**Example language:**
- "Anxiety symptoms align with GAD criteria; panic features noted but not meeting full criteria."
- "Making progress on goal #2 (emotional regulation) as evidenced by using grounding techniques independently."
- "Formulation: Childhood attachment patterns manifesting in current relationship dynamics."

### 5. Intervention & Process (What We Did)

**Prompt:** *What therapeutic work occurred?*
- Interventions used: [CBT, IFS, somatic, psychodynamic, etc.]
- Skills practiced: [specific techniques]
- Process/themes explored: [what emerged in session]
- Psychoeducation provided: [topics covered]
- Homework assigned: [specific tasks]

**Example language:**
- "Practiced 4-7-8 breathing for anxiety management."
- "Explored internal parts work using IFS framework — identified 'manager' part."
- "Assigned: Daily gratitude journaling and one body scan practice."

### 6. Plan & Recommendations (Next Steps)

**Prompt:** *What's the plan moving forward?*
- Next session focus: [planned topics/interventions]
- Treatment modifications: [adjustments to approach]
- Referrals/collaboration: [other providers involved]
- Follow-up tasks: [for therapist]
- Client homework: [specific, time-bound]

**Example language:**
- "Continue IFS parts work with focus on exiled parts."
- "Refer to psychiatrist for medication evaluation (client agreed)."
- "Therapist to research local support groups for social anxiety."

### 7. Outcome Measures (If Used)

**Prompt:** *Quantitative progress tracking*
- PHQ-9/GAD-7 scores: [current vs. previous]
- ORS/SRS: [outcome and session ratings]
- Custom measures: [track specific symptoms/behaviors]

**Example language:**
- "GAD-7: 18 (severe) → 14 (moderate) over 4 sessions."
- "ORS: 22/40, SRS: 36/40 — alliance strong, outcomes improving."

### 8. Special Considerations

**Prompt:** *Anything exceptional?*
- Crisis intervention: [if applicable]
- Ethical dilemmas: [noted and addressed]
- Consultation/supervision: [sought or needed]
- Documentation of unusual events: [late arrivals, payments, boundary issues]

**Example language:**
- "Crisis protocol initiated when client expressed SI with plan; safety planning completed."
- "Consulted with supervisor regarding complex transference dynamics."

## Template Variations by Modality

### Individual Therapy (Standard)
Use full template above.

### Couples Therapy
- Add: "Each partner's perspective" section
- Include: "Interaction patterns observed"
- Note: "Alliance with each partner"
- Track: "Relationship satisfaction measures"

### Group Therapy
- Add: "Group dynamics" section
- Include: "Member interactions/contributions"
- Note: "Cohesion level, norms, conflicts"
- Track: "Individual progress within group context"

### Telehealth Sessions
- Add: "Technology/Environment" section
- Include: "Connection quality, privacy confirmed"
- Note: "Unique telehealth considerations"

## Time-Saving Tips

**5-Minute Note System:**
1. **During session:** Jot key phrases in template sections
2. **Immediately after:** Fill gaps (5 minutes max)
3. **Review:** Quick proofread for completeness
4. **File:** Secure storage per HIPAA

**Abbreviations/Shortcuts:**
- SI = suicidal ideation
- HI = homicidal ideation  
- GAD = generalized anxiety disorder
- IFS = Internal Family Systems
- CBT = Cognitive Behavioral Therapy
- PHQ-9 = Patient Health Questionnaire-9
- ORS/SRS = Outcome Rating Scale/Session Rating Scale

**Auto-text/Phrases:**
Create common clinical descriptions as templates:
- "Client engaged collaboratively in session..."
- "Denied SI/HI, contracts for safety..."
- "Homework completed/not completed due to..."

## Sample Completed Note

**Client:** Jane Doe
**Date:** 2026-03-20
**Session #:** 6 of 12 (planned)
**Time:** 2:00-2:50 PM (Duration: 50 minutes)
**Modality:** Individual
**Format:** Virtual
**Present:** Client only

**Subjective:** Client reported "better week" with fewer panic attacks (1 vs. 4 previous week). Implemented morning grounding routine 4/7 days. Work stress decreased after setting boundaries with supervisor. Denied SI/HI, no self-harm urges. Sleeping 6-7 hours nightly.

**Objective:** Appeared relaxed, smiling, good eye contact. Speech normal rate/volume. Affect bright, congruent with content. Engaged actively in session. Demonstrated good insight into anxiety patterns.

**Assessment:** GAD symptoms improving (GAD-7: 16 → 12). Making progress on treatment goals #1 (reduce panic frequency) and #3 (improve work functioning). Protective factors strong: social support, coping skills use, medication adherence.

**Interventions:** Reviewed panic log, identified triggers (perfectionism, conflict avoidance). Practiced "5-4-3-2-1" grounding technique. Psychoeducation on anxiety cycle. Assigned: Continue panic log, practice grounding daily, schedule "worry time."

**Plan:** Next session focus on underlying beliefs driving perfectionism. Consider IFS parts work if client ready. Continue current interventions.

**Outcome Measures:** GAD-7: 12 (moderate), down 4 points. ORS: 28/40 (improving), SRS: 38/40 (strong alliance).

**Special Considerations:** None.

**Therapist:** Nicole, APC  
**Signature:** [Electronic signature]  
**Date Note Finalized:** 2026-03-20

## Ethical/Legal Protection

**What this template protects against:**
- **Malpractice claims** — Thorough documentation defends clinical decisions
- **Insurance audits** — Clear medical necessity and treatment linkage
- **Board complaints** — Demonstrates standard of care
- **Subpoenas/court** — Provides complete clinical record

**Risk management tips:**
- Never alter notes after the fact (addendums only with original date/time)
- Document consultations/supervision
- Keep notes objective, avoid judgmental language
- Regular backup/security for electronic records
