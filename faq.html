Frequently Asked Questions

Rooted and Rising

We understand that starting therapy is a big step. Here are answers to some common questions to help you feel more comfortable.

🌿 Getting Started

What happens in the first session?

Your first session is an opportunity for us to get to know each other. We'll discuss what brings you to therapy, your history, and your goals. It's also a chance for you to see if we're a good fit—therapy works best when there's mutual trust and connection.

How long are therapy sessions?

Individual sessions are typically 45 minutes. Couples sessions range from 50-80 minutes depending on your needs. Group therapy sessions are usually 60-90 minutes.

How often will we meet?

Most clients start with weekly sessions to build momentum and establish a therapeutic relationship. As progress is made, we may move to biweekly or monthly sessions.

🍃 Our Approach

What is trauma-informed therapy?

Trauma-informed care recognizes that trauma affects the whole person—body, mind, and spirit. We create a safe environment, understand trauma's impact, and use approaches that help you process and heal without retraumatization.

What does LGBTQ+ affirming mean?

We celebrate and validate all gender identities and sexual orientations. We understand the unique challenges LGBTQ+ individuals face and provide culturally competent care that respects your identity without judgment or assumption.

Do you offer telehealth?

Yes! We offer secure video sessions for clients throughout Georgia. Telehealth provides flexibility and accessibility while maintaining the same quality of care as in-person sessions.

🌱 Practical Matters

Do you accept insurance?

We are a concierge practice and do not bill insurance directly. We provide superbills for you to submit for potential reimbursement. This allows us to provide the highest quality care without insurance company restrictions.

What is your cancellation policy?

We require 24-hour notice for cancellations. Late cancellations or no-shows may be charged the full session fee, as that time has been reserved specifically for you.

How do I know if therapy is working?

Progress looks different for everyone, but common signs include: increased self-awareness, better coping skills, improved relationships, reduced symptoms, and a greater sense of agency in your life. We'll regularly check in about your goals and progress.

🕊️ Specialized Care

What is somatic therapy?

Somatic approaches recognize that trauma and stress are stored in the body. We use techniques like grounding, breathwork, and body awareness to help you release tension and regulate your nervous system.

What is IFS (Internal Family Systems)?

IFS is an evidence-based approach that views the mind as made up of different "parts" with different roles. We work to understand these parts with curiosity and compassion, helping them work together harmoniously.

Do you work with neurodivergent clients?

Absolutely. We have specialized training in working with ADHD, autism, and other neurodivergent conditions. We take a strengths-based approach that honors neurodiversity rather than pathologizing it.
