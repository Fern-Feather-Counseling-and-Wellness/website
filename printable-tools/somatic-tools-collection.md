## 1. Daily Nervous System Tracker

### Purpose
Help clients track their nervous system states throughout the day, identify patterns, and build self-awareness.

### Design Specifications
- **Size:** Letter (8.5" × 11") or A4
- **Layout:** Portrait
- **Pages:** 1 (printable daily)

### Content

**Header:**
- Date field
- Sleep quality scale (1-5)
- Energy level scale (1-5)
- Morning check-in (Ventral/Sympathetic/Dorsal)

**Hourly Check-ins:**
- Time blocks (6-9am, 9-12pm, 12-3pm, 3-6pm, 6-9pm, 9pm+)
- State tracking (V/S/D)
- Trigger/context notes
- Regulation strategy used

**Evening Reflection:**
- What shifted my state today?
- What regulation strategy worked best?
- One thing I noticed about my nervous system
- Tomorrow I want to try

### Usage Notes for Clients
- Print 7 copies for a week of tracking
- Keep by your bed or workspace for easy access
- No judgment—this is data, not a report card
- Look for patterns after 1-2 weeks

## 2. Window of Tolerance Worksheet

### Purpose
Help clients understand their unique window of tolerance and recognize when they're outside of it.

### Design Specifications
- **Size:** Letter (8.5" × 11") or A4
- **Layout:** Landscape (for diagram)
- **Pages:** 2

### Content Page 1: The Diagram

**Visual:** Stacked boxes showing:
- Top: HYPERAROUSAL (Sympathetic) — Racing thoughts, fast heartbeat, irritability, can't sit still, shallow breathing, panic
- Middle: WINDOW OF TOLERANCE — Here you can think clearly, connect with others, handle stress, make decisions
- Bottom: HYPOROUSAL (Dorsal Vagal) — Disconnected, foggy, low energy, can't feel

**Fill-in sections:**
- Your signs (hyperarousal)
- Your cues you're in window
- Your signs (hypoarousal)

### Content Page 2: Regulation Strategies

**Sections:**
- WHEN I'M HYPERAROUSED (too activated) — 4 blank lines for strategies
- WHEN I'M HYPOROUSED (too shut down) — 4 blank lines for strategies
- TO EXPAND MY WINDOW (long-term practices) — 3 blank lines
- EARLY WARNING SIGNS — 3 blank lines
- PEOPLE WHO HELP ME REGULATE — 2 blank lines
- SAFE PLACES THAT HELP ME REGULATE — 2 blank lines

### Usage Notes
- Fill out with therapist or on your own
- Keep visible (fridge, desk, phone wallpaper)
- Update as you learn more about your patterns
- Share with trusted people so they can support you

## 3. Grounding Technique Cards

### Purpose
Pocket-sized cards with quick somatic grounding techniques for moments of dysregulation.

### Design Specifications
- **Size:** 3" × 5" cards (index card size)
- **Layout:** One technique per card, double-sided
- **Quantity:** 12 cards in set
- **Finish:** Recommend printing on cardstock, laminating optional

### Card Set Contents

**CARD 1: 5-4-3-2-1 Senses Grounding**
- Front: Technique name + 5 senses list + Best for (anxiety spikes, dissociation, panic)
- Back: Step-by-step instructions

**CARD 2: Hand on Heart**
- Front: Hand on heart + hand on belly + Best for (self-soothing, shame spirals, loneliness)
- Back: How to use + optional affirmation

**CARD 3: Cold Water Reset**
- Front: Splash cold water or hold ice cube + Best for (overwhelming emotions, racing thoughts, activation spike)
- Back: Instructions + why it works (mammalian dive reflex)

**CARD 4: Orienting**
- Front: Let eyes wander slowly + Best for (freeze state, dissociation, feeling stuck)
- Back: Step-by-step with curiosity emphasis

**CARD 5: Feet on Floor**
- Front: Feel feet press into ground + Best for (anxiety, floating feeling, before difficult conversations)
- Back: Instructions + grounding affirmation

**CARD 6: Butterfly Hug**
- Front: Cross arms, tap shoulders alternating + Best for (distress, flashbacks, self-comfort)
- Back: Instructions + note that this is self-havening

**CARD 7: Name It to Tame It**
- Front: Name the emotion + say it out loud + Best for (emotional floods, overwhelm, reactivity)
- Back: Instructions + examples

**CARD 8: Box Breathing**
- Front: Inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4 + Best for (pre-meeting jitters, sleep prep, focus)
- Back: Visual box diagram + modification note

**CARD 9: 5-Minute Body Scan**
- Front: Notice body parts from feet to head + Best for (daily practice, coming back to body, morning routine)
- Back: Body part sequence + inquiry questions

**CARD 10: Temperature Change**
- Front: Hold something warm OR cold + Best for (dissociation, numbness, grounding)
- Back: Warm options list + cold options list

**CARD 11: Self-Havening**
- Front: Rub arms or face while thinking positive + Best for (distressing memories, anxiety, building neural pathways)
- Back: Instructions + explanation of why it works

**CARD 12: When to Use These**
- Front: Use ANY card when: overwhelmed, can't think clearly, trauma response, need to reset, before bed, after conflict
- Back: Reminder to practice regularly + Fern & Feather branding

## 4. Habit Tracker for Somatic Practices

### Purpose
Help clients build consistent somatic regulation habits through visual tracking.

### Design Specifications
- **Size:** Letter (8.5" × 11") or A4
- **Layout:** Portrait
- **Pages:** 1 (print 4 for a month)

### Content

**Header:**
- Month field
- Focus practice field
- Practice description
- Why this matters to me

**Daily Practices Grid:**
- Columns: Week 1, Week 2, Week 3, Week 4
- Rows: Morning check-in, Breath practice (5+ min), Body scan/grounding, Evening check-in, Used grounding card when needed
- Checkboxes for each day (M T W T F S S)

**Weekly Reflections:**
- 4 sections (one per week)
- What I noticed about my nervous system
- What was challenging
- What helped

**Month-End Reflection:**
- My nervous system feels: (circle options)
- One thing I learned about myself
- One practice I'll continue
- Celebration (no matter how small!)

## 7. Polyvagal State Tracking Wheel

### Purpose
Visual tool for tracking which autonomic state is dominant throughout the day.

### Design Specifications
- **Size:** Letter (8.5" × 11")
- **Layout:** Portrait with circular diagram
- **Pages:** 1

### Content

**Circular Diagram divided into three sections:**

**VENTRAL VAGAL (Green zone)**
- Safe & Social
- Calm, connected, grounded
- Can think clearly
- Open to connection

**SYMPATHETIC (Red zone)**
- Mobilized
- Anxious, restless, activated
- Fight or flight energy
- Ready for action

**DORSAL VAGAL (Blue zone)**
- Immobilized
- Numb, disconnected, low energy
- Shutdown or collapse
- Conserving energy

**Daily Tracking:**
- Color in or mark which zone you're in at different times
- Space for notes on what shifted you between zones

## Implementation Notes

### For Fern & Feather Practice

**Client Use:**
- Provide at intake or first session
- Review together to ensure understanding
- Encourage regular use between sessions
- Check in on tracking during sessions

**Marketing Use:**
- Grounding cards can be lead magnet (free PDF download)
- Window of tolerance worksheet can be blog post content upgrade
- Habit tracker can be email sequence freebie

**Digital Product Potential:**
- Printable bundle for sale ($7-15)
- Fillable PDF versions for digital use
- Custom branded versions for other therapists (B2B)

### Printing Recommendations

**Professional Printing:**
- Use 100lb cardstock for cards
- Matte finish reduces glare
- Consider rounded corners for cards
- Use eco-friendly paper for brand alignment

**DIY Printing:**
- Print cards on heavy cardstock (110lb+)
- Laminate for durability
- Use paper cutter for clean edges
- Store in small bag or ring
