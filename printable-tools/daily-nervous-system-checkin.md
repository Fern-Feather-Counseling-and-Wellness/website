# Daily Nervous System Check-In

*A Printable Worksheet for Tracking Your Nervous System State*

**Fern & Feather Counseling and Wellness**

---

## Instructions

Use this worksheet daily (morning, midday, or evening) to build awareness of your nervous system patterns. With practice, you'll notice state shifts earlier and have more choice in your responses.

**Time to complete:** 3-5 minutes  
**Best used:** Consistently for 30 days to establish baseline patterns

## PART 4: Regulation Practice

### Choose ONE practice based on your state:

**IF SYMPATHETIC AROUSAL (Fight/Flight):**
- [ ] Physiological Sigh: 2 sharp inhales through nose, long exhale (x3)
- [ ] Cold water on wrists or splash face
- [ ] Ground through feet: Press firmly into floor for 30 seconds
- [ ] Orient: Look around, name 5 things you see
- [ ] Movement: Shake out hands, stretch, brief walk

**IF DORSAL SHUTDOWN (Freeze):**
- [ ] Gentle movement: Rock side to side, sway
- [ ] Warmth: Hold warm drink, wrap in blanket
- [ ] Vocalization: Hum, sigh, or make low sounds
- [ ] Reach out: Text/call someone safe
- [ ] Small action: Do one tiny task to build momentum

**IF ALREADY REGULATED (Ventral Vagal):**
- [ ] Savor the moment: Notice and appreciate this state
- [ ] Connect: Reach out to someone you care about
- [ ] Create: Do something expressive while feeling good
- [ ] Rest: Allow yourself to just be

## QUICK REFERENCE: Regulation Tools

**Emergency Regulation (30 seconds):**
- Physiological sigh (2 inhales, 1 long exhale)
- Cold water on face/wrists
- Press feet firmly into ground
- Look around and name your location

**Short Reset (2-3 minutes):**
- Box breathing (4 counts: in, hold, out, hold)
- Orient to the room using all 5 senses
- Gentle stretching or shaking
- Splash cold water on face

**Deep Regulation (10+ minutes):**
- Co-regulation with trusted person or pet
- Somatic tracking (noticing body sensations without judgment)
- Trauma-informed yoga or gentle movement
- Grounding in nature

## SCIENCE BEHIND THIS PRACTICE

This check-in is based on **polyvagal theory** (Porges, 2011), which describes how the autonomic nervous system responds to safety and threat. Regular tracking builds **interoceptive awareness** — the ability to sense internal body states — which research links to improved emotion regulation and reduced anxiety (Farb et al., 2015).

Tracking nervous system states isn't about judgment or "fixing" yourself. It's about building the capacity to notice, name, and eventually have more choice in your responses.
