## Card Designs (8 total)

### Card 1: 5-4-3-2-1 Sensory Grounding
**Front:**
**Title:** 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding
**Tagline:** "Come back to your senses"
**Visual:** Simple icon showing five senses

**Back:**
**Instructions:**
1. **5 things you can SEE** (name them)
2. **4 things you can TOUCH** (describe textures)
3. **3 things you can HEAR** (identify sounds)
4. **2 things you can SMELL** (or remember smells)
5. **1 thing you can TASTE** (or imagine)

**Why It Works:** Activates sensory cortex, interrupts panic cycle, brings awareness to present moment.
**Tip:** Start with your dominant sense if overwhelmed.

### Card 2: Box Breathing
**Front:**
**Title:** Box Breathing
**Tagline:** "Reset your nervous system"
**Visual:** Square with arrows showing 4-count pattern

**Back:**
**Instructions:**
1. **Inhale** for 4 counts
2. **Hold** for 4 counts
3. **Exhale** for 4 counts
4. **Hold** for 4 counts
5. Repeat 4-6 times

**Why It Works:** Stimulates vagus nerve, increases heart rate variability, signals safety to nervous system.
**Tip:** Use when feeling anxious, before difficult conversations, or to improve focus.

### Card 3: Temperature Shift
**Front:**
**Title:** Temperature Grounding
**Tagline:** "Shock your system gently"
**Visual:** Thermometer or ice cube icon

**Back:**
**Options:**
- **Cold water** on wrists/face
- **Ice cube** in hand (notice melting)
- **Warm tea** (feel cup warmth)
- **Contrast shower** (alternate warm/cold)

**Why It Works:** Temperature changes activate the mammalian diving reflex, slowing heart rate and reducing panic.
**Tip:** Keep a cold pack in freezer for panic moments.

### Card 4: Body Scan
**Front:**
**Title:** Body Scan
**Tagline:** "Where are you holding tension?"
**Visual:** Outline of body with highlighted areas

**Back:**
**Instructions:**
1. Start at **feet** — notice sensations
2. Move to **legs** — tension? Temperature?
3. Continue upward: **hips, belly, chest, shoulders, arms, hands, neck, face**
4. Simply notice — no judgment

**Why It Works:** Increases interoception (body awareness), reduces dissociation, identifies where stress is stored.
**Tip:** Practice daily to build body awareness.

### Card 5: Mantra Repetition
**Front:**
**Title:** Mantra Grounding
**Tagline:** "Words to anchor you"
**Visual:** Speech bubble with calming word

**Back:**
**Mantra Options:**
- "I am here. I am safe."
- "This feeling will pass."
- "Breathe in calm, breathe out stress."
- "I am more than my anxiety."
- "One moment at a time."

**Instructions:** Choose one. Repeat silently or aloud 10+ times.

**Why It Works:** Activates language centers, disrupts catastrophic thinking, provides cognitive anchor.
**Tip:** Create your own personal mantra.

### Card 6: Progressive Muscle Relaxation
**Front:**
**Title:** Muscle Relaxation
**Tagline:** "Tense, then release"
**Visual:** Muscle diagram showing tension/release

**Back:**
**Instructions:**
1. **Feet** — curl toes, hold 5s, release
2. **Legs** — tense thighs, hold, release
3. **Buttocks** — squeeze, hold, release
4. **Belly** — tighten, hold, release
5. **Chest/back** — squeeze shoulders together
6. **Arms/hands** — make fists
7. **Face** — scrunch, then relax

**Why It Works:** Releases physical tension stored from stress, teaches body difference between tense/relaxed.
**Tip:** Best done lying down before bed.

### Card 7: Orienting to Environment
**Front:**
**Title:** Environment Scan
**Tagline:** "Where am I really?"
**Visual:** Comprose or room icon

**Back:**
**Instructions:**
1. **Name** where you are (building, room)
2. **Date and time** (say it aloud)
3. **Three safe things** you can see
4. **One exit** you could use
5. **One comforting object** nearby

**Why It Works:** Reduces dissociation, re-establishes time/place awareness, identifies safety resources.
**Tip:** Useful after flashbacks or dissociation.

### Card 8: Self-Hug / Self-Massage
**Front:**
**Title:** Self-Soothing Touch
**Tagline:** "Your own comforting touch"
**Visual:** Hands giving self-hug

**Back:**
**Options:**
- **Self-hug** — cross arms, gently squeeze
- **Hand on heart** — feel heartbeat
- **Hand on belly** — gentle circles
- **Temple massage** — small circles
- **Shoulder squeeze** — both hands

**Why It Works:** Releases oxytocin (bonding hormone), provides proprioceptive input, simulates comforting touch.
**Tip:** Use when feeling lonely or needing comfort.
