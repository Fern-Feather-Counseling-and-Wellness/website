## Part 3: Maintaining Momentum

### The Natural Ebb and Flow

Your practice will not be consistent—and that's normal. There will be:

**High-Momentum Periods:**
- When life is stable
- When you have support
- When parts are cooperative
- When you see progress

**Low-Momentum Periods:**
- During life stressors
- When deeper material surfaces
- When parts are resistant
- When you're tired or overwhelmed

**Both are part of the process.**

### Strategies for Maintaining Practice

**When Momentum Is High:**
- Deepen your practice—try new exercises
- Work with parts you've been avoiding
- Build habits and routines
- Document insights

**When Momentum Is Low:**
- Go back to basics—simple grounding, breathing
- Focus on relationship with parts rather than "progress"
- Be gentle with yourself
- Remember: rest is also practice

**The Minimum Viable Practice:**
Even when you can't do your full practice, you can:
- Take three conscious breaths
- Do a 30-second body check-in
- Use your Self-energy anchor once
- Thank your parts for their work

## Part 5: Living Somatic IFS

### The Ongoing Practice

Somatic IFS is not something you "finish." It's a way of being in relationship with yourself. Here's what that looks like:

**Daily:**
- Brief check-ins with your body
- Moments of Self-energy
- Relationship with activated parts

**Weekly:**
- Deeper practice sessions
- Journaling
- Review and reflection

**Monthly:**
- Pattern review
- Practice adjustment
- Celebration of growth

**Ongoing:**
- Relationship with your internal system
- Learning and deepening
- Integration into all areas of life

### The Signs of a Living Practice

You'll know your practice is alive when:
- You notice parts in real-time during your day
- You can access Self-energy when you need it
- Your body feels like a resource rather than an enemy
- You have compassion for your struggles
- You're curious about your reactions rather than judgmental
- You can repair when parts take over
- You keep learning and growing

## Appendix: Quick Reference Guide

### The Eight C's of Self-Energy
1. **Compassion** — Warmth for all parts
2. **Curiosity** — Genuine interest without judgment
3. **Calm** — Settled presence
4. **Connectedness** — Sense of relationship
5. **Confidence** — Trust in one's capacity
6. **Creativity** — Fresh perspectives
7. **Courage** — Willingness to face difficulty
8. **Clarity** — Clear seeing

### The Three Types of Parts
1. **Managers** — Proactive protectors (control, plan, achieve)
2. **Firefighters** — Reactive protectors (extinguish pain when it breaks through)
3. **Exiles** — Hold pain, shame, trauma (protected by managers and firefighters)

### The Grounding Check (Quick Version)
1. Orient to present (5-4-3-2-1 senses)
2. Feel your ground (feet, seat, back)
3. Slow your exhale
4. Use your anchor

### The Relational Pause
1. Name it: "A part of me is activated"
2. Feel it: Notice the somatic signature
3. Breathe it: Two slow breaths
4. Choose: What would Self do?

### When to Seek Professional Help
- Suicidal thoughts or self-harm urges
- Unable to function in daily life
- Flashbacks you can't control
- Feeling stuck or overwhelmed
- Using substances to cope
