## Welcome: Why This Workbook Exists

**For years, I watched clients have profound insights in therapy... and then struggle to integrate them.**

They'd understand intellectually that their anxiety was a protective part trying to help. They'd grasp that their inner critic wasn't the enemy. They'd have moments of genuine Self-energy and compassion.

**But their bodies didn't get the memo.**

The racing heart. The clenched jaw. The frozen shoulders. The shallow breathing. These somatic patterns persisted even when their minds understood what was happening.

**This is the gap this workbook fills.**

Internal Family Systems (IFS) gives us a brilliant map of the psyche. Somatic practices give us access to the body where these parts live. Together, they create something powerful: **embodied parts work**.

### What Makes This Different

This isn't another IFS manual for therapists. It's not a generic somatic therapy workbook. It's specifically designed for **clients** who:
- Are already familiar with IFS concepts (through therapy or self-study)
- Want to deepen their parts work through the body
- Need practical, between-session support
- Are ready to move from insight to embodied transformation

**Every exercise in this workbook is:**
- ✅ Evidence-based (with APA citations)
- ✅ Trauma-informed
- ✅ Neurodiversity-affirming
- ✅ Designed for real humans with busy lives
- ✅ Grounded in both science and compassion

### How to Use This Workbook

**8 weeks. One module per week.** Each module includes:
- **Educational content** (the science and theory)
- **Somatic practices** (embodied exercises)
- **Parts work integration** (connecting body awareness to your internal system)
- **Daily micro-practices** (5-minute exercises for busy days)
- **Reflection prompts** (journaling to deepen understanding)

**Important:** This workbook complements therapy—it doesn't replace it. If you're working with an IFS therapist, bring your discoveries to sessions. If you're not in therapy, this can be a powerful self-study tool, but consider finding a therapist if you encounter intense trauma material.

### A Note on Language

Throughout this workbook, I use **parts language** ("a part of me feels anxious" rather than "I am anxious"). This isn't just semantics—it's a fundamental shift in how we relate to our internal experience. When we recognize that our thoughts, feelings, and sensations are parts of us (not all of us), we create space for something else: our Self.

**Self** (with a capital S) refers to that core essence within each of us that is compassionate, curious, calm, and connected. It's not another part—it's who we are beneath all the protective layers.

### What You'll Need

- This workbook (digital or printed)
- A journal for reflections
- Comfortable clothing for movement
- A quiet space where you won't be interrupted
- An open mind and a willingness to be surprised by your own system

### One Last Thing

**This work can be profound. It can also be uncomfortable.** Parts that have been protecting you for years may not immediately trust this new approach. That's normal. Go slowly. Honor your system's timing. And remember: you don't have to do this perfectly. There's no such thing as perfect parts work.

**Welcome to your embodied IFS journey.**

Let's begin.
