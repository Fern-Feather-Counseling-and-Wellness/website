## Executive Summary

This guide provides a complete 60-minute trauma-informed yoga sequence designed to regulate the nervous system, increase interoceptive awareness, and create a safe container for somatic exploration. Unlike traditional yoga classes, this sequence prioritizes **choice, predictability, and somatic safety** over achieving poses.

**Key Differentiators:**
- **Trauma-informed cueing** — language that avoids triggering metaphors
- **Polyvagal-aligned pacing** — gradual arousal regulation
- **Choice-based sequencing** — options for every pose, no "shoulds"
- **Somatic integration prompts** — bridging body awareness to emotional regulation
- **Science-backed** — incorporates 2024-2025 research on yoga for trauma recovery

## The 60-Minute Sequence

### Phase 1: Grounding & Orientation (10 minutes)
**Goal:** Establish safety, predictability, and present-moment awareness

**1. Welcome & Invitation (2 min)**
- "You're invited to participate in whatever way feels safe and accessible today"
- "You can modify, skip, or rest at any time — your choice is respected"
- "Notice the points of contact between your body and the floor/mat"

**2. Supported Breath Awareness (3 min)**
- Lying supine with knees bent, feet flat
- Hands resting on lower ribs or belly (optional)
- "Notice the natural rhythm of your breath — no need to change it"
- "If attention wanders, gently return to the sensation of breath"

**3. Gentle Joint Circles (5 min)**
- Ankles, wrists, neck (micro-movements only)
- **Language cue:** "Exploring what feels available today"
- **Avoid:** "Open your heart" or "Release trauma" (potentially triggering)
- **Modification:** Seated in chair, smaller range of motion

### Phase 2: Gentle Movement & Interoception (20 minutes)
**Goal:** Increase body awareness without overwhelm

**4. Cat-Cow with Variations (5 min)**
- Traditional cat-cow flow
- **Trauma-informed variations:**
  - Hands on wall instead of floor (more stability)
  - Seated version in chair
  - Micro-movements only (nodding head, gentle spinal flexion)
- **Cueing:** "Notice the sensations along your spine as you move"

**5. Supported Child's Pose & Variations (5 min)**
- Traditional child's pose
- **Trauma-informed variations:**
  - Side-lying fetal position
  - Seated forward fold with forehead on stacked hands
  - Prone relaxation (belly down) if grounding needed
- **Cueing:** "Find a shape that feels containing and supportive"

**6. Gentle Spinal Twist (5 min)**
- Supine twist with knees bent
- **Trauma-informed approach:**
  - Keep both shoulders grounded (less vulnerability)
  - Use blanket under knees for support
  - Option to keep gaze forward (not turned away)
- **Cueing:** "Notice the difference between left and right sides"

**7. Legs-Up-the-Wall (or Chair) (5 min)**
- **Classic:** Legs up wall with pelvis supported
- **Trauma-informed alternatives:**
  - Legs on chair seat
  - Supine with calves on bolster
  - Seated with legs extended
- **Cueing:** "Allow the support to hold you — you don't have to hold yourself up"

### Phase 3: Somatic Integration & Regulation (15 minutes)
**Goal:** Bridge body awareness to nervous system regulation

**8. Somatic Scanning (5 min)**
- Lying supine or seated
- **Non-judgmental inquiry:** "What sensations are present?"
- **Avoid interpretation:** Don't label sensations as "good/bad" or "trauma"
- **Focus on neutral descriptors:** Temperature, texture, pressure, movement
- **Example cues:** "Notice areas of warmth or coolness" / "Observe where you feel most connected to the surface beneath you"

**9. Titrated Breath Practices (5 min)**
- **Option A:** Extended exhale (4-count inhale, 6-count exhale)
- **Option B:** Balanced breathing (equal inhale/exhale)
- **Option C:** Natural breath observation only
- **Safety note:** Some trauma survivors experience breathwork as triggering — always offer "no change" option

**10. Resource Anchoring (5 min)**
- **Identify a neutral or pleasant sensation** (cool floor, soft blanket, gentle warmth)
- **Attend to it for 20-30 seconds**
- **Practice shifting attention** between neutral sensation and breath
- **Builds capacity for:** Emotional regulation without dissociation

### Phase 4: Transition & Integration (15 minutes)
**Goal:** Prepare for re-entry into daily life

**11. Gentle Transition Movements (5 min)**
- Slow rocking side-to-side
- Gentle wrist and ankle circles
- Micro-stretches based on individual preference
- **Cueing:** "Move in whatever way feels supportive as you transition"

**12. Seated Integration (5 min)**
- Comfortable seated position
- **Integration prompts (choose one):**
  - "What's one sensation you'll carry with you?"
  - "What felt supportive today?"
  - "What's one small way you can offer yourself kindness today?"
- **No sharing required** — internal reflection only

**13. Closing & Acknowledgement (5 min)**
- "Thank you for showing up for yourself today"
- "Whatever arose — sensations, emotions, thoughts — was welcome"
- "You're invited to move slowly as you transition"
- "Carry with you what supports you, leave behind what doesn't"

## Trauma-Informed Teaching Principles

### Language Guidelines
**✅ DO USE:**
- "You might try..." (invitational)
- "If it's available to you..." (choice-based)
- "Notice what you're experiencing..." (non-judgmental)
- "There's no right way..." (de-pathologizing)

**❌ AVOID:**
- "You should..." (prescriptive)
- "Release your trauma..." (interpretive)
- "Open your heart center..." (metaphorical)
- "This pose will heal..." (promissory)

### Safety Considerations
1. **Predictability:** Maintain consistent sequence structure
2. **Choice:** Offer multiple variations for every pose
3. **Control:** Participant determines depth, duration, participation
4. **Visibility:** Instructor stays in view, avoids unexpected touch
5. **Exit Strategy:** Clear path to door, permission to leave anytime

### Contraindications & Adaptations
**For dissociation:**
- Keep eyes open, focus on external environment
- Use stronger sensory input (cool pack, textured mat)
- Shorten session duration (20-30 min)

**For hyperarousal:**
- Limit dynamic movement
- Focus on grounding (weighted blanket, floor contact)
- Reduce sensory stimulation (dim lights, no music)

**For hypoarousal:**
- Gentle movement to increase arousal
- Brighter lighting, uplifting (non-lyrical) music
- Seated or standing vs. reclining

## Science & Research Integration

### Evidence Base (2024-2025)
- **Yoga for PTSD:** 2024 meta-analysis shows moderate effect sizes (g=0.56) for trauma symptoms reduction (Davis et al., 2024)
- **Interoception & Emotion Regulation:** Increased body awareness correlates with improved emotion regulation in trauma survivors (Füstös et al., 2024)
- **Polyvagal Theory Application:** Slow, predictable movement supports ventral vagal activation (Porges, 2023 update)

### Neurobiological Mechanisms
1. **Bottom-up processing:** Body sensation → emotional awareness → cognitive integration
2. **Window of Tolerance expansion:** Gradual exposure to sensation without overwhelm
3. **Interoceptive acuity:** Improved ability to detect and respond to internal states
4. **Heart rate variability:** Yoga practices increase HRV, correlate with resilience

### Integration with Therapy
**For therapists using this guide:**
- **Pre-session:** 10-minute sequence to ground before therapy
- **Post-session:** 15-minute sequence to integrate emotional material
- **Between sessions:** Home practice to maintain regulation
- **Crisis intervention:** Abbreviated 5-minute grounding sequence
