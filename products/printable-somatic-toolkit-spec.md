# Fern & Feather Printable Somatic Toolkit
## Product Specification & Design Brief

*A collection of 12 evidence-based printable tools for nervous system regulation, mood tracking, and somatic self-awareness*

## The 12 Tools

### 1. Daily Nervous System Check-In
**Purpose:** Morning and evening tracking of arousal states

**Design:**
- Single page, split into AM and PM sections
- Visual scale 1-10 with body-based descriptors:
  - 1-3: Settled, grounded, calm (green zone)
  - 4-6: Activated, alert, engaged (yellow zone)
  - 7-10: Overwhelmed, shutdown, panicked (red zone)
- Body outline for marking where sensation is felt
- Three prompts:
  - "What am I noticing in my body?"
  - "What might my nervous system need?"
  - "One small regulation practice I'll try:"

**Usage:** Daily, 2-minute practice

---

### 2. Window of Temperature Mapping Worksheet
**Purpose:** Visualize personal window of tolerance across time

**Design:**
- Full-page graph with days of week on X-axis
- Arousal level 0-10 on Y-axis
- Color-coded zones (green/yellow/red)
- Space for notes on triggers and regulation strategies used
- Weekly pattern reflection questions at bottom

**Usage:** Weekly tracking to identify patterns

---

### 3. Somatic Grounding Cards (Wallet Size)
**Purpose:** Portable regulation reminders

**Design:**
- 8 cards per page (2.5" x 3.5" each)
- Front: Technique name + simple illustration
- Back: Step-by-step instructions (when printed double-sided)
- Techniques included:
  1. 5-4-3-2-1 Senses
  2. Physiological Sigh
  3. Hand on Heart + Belly
  4. Cold Water Splash
  5. Wall Press
  6. Self-Hug
  7. Orienting (look around)
  8. Foot Grounding

**Usage:** Cut out, laminate optional, carry in wallet/purse

---

### 4. Trigger Tracker & Response Log
**Purpose:** Identify patterns in nervous system activation

**Design:**
- Table format, 6 entries per page
- Columns:
  - Date/Time
  - Trigger (what happened)
  - Body sensation (where + how)
  - Intensity (1-10 scale)
  - Response (what I did)
  - Regulation tool used
  - Time to settle
  - Notes/patterns

**Usage:** Use after activation events to build self-awareness

---

### 5. Parts Mapping Worksheet (IFS-Informed)
**Purpose:** Identify and befriend protective internal parts

**Design:**
- Circular diagram with center "Self" circle
- 4-6 outer circles for different parts
- Prompts for each part:
  - Name/role (e.g., "The Critic," "The Worrier")
  - What it looks/feels like
  - What it's afraid would happen without it
  - What it needs from Self
  - How to recognize when it's activated
- Color-coding option

**Usage:** Initial mapping + update as relationship with parts evolves

---

### 6. Breath Technique Quick Reference
**Purpose:** Easy comparison of 5 evidence-based breathing techniques

**Design:**
- Single page, 5 columns
- Each technique box includes:
  - Name + when to use it
  - Visual breathing pattern diagram
  - Step-by-step instructions
  - Best for: [state]
  - Duration
- Techniques:
  1. Physiological Sigh (acute stress)
  2. Box Breathing (focus/anxiety)
  3. 4-7-8 Breathing (sleep/calm)
  4. Coherent Breathing (daily regulation)
  5. Resonant Breathing (deep practice)

**Usage:** Post near desk/bed, reference during practice

---

### 7. Body Sensation Vocabulary Builder
**Purpose:** Expand language for describing internal experience

**Design:**
- Categorized word banks with space for personal additions:
  - Temperature (hot, cold, warm, cool, burning, icy, neutral...)
  - Texture (smooth, rough, dense, light, heavy, airy, thick...)
  - Shape (contracted, expanded, sharp, dull, diffuse, focused...)
  - Movement (buzzing, pulsing, flowing, stuck, swirling, trembling...)
  - Weight (grounded, floating, sinking, rising, anchored...)
  - Other (numb, tingly, open, closed, spacious...)
- Practice sentences: "Right now I feel ______ in my ______."

**Usage:** Reference when journaling or tracking sensations

---

### 8. Evening Release & Integration Ritual
**Purpose:** Wind-down practice for better sleep and processing

**Design:**
- Single page with guided structure:
  - "What am I carrying from today?" (journaling space)
  - Body scan checklist (head, neck, shoulders, chest, belly, hips, legs, feet)
  - "What can I set down?" (intention setting)
  - Tomorrow's top priority (one thing)
  - Gratitude (3 things)
  - Breath practice selection (checkboxes)
  - Sleep intention

**Usage:** 10-15 minute evening routine

---

### 9. Polyvagal State Tracker
**Purpose:** Track movement between ventral, sympathetic, and dorsal states

**Design:**
- Weekly calendar format (7 days)
- Three colored sections per day:
  - Blue (ventral vagal): Connected, calm, social
  - Yellow (sympathetic): Activated, mobilized, anxious
  - Red (dorsal vagal): Shutdown, numb, disconnected
- Pie chart or bar graph for each day showing time in each state
- Reflection question: "What helped me shift states this week?"

**Usage:** End-of-day 2-minute check-in

---

### 10. Co-Regulation Connection Log
**Purpose:** Track relationships that support nervous system regulation

**Design:**
- Two-column layout:
  - **Regulating Connections** (people/activities/pets that help me settle)
    - Name
    - How they help
    - Last contact
    - Frequency goal
  - **Dysregulating Connections** (people/situations that activate me)
    - Name/situation
    - What happens in my body
    - Boundary needs
    - Support needed
- Middle section: "My co-regulation plan" (intentional connection goals)

**Usage:** Quarterly review and relationship intention-setting

---

### 11. Somatic Experiment Log
**Purpose:** Track effectiveness of different regulation techniques

**Design:**
- Table format, 4 experiments per page
- For each technique tested:
  - Technique name
  - Date/time
  - State before (1-10)
  - State after (1-10)
  - Context (where, what was happening)
  - What worked
  - What didn't
  - Will I use this again? (Y/N/Maybe)

**Usage:** During learning phase to build personalized toolkit

---

### 12. Emergency Regulation Plan
**Purpose:** Pre-planned response for acute activation moments

**Design:**
- Single page, wallet-size option (foldable)
- Sections:
  - **My early warning signs:** (personal checklist)
  - **Step 1 - Immediate grounding:** (my go-to technique)
  - **Step 2 - If still activated:** (secondary technique)
  - **Step 3 - If overwhelmed:** (who to call, where to go)
  - **My regulation reminders:** (affirmations that help)
  - **Emergency contacts:** (therapist, crisis line, trusted person)
  - **What NOT to do:** (personal unhelpful patterns)

**Usage:** Keep visible (fridge, desk) and carry copy in wallet

## Design Specifications

### Visual Style
**Aesthetic:** Calm, organic, trauma-informed
- Soft, muted color palette:
  - Sage green (#9CAF88)
  - Terracotta (#D4A373)
  - Warm cream (#F5F1E8)
  - Soft blue (#A8C4D9)
  - Dusty rose (#D4A5A5)
- Nature elements: Ferns, feathers, leaves, organic shapes
- Plenty of white space (not overwhelming)
- Rounded corners on boxes (softer feel)
- Readable fonts: Sans-serif for headers, serif or clean sans for body

- High contrast text options
- Large enough fill-in spaces
- Dyslexia-friendly font option
- Black and white print-friendly version
- Fillable PDF fields for digital use

- Tagline: "Where Biology Meets Being Human"

## Usage Instructions (For Customers)

### How to Use This Toolkit

**Getting Started:**
1. Print the entire toolkit or select pages you need
2. Consider laminating wallet cards for durability
3. Put emergency plan somewhere visible
4. Choose one tool to start with (don't overwhelm yourself)

**Daily Practice:**
- Morning: Daily Nervous System Check-In (2 min)
- Throughout day: Grounding Cards as needed
- Evening: Evening Release Ritual (10 min)

**Weekly Practice:**
- Window of Tolerance Mapping
- Polyvagal State Tracker review
- Co-Regulation Connection check

**As Needed:**
- Trigger Tracker after activation events
- Somatic Experiment Log when trying new techniques
- Parts Mapping during IFS work

**Crisis Moments:**
- Emergency Regulation Plan
- Wallet-size grounding cards
