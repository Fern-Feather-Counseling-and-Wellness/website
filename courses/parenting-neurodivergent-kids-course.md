## A Digital Course for Parents of ADHD & Autistic Children
### Nervous System Regulation for the Whole Family

## Course Overview

**Format:** 8-module self-paced online course with video lessons, worksheets, and downloadable tools  
**Target Audience:** Parents of children with ADHD, autism, or sensory processing differences  
**Price Point:** $197 (self-paced) / $397 (with monthly Q&A calls)  
**Platform:** Teachable or Kajabi  
**Estimated Length:** 6-8 hours of content + implementation

**Why This Course:**
Parents of neurodivergent kids are exhausted, overwhelmed, and drowning in conflicting advice. They're told to "be consistent" and "set boundaries" but their child's nervous system doesn't respond to standard parenting approaches. This course meets them where they are — with science-backed, nervous system-informed strategies that actually work.

**Learning Outcomes:**
By the end of this course, parents will:
- Understand their child's unique nervous system
- Recognize signs of dysregulation vs. "bad behavior"
- Have a toolkit of co-regulation strategies
- Know how to advocate effectively at school
- Build sustainable self-care practices (without guilt)
- Create a family environment that supports everyone's regulation

## Module 1: Understanding Your Neurodivergent Child's Nervous System

**Learning Objectives:**
- Learn the neuroscience of ADHD and autism
- Understand sensory processing differences
- Distinguish between behavior and nervous system dysregulation

**Video Lesson 1.1:** "It's Not Defiance, It's Dysregulation" (22 min)
- ADHD neuroscience: Executive function, dopamine, time blindness, RSD
- Autism neuroscience: Sensory processing, need for predictability, monotropism
- Window of tolerance — ND edition
- Behavior vs. dysregulation

**Video Lesson 1.2:** "Sensory Processing Deep Dive" (18 min)
- The 8 senses (including vestibular, proprioception, interoception)
- Sensory triggers vs. preferences
- Sensory seeking vs. avoiding

**Worksheets:**
- My Child's Nervous System Profile
- Sensory Environment Audit

## Module 4: Beyond Behavior — Collaborative Problem-Solving

**Learning Objectives:**
- Learn Ross Greene's Collaborative & Proactive Solutions model
- Distinguish skill deficits from performance problems
- Practice Plan B problem-solving

**Video Lesson 4.1:** "The Skills Model" (22 min)
- "Kids do well if they can" philosophy
- Lagging skills vs. unsolved problems
- Three plans: Plan A (impose will), Plan B (collaborate), Plan C (drop for now)

**Video Lesson 4.2:** "Collaborative Problem-Solving in Action" (20 min)
- The three steps: Empathy, Define the Problem, Invitation
- Video examples: Homework, screen time, morning routines, sibling conflicts

**Worksheets:**
- Lagging Skills Assessment
- Unsolved Problems Inventory
- Plan B Practice Template
