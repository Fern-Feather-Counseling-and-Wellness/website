## Week 1: Understanding Your Nervous System

### Learning Objectives
- Learn the neuroscience of anxiety and stress responses
- Understand the difference between acute stress and chronic dysregulation
- Identify personal nervous system patterns
- Map your unique triggers and regulation patterns

### Video Lesson 1: The Biology of Anxiety (18 minutes)
**Script Outline:**

*[Opening hook — 30 seconds]*
"Your anxiety isn't a character flaw. It's biology. And once you understand the biology, you can work with your body instead of fighting it."

*[Core content]*
1. **The Stress Response System** (4 min)
   - Sympathetic activation (fight/flight/freeze)
   - The role of the amygdala and HPA axis
   - Why your body responds the way it does
   - The neuroscience: LeDoux's dual-pathway model (2024)

2. **Chronic vs. Acute Stress** (5 min)
   - How the nervous system is supposed to work
   - What happens when stress becomes chronic
   - The cost of dysregulation: McEwen's allostatic load research
   - Why "just relax" doesn't work

3. **Your Unique Pattern** (6 min)
   - Fight, flight, freeze, fawn — which is your default?
   - Understanding your personal trigger map
   - The concept of "neuroceptive" bias (Porges, 2021)
   - Why your childhood shaped your nervous system

4. **The Window of Tolerance** (3 min)
   - Siegel's model explained
   - Hyperarousal vs. hypoarousal
   - Expanding your capacity for stress
   - The goal: flexibility, not constant calm

*[Closing — 30 seconds]*
"This week, you're becoming a student of your own nervous system. The awareness you're building is the foundation for everything else."

### Worksheet 1.1: My Nervous System Profile
**Downloadable PDF with sections:**
- My typical stress response (fight/flight/freeze/fawn checklist)
- Common triggers (people, places, situations, sensations)
- Early warning signs my body gives me
- What regulation feels like in my body
- My current coping strategies (adaptive vs. maladaptive)
- My window of tolerance: Where do I spend most of my time?

### Worksheet 1.2: Trigger Mapping Exercise
**Instructions:**
"Think of the last time you felt overwhelmed, anxious, or dysregulated. Walk through this timeline:"

1. **The Setup** (2 hours before)
   - What was happening in your environment?
   - What were you thinking about?
   - How was your body feeling?

2. **The Trigger** (moment of activation)
   - What specifically happened?
   - What did you notice first?

3. **The Escalation** (0-10 minutes after)
   - How did your body respond?
   - What thoughts came up?
   - What did you feel compelled to do?

4. **The Peak** (10-30 minutes after)
   - What was your internal experience?
   - How intense was it (0-10)?

5. **The Recovery** (30+ minutes after)
   - How did you come back to baseline?
   - What helped? What didn't?

### Audio Practice 1: Body Scan for Nervous System Awareness (12 minutes)
**Guided meditation script:**

*[Opening — 1 min]*
"Find a comfortable position... This practice is about mapping your nervous system from the inside out..."

*[Body scan — 9 min]*
- Grounding through breath and sensation
- Scanning for areas of tension, activation, or numbness
- Noticing temperature, texture, weight
- Observing without judgment
- Tracking the impulse to fix or change

*[Closing — 2 min]*
"This is your baseline. This is where you start. Every practice from here builds on this awareness."

### Week 1 Homework
1. Complete Nervous System Profile worksheet
2. Do one trigger mapping exercise (real-time or recent memory)
3. Body scan practice: 5 times this week
4. Daily check-ins: Rate arousal level (1-10) 3x/day

## Week 2: The Language of Sensation

### Learning Objectives
- Shift from cognitive to somatic awareness
- Learn to track bodily sensations as information
- Understand the difference between thoughts and sensations
- Build vocabulary for internal experience

### Video Lesson 2: Listening to Your Body (22 minutes)

*[Opening hook]*
"Your body has been trying to talk to you your whole life. This week, you're finally learning its language."

*[Core content]*
1. **Why We Ignore Our Bodies** (5 min)
   - Cultural messaging: "Mind over matter"
   - Trauma and dissociation
   - The cost of disembodiment
   - Research: Interoceptive awareness and mental health (2024)

2. **Sensation vs. Interpretation** (6 min)
   - The difference between "heart racing" and "I'm dying"
   - How thoughts amplify sensations
   - The anxiety feedback loop
   - Practice: Can you separate sensation from story?

3. **Building Somatic Vocabulary** (7 min)
   - Temperature: hot, cold, warm, cool, burning, icy
   - Texture: smooth, rough, dense, light, heavy, airy
   - Shape: contracted, expanded, sharp, dull, diffuse, focused
   - Movement: buzzing, pulsing, flowing, stuck, swirling
   - Weight: grounded, floating, sinking, rising
   - Practice: Describing sensations without interpretation

4. **The Felt Sense** (4 min)
   - Gendlin's concept explained
   - The "bodily knowing" that precedes words
   - How to access the felt sense
   - The role of slowing down

- Movement words that fit my experience

**Guided practice:**
- Grounding and settling
- Choosing one sensation to track
- Observing without interpretation (5 minutes)

### Experiential Exercise: The 5-Minute Check-In
**Instructions for daily practice:**
1. Set a timer for 5 minutes
2. Close eyes or soften gaze
3. Ask: "What am I sensing right now?"
4. Name 3 sensations using descriptive words only
5. No interpretation, no fixing, just noticing
6. When timer ends, take 3 breaths and open eyes

## Week 4: Breath as Regulation Tool

### Learning Objectives
- Understand the physiology of breathwork
- Learn 5 evidence-based breathing techniques
- Match breath techniques to different nervous system states
- Create a personal breath practice

### Video Lesson 4: The Power of the Breath (20 minutes)

*[Opening hook]*
"Your breath is the only autonomic function you can consciously control. That's not an accident — that's your built-in regulation tool."

*[Core content]*
1. **Breath & the Nervous System** (5 min)
   - The vagus nerve and breath
   - How breathing affects heart rate variability
   - The science of slow breathing (4-6 breaths/minute)
   - Research: Breathwork for anxiety and PTSD (2024)

2. **Technique 1: Physiological Sigh** (3 min)
   - Stanford research (Huberman Lab)
   - Double inhale through nose, long exhale through mouth
   - When to use it: acute stress, overwhelm, panic
   - Practice demonstration

3. **Technique 2: Box Breathing** (3 min)
   - Navy SEAL technique
   - Inhale-hold-exhale-hold (4 counts)
   - When to use it: focus, preparation, mild anxiety
   - Practice demonstration

4. **Technique 3: 4-7-8 Breathing** (3 min)
   - Dr. Andrew Weil's technique
   - Inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8
   - When to use it: sleep, deep relaxation
   - Practice demonstration

5. **Technique 4: Coherent Breathing** (3 min)
   - 5-6 breaths per minute
   - Equal inhale and exhale
   - When to use it: daily practice, HRV optimization
   - Practice demonstration

6. **Technique 5: Resonant Breathing** (3 min)
   - Finding your personal resonance frequency
   - Usually around 3-7 breaths per minute
   - When to use it: deep regulation, trauma work
   - Practice demonstration

## Week 5: Expanding Your Window of Tolerance

### Learning Objectives
- Understand the concept of titrated exposure
- Learn pendulation between activation and regulation
- Build capacity for uncomfortable sensations
- Practice staying with activation without overwhelm

### Video Lesson 5: Building Resilience (28 minutes)

*[Opening hook]*
"You can't eliminate stress from your life. But you can expand your capacity to handle it. That's what this week is about."

*[Core content]*
1. **The Window of Tolerance Revisited** (5 min)
   - Siegel's model in depth
   - Hyperarousal (fight/flight) vs. hypoarousal (freeze)
   - The goal: widening the window, not staying in the middle
   - Research: Window expansion through somatic practices (2024)

2. **Titration: The Key to Expansion** (6 min)
   - What is titration? (Levine's concept)
   - Touching into activation, then returning to regulation
   - Why we don't flood ourselves
   - The importance of choice and control
   - Practice: Finding your edge

3. **Pendulation Practice** (7 min)
   - Noticing activation (even mild)
   - Tracking the sensation
   - Finding a resource/regulation
   - Moving between the two
   - Building the "regulation muscle"
   - Demonstration of the practice

4. **Working with Discharge** (5 min)
   - What is somatic discharge?
   - Trembling, heat, tears, yawning — all normal
   - Not interpreting discharge
   - Creating safe space for completion
   - When to seek professional support

5. **Building Capacity Over Time** (5 min)
   - This is a practice, not a quick fix
   - Tracking progress over weeks/months
   - Celebrating small wins
   - The spiral nature of healing

### Audio Practice 5: Guided Pendulation (20 minutes)
**Advanced somatic practice:**
- Grounding and settling (3 min)
- Finding a mild activation (3 min)
- Tracking the sensation (4 min)
- Finding a resource (3 min)
- Moving between activation and resource (5 min)
- Integration and closing (2 min)

### Experiential Exercise: The 10-Minute Edge Practice
**Instructions:**
1. Find a quiet space where you won't be interrupted
2. Recall a mildly activating situation (3-4/10 intensity)
3. Notice where you feel it in your body
4. Track the sensation for 2-3 minutes
5. When it feels like too much, shift to your favorite grounding technique
6. Once regulated, check: Can you go back to the activation?
7. Repeat pendulation 2-3 times
8. Close with 2 minutes of grounding

**Important:** If you go above 6/10, stop and ground. This is titration, not flooding.

### Week 5 Homework

3. Try the 10-minute edge practice once
4. Log each pendulation experience
5. Notice one moment where you stayed with mild activation instead of escaping
