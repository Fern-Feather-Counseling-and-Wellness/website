## Workshop Overview

**Format:** 6-hour live workshop (can be delivered in-person or virtually)  
**CEU Credits:** 6 CEUs (pending approval from APA, NBCC, GA LPCA)  
**Target Audience:** Licensed mental health professionals, counselors, social workers, psychologists  
**Price:** $199 early bird / $249 regular  
**Capacity:** 20-30 participants (optimal for interaction)  
**Delivery:** In-person (Alpharetta, GA) or via Zoom with breakout rooms

**Why This Workshop:**
Therapist burnout is at epidemic levels. 52% of therapists report burnout (SimplePractice, 2023). Yet most CEUs focus on clinical skills, not clinician wellbeing. This workshop fills that gap — giving therapists evidence-based tools to recover from burnout and build sustainable practices.

**Learning Objectives:**
By the end of this workshop, participants will:
- Understand the neurobiology of burnout and compassion fatigue
- Assess their own burnout risk and current status
- Implement evidence-based nervous system regulation techniques
- Create personalized burnout prevention and recovery plans
- Design sustainable clinical practices
- Build professional resilience

## Workshop Schedule (6 Hours)

### 9:00 AM - 9:30 AM | Welcome & Check-In (30 min)

**Opening Ritual:**
- Grounding exercise for the group
- Name, credential, one word for how you're arriving
- Setting container: Confidentiality, self-care, permission to step out

**Workshop Overview:**
- Agenda for the day
- CEU requirements
- Materials and resources

---

### 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM | Session 1: The Neurobiology of Burnout (90 min)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand burnout as nervous system dysregulation
- Learn the difference between burnout, compassion fatigue, and vicarious trauma
- Recognize personal warning signs

**Content:**

**1. Defining Burnout (20 min)**
- Maslach's three dimensions: Exhaustion, cynicism, inefficacy
- Beyond "self-care": Why bubble baths aren't enough
- The unique factors that make therapists vulnerable
- Research: Therapist burnout rates and trends (2023-2024 data)

**2. The Neurobiology (25 min)**
- HPA axis dysregulation in chronic stress
- Allostatic load: The wear and tear of caring
- The neuroscience of empathy and mirror neurons
- When helping hurts: The physiology of vicarious trauma
- Research: Neurobiological markers of burnout (2024 studies)

**3. Compassion Fatigue vs. Burnout vs. Vicarious Trauma (20 min)**
- Compassion fatigue: Empathy exhaustion
- Burnout: Systemic overwhelm
- Vicarious trauma: Changed worldview from exposure
- How they overlap and interact
- Self-assessment exercise

**4. Personal Warning Signs (15 min)**
- Physical, emotional, cognitive, behavioral, spiritual indicators
- Group share: "I know I'm burning out when..."
- Creating your personal early warning system

**Experiential (10 min):**
- Body scan for burnout symptoms
- Noticing without judgment

**Break:** 11:00 AM - 11:15 AM (15 min)

---

### 11:15 AM - 12:45 PM | Session 2: Nervous System Regulation for Therapists (90 min)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Learn evidence-based regulation techniques
- Practice techniques in real-time
- Build personalized regulation toolkit

**Content:**

**1. Why Therapists Struggle to Self-Regulate (15 min)**
- The "helper's trap": Putting others first
- Professional identity and "being fine"
- Time scarcity and client demands
- The myth of the "wounded healer"

**2. Bottom-Up Regulation (30 min)**
- Polyvagal-informed techniques
- Breathwork for therapists (physiological sigh, coherent breathing)
- Grounding and orienting
- Movement and somatic release
- **Practice:** Physiological sigh (3 rounds)

**3. Top-Down Regulation (20 min)**
- Cognitive reframing
- Boundaries as regulation
- Values clarification
- The "good enough" therapist
- **Practice:** Values-based boundary setting

**4. Relational Regulation (15 min)**
- Co-regulation with colleagues
- Supervision and consultation as nervous system support
- Peer support groups
- When to seek therapy

**5. Building Your Regulation Toolkit (10 min)**
- Quick techniques (under 2 minutes)
- Daily practices
- Emergency interventions
- Worksheet: My Regulation Menu

**Break:** 12:45 PM - 1:30 PM (45 min lunch)

---

### 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM | Session 3: Sustainable Practice Design (90 min)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Analyze current practice for burnout risk factors
- Design sustainable caseload and schedule
- Implement boundaries and systems

**Content:**

**1. The Burnout-Inducing Practice (20 min)**
- Caseload size and complexity
- Schedule density
- Client population and trauma exposure
- Administrative burden
- Financial pressure
- Isolation and lack of support
- Self-assessment: What's driving your burnout?

**2. Designing Your Sustainable Practice (30 min)**
- Optimal caseload: Research and reality
- Schedule design: Buffer time, variety, energy management
- Client mix: Balancing intensity
- The 4-day work week option
- Group vs. individual work
- **Exercise:** Redesign your ideal week

**3. Boundaries as Professional Practice (20 min)**
- Session length and frequency
- Contact between sessions
- Cancellation policies
- Scope of practice
- Saying no to referrals
- **Role-play:** Boundary conversations

**4. Systems and Automation (15 min)**
- EHR and practice management
- Automated scheduling and billing
- Templates and workflows
- Delegation and virtual assistants
- Reducing administrative drag

**5. Financial Sustainability (5 min)**
- Fee setting and worth
- Insurance vs. private pay
- Diversifying income (groups, courses, consulting)
- The business of therapy

## Facilitator Guide

### Nicole's Role

**As Facilitator:**
- Share expertise on nervous system regulation
- Create safe container for vulnerability
- Model self-compassion and authenticity
- Balance education with experiential learning

**As Fellow Therapist:**
- Acknowledge shared experience
- Share appropriate personal stories
- Avoid "expert" stance — emphasize learning together
- Normalize struggle

### Facilitation Tips

**Creating Safety:**
- Acknowledge burnout is stigmatized
- Emphasize confidentiality
- Give permission to opt out of exercises
- Normalize stepping out

**Managing Intensity:**
- Burnout work can be activating
- Have grounding tools ready
- Offer breaks as needed
- Know referral resources

**Balancing Content:**
- Education vs. experience: 50/50
- Group vs. individual work
- Heavy content with lightness
- Space for humor

### Logistics

**In-Person Setup:**
- Comfortable seating (not classroom style)
- Space for movement
- Natural light if possible
- Regulation tools available (fidgets, blankets)
- Healthy snacks and beverages
- Quiet space for breaks

**Virtual Setup:**
- Zoom with breakout rooms
- Chat for resource sharing
- Recording for review (optional)
- Digital workbook delivery
- Breakout room facilitation plan
