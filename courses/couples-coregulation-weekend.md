## A 2-Day Retreat for Couples Ready to Transform Their Connection

**Fern & Feather Wellness Center**

## Retreat Overview

**Format:** 2-day intensive weekend retreat (Saturday 9 AM - 6 PM, Sunday 9 AM - 4 PM)  
**Location:** Fern & Feather Wellness Center, Alpharetta, GA (or retreat venue)  
**Capacity:** 6-8 couples (12-16 participants)  
**Price:** $1,200 per couple ($600/person)  
**Includes:** All materials, meals, take-home practice kit  

**Target Participants:**
- Couples who feel disconnected or stuck in conflict patterns
- Partners where one or both have anxiety, trauma, or attachment wounds
- Couples who want to understand each other on a nervous system level
- Those ready for intensive experiential work (not in acute crisis)

**Prerequisites:**
- Brief intake call with both partners
- Not in active addiction or domestic violence
- Both partners willing to participate
- Commitment to attend full weekend

**Learning Outcomes:**
By the end of this retreat, couples will:
- Understand each partner's unique nervous system patterns
- Recognize their "couple dance" through a nervous system lens
- Practice co-regulation techniques together
- Build a shared language for states of activation
- Create a personalized "co-regulation toolkit"
- Design rituals for ongoing connection

## Theoretical Framework

### Core Models

**1. Polyvagal Theory (Porges)**
- Neuroception of safety in relationship
- Co-regulation as biological imperative
- The social engagement system in couples
- Detecting safety vs. threat in partner's cues

**2. Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) — Sue Johnson**
- Attachment needs in adult relationships
- The pursuer-distancer dance
- Creating secure bonding events
- Emotional accessibility and responsiveness

**3. Interpersonal Neurobiology (Siegel)**
- Mindsight in couples
- Integration and differentiation
- The "we" of relationship
- Resonance circuits

**4. Somatic Experiencing (Levine)**
- Tracking bodily states
- Titrated activation and repair
- Pendulation between challenge and resource
- Completing defensive responses

**5. Internal Family Systems (Schwartz)**
- Parts that show up in relationship
- Self-energy as healing force
- Unburdening relational wounds
- Protector parts and vulnerability

## Retreat Schedule

---

## SATURDAY

### 9:00 AM - 9:30 AM | Arrival & Opening Circle (30 min)

**Welcome:**
- Couples arrive, settle in, light breakfast available
- Name tags with first names only
- Comfortable seating in circle

**Opening Ritual:**
- Grounding exercise as a group
- Introductions: Names, one word for why you're here
- Container setting: Confidentiality, safety, consent
- Overview of the weekend

---

### 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM | Session 1: The Neurobiology of Us (90 min)

**Theme:** Understanding your nervous systems as a couple

**Content:**

**1. Welcome & Orientation (15 min)**
- Retreat philosophy: Biology meets being human
- The couple as a nervous system unit
- What to expect this weekend
- Permission to go at your own pace

**2. Your Individual Nervous System Profile (30 min)**
- Lecture: Window of tolerance, triggers, regulation patterns
- Exercise: Each partner completes "My Nervous System Profile"
  - How I know I'm activated
  - What dysregulation looks like for me
  - What helps me regulate
  - My attachment style patterns
- Share with partner (15 min structured sharing)

**3. The Couple Dance (30 min)**
- Lecture: How nervous systems interact
- Common patterns:
  - Anxious-anxious (amplification)
  - Avoidant-avoidant (distance)
  - Anxious-avoidant (the trap)
  - Mixed/secure combinations
- Exercise: Identify your pattern
- Group share: Common themes

**Break:** 11:00 AM - 11:15 AM (15 min)

---

### 11:15 AM - 12:45 PM | Session 2: Tracking Activation in Real Time (90 min)

**Theme:** Learning to notice and name states

**Content:**

**1. The Language of Sensation (20 min)**
- Beyond "I'm upset" — learning somatic vocabulary
- Temperature, texture, shape, movement, weight
- Practice: Describing activation without interpretation

**2. The Signal Exercise (40 min)**
- Couples face each other
- Partner A: Notice and describe internal state
- Partner B: Mirror back what they hear, observe nonverbally
- Switch roles
- Debrief: What did you notice about your partner? About yourself?

**3. Building the Pause (20 min)**
- The power of the pause in conflict
- Recognizing early warning signs
- Practice: "I'm noticing I'm getting activated. I need a pause."
- Creating a shared signal for timeout

**4. Repair Attempts (10 min)**
- What is a repair attempt? (Gottman)
- How nervous system state affects repair
- Practice: Simple repair scripts

**Lunch:** 12:45 PM - 1:45 PM (60 min)
- Catered lunch provided
- Couples eat together, informal connection
- Optional: Journaling prompts

---

### 1:45 PM - 3:15 PM | Session 3: Co-Regulation Practices (90 min)

**Theme:** Learning to regulate together

**Content:**

**1. What is Co-Regulation? (15 min)**
- The science: Mirror neurons, ventral vagal connection
- Why proximity matters
- The difference between co-regulation and dependence

**2. Co-Regulation Techniques (45 min)**
- **Synchronized breathing:** Breathing together
- **Grounding together:** Feet on floor, back-to-back
- **The hold:** Safe embrace (if comfortable)
- **Eye gazing:** Soft gaze practice
- **Movement:** Swaying, rocking together
- Couples try each technique, find what works

**3. The Repair Conversation (30 min)**
- Structure: Speaker/Listener
- Speaker: Share from vulnerable place
- Listener: Track own nervous system while listening
- Switch roles
- Practice with facilitator guidance

**Break:** 3:15 PM - 3:30 PM (15 min)

---

### 3:30 PM - 5:00 PM | Session 4: Working with Conflict (90 min)

**Theme:** Transforming the couple dance

**Content:**

**1. Understanding Your Conflict Pattern (20 min)**
- Lecture: The anatomy of a fight
- Escalation cycles
- Protest behaviors vs. true needs
- Exercise: Map your last conflict

**2. The Conflict Re-Do (50 min)**
- Couples choose a recent conflict
- Replay it "automatically" (5 min)
- Identify: What was happening in each nervous system?
- Replay with:
  - Pauses for regulation
  - Naming states
  - Repair attempts
  - Vulnerable sharing
- Debrief: What shifted?

**3. Creating New Patterns (20 min)**
- Identifying your "exit ramps" from the cycle
- Building a shared commitment
- Practice: "When I get activated, I will... When you get activated, I will..."

---

### 5:00 PM - 6:00 PM | Integration & Evening Transition (60 min)

**Content:**

**1. Dyad Processing (30 min)**
- Couples have unstructured time to process
- Journaling: "Today I learned..."
- Facilitators available for brief check-ins

**2. Group Closing (20 min)**
- One word check-out
- Preview of Sunday
- Evening assignment: Practice one technique at home

**3. Evening Free Time (10 min transition)**
- Couples depart for evening
- Dinner on their own (recommendations provided)
- Evening free — rest, connect, practice

---

## SUNDAY

### 9:00 AM - 9:30 AM | Arrival & Check-In (30 min)

**Morning Ritual:**
- Light breakfast available
- Grounding exercise
- Check-in: One insight from yesterday, one intention for today
- Q&A about homework

---

### 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM | Session 5: Attachment & Vulnerability (90 min)

**Theme:** Deepening connection through secure attachment

**Content:**

**1. Attachment Needs in Adult Relationships (20 min)**
- Lecture: What we really need from partners
- Accessibility, responsiveness, engagement (ARE)
- The fears beneath the fights

**2. The Vulnerable Share (50 min)**
- Structured exercise:
  - Partner A shares a fear or longing
  - Partner B: Listen without fixing, track own nervous system
  - Partner B responds with validation and care
  - Switch roles
- Facilitator coaches couples through activation
- Group process: What was hard? What felt good?

**3. Creating Secure Moments (20 min)**
- What is a "bonding event"?
- Small moments of connection
- Practice: Creating one secure moment together

**Break:** 11:00 AM - 11:15 AM (15 min)

---

### 11:15 AM - 12:45 PM | Session 6: Somatic Practices for Couples (90 min)

**Theme:** Embodied connection

**Content:**

**1. Introduction to Somatic Couples Work (15 min)**
- Why the body matters in relationship
- Beyond talk therapy
- Safety and consent

**2. The Body Dialogue (40 min)**
- Partner A: Notice body sensation, share with words
- Partner B: Witness, offer touch (if requested and consented)
- Switch roles
- Options: Hand on heart, back-to-back breathing, seated embrace
- Emphasis: Choice, consent, safety

**3. Movement Practices (20 min)**
- Swaying together
- Mirroring movement
- Walking together in sync
- Finding rhythm as a couple

**4. Grounding Together (15 min)**
- Practice for daily life
- Creating a shared grounding ritual
- Resources for home practice

**Lunch:** 12:45 PM - 1:45 PM (60 min)
- Catered lunch
- Couples process morning
- Informal connection

---

### 1:45 PM - 3:15 PM | Session 7: Building Your Co-Regulation Toolkit (90 min)

**Theme:** Creating sustainable practices

**Content:**

**1. Review & Integration (20 min)**
- What have you learned about yourselves?
- What have you learned about each other?
- What has shifted?

**2. The Co-Regulation Toolkit Workshop (50 min)**
- Couples create personalized toolkit:
  - Early warning signs for each partner
  - Shared signals for "I need regulation"
  - Go-to co-regulation techniques
  - Repair scripts that work for us
  - Daily connection rituals
  - Weekly check-in structure
- Worksheet: Our Co-Regulation Plan
- Facilitators circulate to support

**3. Troubleshooting (20 min)**
- Common obstacles
- When one partner is more regulated than the other
- What to do when techniques don't work
- When to seek additional support
- Group Q&A

**Break:** 3:15 PM - 3:30 PM (15 min)

---

**1. Couple Appreciation Ritual (15 min)**
- Partners face each other

- "What I'm committed to..."

**2. Group Closing (10 min)**
- Each couple shares one commitment

- Take-home kits distributed

## Facilitator Guide

### Facilitator Requirements
- Licensed couples therapist
- Training in EFT, somatic approaches, or polyvagal-informed therapy
- Experience with group work
- Comfort with experiential/expressive modalities

### Co-Facilitation Recommended
- One facilitator focuses on content
- One facilitator tracks group process
- Gender balance if possible
- Facilitators model co-regulation

### Safety Considerations
- Screen for domestic violence in intake
- Have safety plan if needed
- Clear consent for touch exercises
- Options for all exercises (no forced participation)
- Breakout space available
- Facilitators available during breaks

### Managing Intensity
- Couples work is activating
- Expect strong emotions
- Normalize activation
- Have grounding tools ready
- Know when to pause
- Individual support available if needed

### Follow-Up
- 1-week check-in email
- 1-month follow-up survey
- Offer to schedule individual sessions if needed
- Invitation to advanced couples group
- Resource referrals
