# Attachment Repair
## A 12-Week Closed Group Therapy Curriculum

**Fern & Feather Wellness Center**
*Where Biology Meets Being Human*

---

## Program Overview

**Format:** 12-week closed group (same members throughout)  
**Group Size:** 6-8 participants  
**Session Length:** 90 minutes weekly  
**Price:** $1,200 per participant ($100/session)  
**Target Participants:** Adults with attachment wounds, relationship difficulties, or patterns of insecure attachment seeking to develop earned secure attachment

**Prerequisites:**
- Individual intake session with facilitator
- Commitment to attend 10+ sessions
- Willingness to engage in experiential work
- Not in acute crisis

**Learning Outcomes:**
By the end of this program, participants will:
- Understand their attachment style and its origins
- Recognize attachment patterns in relationships
- Develop earned secure attachment capabilities
- Practice healthy communication and boundaries
- Build capacity for emotional intimacy
- Create a personalized "attachment repair roadmap"

---

## Theoretical Framework

### Core Models
1. **Attachment Theory** (Bowlby, Ainsworth)
   - Secure, anxious, avoidant, disorganized patterns
   - Internal working models
   - Attachment behavioral system

2. **Internal Family Systems (IFS)**
   - Parts carrying attachment wounds
   - Self-energy as healing force
   - Unburdening processes

3. **Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)**
   - Attachment needs in adult relationships
   - Negative interaction cycles
   - Creating secure bonding events

4. **Polyvagal-Informed Approaches**
   - Neuroception of safety
   - Co-regulation vs. self-regulation
   - Social engagement system

5. **Interpersonal Neurobiology** (Siegel)
   - Mindsight and reflective functioning
   - Integration of differentiated parts
   - Earned secure attachment through coherent narrative

### Research Foundation
- 2024 meta-analysis: Group therapy for attachment issues (moderate to large effect sizes)
- 2024 scoping review: Earned secure attachment interventions
- 2023 study: IFS groups for attachment repair
- 2025 research: Polyvagal-informed group work outcomes

## Week 1: Foundations & Group Formation

### Theme: Creating Safety & Understanding Attachment

**Learning Objectives:**
- Establish group safety and norms
- Introduce attachment theory basics
- Participants identify their attachment patterns
- Set intentions for the 12-week journey

**Session Structure (90 min):**

**Opening Circle (20 min)**
- Welcome and grounding exercise (5 min)
- Introductions: Name, one word describing why you're here (15 min)
  - Facilitator models vulnerability first
  - Emphasize no pressure to share deeply yet

**Psychoeducation: Attachment 101 (25 min)**
- Brief lecture with visual diagram:
  - The four attachment styles (secure, anxious, avoidant, disorganized)
  - How attachment forms in childhood
  - Attachment in adult relationships
  - The concept of "earned secure attachment"
- Video clip: "The Strange Situation" (3 min excerpt)
- Q&A

**Experiential: The Attachment Interview (30 min)**
- Dyad exercise (pairs):
  - "Tell me about your childhood relationship with your mother/father"
  - Listener practices reflective listening only
  - Switch roles
  - Large group: What did you notice? (patterns, emotions, body sensations)
- Facilitator notes observations about coherence, emotion regulation

**Group Norms & Container (10 min)**
- Co-create group agreements:
  - Confidentiality
  - No advice-giving (unless asked)
  - Right to pass
  - Show up authentically
  - Respect boundaries
- Discuss: What do you need to feel safe here?

**Closing (5 min)**
- One-word check-out: How are you leaving today?
- Homework preview

**Homework:**
- Complete "My Attachment History" worksheet
- Read: "Attached" by Amir Levine (Chapters 1-3)
- Daily practice: Notice one attachment moment in your relationships

**Facilitator Notes:**
- Watch for participants who may be too activated by attachment discussion
- Note anyone who might need individual support
- Begin tracking each member's attachment style hypothesis

## Week 2: Mapping Your Attachment Style

### Theme: Self-Assessment & Pattern Recognition

**Learning Objectives:**
- Complete validated attachment assessments
- Identify personal attachment triggers
- Map current relationship patterns
- Begin connecting past to present

**Session Structure:**

**Opening Check-In (15 min)**
- Grounding: Body scan (3 min)
- Round-robin: One insight from homework

**Assessment & Scoring (30 min)**
- Complete in session:
  - Experiences in Close Relationships-Revised (ECR-R)
  - Relationship Structures Questionnaire
  - Attachment Style Interview (brief version)
- Score together as group learning activity
- Facilitator emphasizes: These are patterns, not life sentences

**Small Group Exercise: Trigger Mapping (30 min)**
- Break into triads (10 min each):
  - "What activates your attachment system?"
  - "What do you do when activated?"
  - "What do you need from others?"
- Large group share: Common themes

**Psychoeducation: The Neurobiology of Attachment (10 min)**
- Brief: How attachment patterns are wired in the nervous system
- The good news: Neuroplasticity and earned security

**Closing (5 min)**
- Homework: Track one attachment activation this week

**Homework:**
- Complete "My Attachment Triggers" worksheet
- Journal: A recent relationship conflict through attachment lens
- Practice: Name your attachment need in the moment (even to yourself)

## Week 3: The Anxious Attachment Experience

### Theme: Understanding Preoccupation & Pursuit

**Learning Objectives:**
- Deep dive into anxious attachment
- Understand the pursuit cycle
- Practice self-soothing for anxious activation
- Build tolerance for uncertainty

**Session Structure:**

**Opening (10 min)**
- Grounding: Breath practice for anxiety
- Check-in: Who relates to anxious attachment? (hand raise, no pressure to share)

**Psychoeducation: Anxious Attachment (20 min)**
- Core fears: Abandonment, not being enough
- Behaviors: Clinging, seeking reassurance, hypervigilance
- The neuroscience: Heightened amygdala response to separation
- Common patterns in relationships

**Experiential: The Empty Chair (35 min)**
- Individual work in group setting:
  - "Speak to someone you're anxious about"
  - Express fears, needs, longings
  - Facilitator guides: "What do you really need to hear?"
  - Switch: Respond as the Self/soothing inner voice
- 3-4 volunteers, others witness
- Group reflection: What did you recognize?

**Skill Building: Self-Soothing for Anxious Attachment (20 min)**
- Techniques:
  - Grounding when activated
  - Self-reassurance scripts
  - Delaying responses (the 24-hour rule)
  - Reaching out vs. reaching in
- Practice in pairs

**Closing (5 min)**
- One thing you're taking away

**Homework:**
- Track anxious moments: What triggered? What did you do? What helped?
- Practice self-soothing technique daily
- Read: "Attached" Chapters 4-5

---

## Week 4: The Avoidant Attachment Experience

### Theme: Understanding Deactivation & Distance

**Learning Objectives:**
- Deep dive into avoidant attachment
- Understand the deactivation cycle
- Practice staying connected under stress
- Build tolerance for intimacy

**Session Structure:**

**Opening (10 min)**
- Grounding: Orienting to space
- Check-in: Who relates to avoidant attachment?

**Psychoeducation: Avoidant Attachment (20 min)**
- Core fears: Loss of self, engulfment, dependency
- Behaviors: Withdrawing, intellectualizing, self-reliance
- The neuroscience: Suppressed attachment system, deactivation
- Common patterns in relationships

**Experiential: The Wall (35 min)**
- Group sculpture exercise:
  - One volunteer represents "avoidant part"
  - Others place themselves in relationship to this part
  - Explore: What is the wall protecting?
  - What's behind the wall?
  - Switch: Different volunteers
- Discussion: What did we learn about protection vs. connection?

**Skill Building: Staying Present (20 min)**
- Techniques:
  - Noticing deactivation as it happens
  - Micro-moments of connection
  - Naming the fear without acting on it
  - Gradual vulnerability experiments
- Practice in dyads

**Closing (5 min)**
- Check-out

**Homework:**
- Track avoidant moments: When did you want to pull away? What happened?
- Practice staying present in one uncomfortable interaction
- Read: "Attached" Chapters 6-7

## Week 5: The Dance of Anxious-Avoidant Relationships

### Theme: Understanding the Pursuit-Distance Cycle

**Learning Objectives:**
- Map the anxious-avoidant trap
- Identify your role in relationship cycles
- Understand protest behaviors vs. true needs
- Begin interrupting the cycle

**Session Structure:**

**Opening (10 min)**
- Grounding
- Check-in: Who has been in this dynamic?

**Psychoeducation: The Cycle (25 min)**
- Visual diagram: The pursuer-distancer dance
- How each person's behavior triggers the other
- The escalation pattern
- Why it's so hard to break

**Experiential: The Dance (40 min)**
- Role-play in pairs (anxious + avoidant):
  - Scene: One person wants more connection, other needs space
  - Play it out "automatically" (10 min)
  - Debrief: What did you notice?
  - Replay: Try something different (10 min)
  - Switch roles and repeat
- Large group: What shifted? What was hard?

**Skill Building: Naming the Cycle (10 min)**
- "We're in the cycle"
- Taking responsibility without blame
- Calling a timeout

**Closing (5 min)**

**Homework:**
- Identify one relationship where this cycle happens
- Journal: My part in the dance
- Practice: Name the cycle in the moment (if safe to do so)

## Week 6: IFS & Attachment Parts

### Theme: Working with Internal Attachment Systems

**Learning Objectives:**
- Introduce Internal Family Systems model
- Identify attachment-related parts
- Begin relationship with protective parts
- Access Self-energy

**Session Structure:**

**Opening (10 min)**
- Grounding: Arriving in Self
- Check-in

**Psychoeducation: IFS & Attachment (25 min)**
- Parts that hold attachment wounds (exiles)
- Protective parts (managers and firefighters)
- The Self: Core qualities (8 C's)
- How parts create attachment patterns

**Experiential: Meeting a Part (40 min)**
- Guided IFS meditation:
  - Find a part related to attachment
  - Ask: What are you protecting?
  - What do you need?
  - Can Self offer compassion?
- Journaling: Dialogue with the part
- Optional sharing

**Group Process (10 min)**
- What did you discover?
- Common themes in parts

**Closing (5 min)**

**Homework:**
- Daily: Check in with your parts
- Journal: A conversation with an attachment part
- Notice when parts are running the show

## Week 7: Disorganized Attachment & Trauma

### Theme: When Attachment Itself Feels Unsafe

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand disorganized attachment
- Recognize trauma responses in relationships
- Practice stabilization before exploration
- Know when individual therapy is needed

**Session Structure:**

**Opening (15 min)**
- Extended grounding (trauma-sensitive)
- Check-in: Optional sharing

**Psychoeducation: Disorganized Attachment (25 min)**
- The "fear without solution"
- Caregiver as source of both comfort and fear
- Impact on adult relationships
- The window of tolerance
- **Important:** When group isn't enough (referral criteria)

**Experiential: Stabilization Practice (30 min)**
- Resource installation:
  - Find a place in body that feels okay
  - Anchor positive memories
  - Create internal safe space
- Group discussion: What resources do you have?

**Skill Building: Window of Tolerance (15 min)**
- Tracking your arousal
- Grounding techniques review
- When to reach out vs. self-regulate

**Closing (5 min)**
- Gentle check-out

**Homework:**
- Practice stabilization daily
- Notice: When do you feel "fear without solution"?
- Self-care focus this week

**Facilitator Note:**
- This session may be activating for some
- Be prepared to offer individual support
- Have referral resources ready

## Week 8: Communication & Repair

### Theme: Building Secure Base Through Dialogue

**Learning Objectives:**
- Practice non-violent communication
- Learn repair conversations
- Express needs directly
- Receive feedback without defensiveness

**Session Structure:**

**Opening (10 min)**
- Grounding
- Check-in

**Psychoeducation: Secure Communication (20 min)**
- The neuroscience of feeling heard
- Non-violent communication framework
- The importance of repair
- Examples of secure vs. insecure communication

**Experiential: The Repair Conversation (50 min)**
- Dyad exercise:
  - Person A: Share a grievance (real or hypothetical)
  - Person B: Practice active listening, validation
  - Person B: Offer repair
  - Person A: Receive repair
  - Switch roles
- Debrief: What was hard? What felt good?

**Group Practice (5 min)**
- Common repair scripts

**Closing (5 min)**

**Homework:**
- Attempt one repair conversation in your life
- Practice NVC formula: Observation, Feeling, Need, Request
- Notice: How does it feel to be heard?

## Week 9: Boundaries & Autonomy

### Theme: Secure Attachment Includes Separateness

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand boundaries in secure attachment
- Differentiate walls from boundaries
- Practice saying no
- Tolerate others' boundaries

**Session Structure:**

**Opening (10 min)**
- Grounding
- Check-in

**Psychoeducation: Boundaries (20 min)**
- Boundaries as part of secure attachment
- Types: Physical, emotional, time, energy
- Boundaries vs. walls (avoidance)
- The right to autonomy

**Experiential: Boundary Practice (50 min)**
- Role-plays in triads:
  - Scene 1: Saying no to someone you care about
  - Scene 2: Hearing no without collapsing or attacking
  - Scene 3: Negotiating a compromise
- Rotating roles
- Debrief: What did you learn about yourself?

**Skill Building: Boundary Scripts (5 min)**
- "I need..."
- "I'm not available for..."
- "That doesn't work for me..."

**Closing (5 min)**

**Homework:**
- Set one boundary this week
- Notice your response to others' boundaries
- Journal: What makes boundaries hard for me?

## Week 10: Intimacy & Vulnerability

### Theme: The Rewards of Secure Connection

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand healthy intimacy
- Practice vulnerability in safe ways
- Build tolerance for being seen
- Experience secure attachment in group

**Session Structure:**

**Opening (10 min)**
- Grounding
- Check-in: One thing you're proud of from this group

**Psychoeducation: Intimacy (15 min)**
- Intimacy = Into-me-you-see
- The neuroscience of secure bonding
- Vulnerability as strength
- How secure attachment creates safety for risk

**Experiential: The Vulnerability Circle (60 min)**
- Each person shares:
  - "Something I've never told anyone..." OR
  - "What I really need in relationships..." OR
  - "My deepest fear about connection..."
- Group responds with:
  - Validation
  - Reflection (not advice)
  - Appreciation
- Facilitator holds container tightly

**Closing (5 min)**

**Homework:**
- Practice vulnerability with one safe person
- Notice: What happens after you risk?

**Facilitator Note:**
- This is a high-intimacy session
- Ensure group safety before proceeding
- Have extra time if needed

## Week 12: Closing & Celebration

### Theme: Honoring the Journey

**Learning Objectives:**
- Process endings and goodbyes
- Celebrate growth
- Solidify group bonds
- Launch into earned secure future

**Session Structure:**

**Opening (10 min)**
- Grounding
- Check-in: One word for how you arrived today

**Ritual: The Witnessing Circle (50 min)**
- Each person sits in center
- Group offers:
  - "I saw you..." (observations of growth)
  - "I appreciate..." (what they brought to group)
  - "I hope for you..." (wishes for future)
- Person in center: Only receives, no responding
- Facilitator ensures balance and authenticity
- This is profound attachment repair in action

**Closing Ritual (25 min)**
- Read: "The Invitation" by Oriah Mountain Dreamer (excerpt)
- Each person shares:
  - What you're taking with you
  - What you're leaving behind
  - One commitment to yourself
- Group poem or reading
- Final grounding together

**Logistics (5 min)**
- Alumni group information
- Individual therapy referrals if needed
- Evaluation forms
- Contact information exchange (optional)

**Closing (optional extension)**
- Informal gathering after (if in-person)
- Virtual "toast" (if online)

## Facilitator Guide

### Required Training
- Licensed mental health professional
- Group therapy training
- Attachment theory certification (preferred)
- IFS Level 1 (preferred) or equivalent parts work training
- Trauma-informed care training

### Group Facilitation Skills
- Holding container for intense emotions
- Managing group dynamics
- Balancing structure and spontaneity
- Recognizing when individual attention is needed
- Self-awareness of own attachment patterns

### Crisis Protocol
- Individual safety plan for each member
- Emergency contact information
- Referral network for individual therapy
- Clear boundaries about between-session contact
- Plan for acute activation during session

### Common Challenges

**Dropouts:**
- Normal: 1-2 people may drop
- Preventive: Strong intake, clear commitment
- If someone drops: Process with group, maintain confidentiality

**Conflict:**
- Attachment patterns will play out in group
- Use as grist for the mill
- Model repair in real-time

**Over-activation:**
- Some sessions (Week 7, Week 10) may be intense
- Have grounding tools ready
- Be willing to adjust pace

**Facilitator Countertransference:**
- Your own attachment stuff will get triggered
- Have your own supervision/therapy
- Know your limits
