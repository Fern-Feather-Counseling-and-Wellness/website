## Executive Summary

### The Vision

Fern & Feather Wellness Center is a justice-oriented, trauma-informed integrative wellness practice combining clinical counseling, somatic therapy, and evidence-based wellness services. Founded by two APCs (Associate Professional Counselors) pending LPC licensure, the practice fills a gap in the Alpharetta/North Atlanta market for high-quality, private-pay trauma care that honors both the science and the systems that shape mental health.

### Mission Statement

To provide LGBTQ+ affirming, trauma-informed clinical care and wellness services that recognize the biological basis of trauma while acknowledging the systemic forces that create and compound it. We believe healing happens at the intersection of science, justice, and embodied practice.

### The "Biology Meets Being Human" Positioning

Nicole's PhD in Biology (Cell/Molecular) combined with her Masters in Clinical Counseling creates a unique value proposition: a therapist who understands the neuroscience of trauma on a cellular level, can read primary research, and won't sugar-coat the science OR the systems. This differentiates Fern & Feather from both "woo-woo" wellness practices and purely clinical settings that ignore systemic context.

## The Problem We're Solving

### For Clients:

1. **The "Woo vs. Science" False Dichotomy**
   - Most trauma therapy is either purely medical/clinical (ignores body/somatic work) OR purely alternative (lacks scientific rigor)
   - Clients with trauma need both: evidence-based interventions AND embodied, holistic approaches

2. **Wait Times and Access**
   - Quality trauma-informed care in North Atlanta often has months-long waitlists
   - Insurance-driven practices rush sessions (45 min) that aren't suitable for trauma processing

3. **Therapist Burnout Affects Care**
   - Overloaded therapists (25+ clients/week) can't provide deep, somatic, trauma-specific work
   - High turnover, inconsistent care, therapist vacations create ruptures

4. **Systemic Invisibility**
   - LGBTQ+ clients, BIPOC clients, and marginalized populations often have to educate their therapists OR settle for "tolerant" rather than "affirming" care
   - Medical trauma, racial trauma, and systemic harm are routinely minimized

### For the Market:

- **Underserved geography:** Alpharetta/North Fulton lacks high-end, trauma-specific private practices
- **Growing demand:** Post-pandemic trauma awareness + Georgia's population growth
- **Competitive gap:** No one combining PhD-level biological literacy with clinical trauma work

## Pricing Strategy & Positioning

### Why $175-250/session?

**Market Context:**
- Atlanta metro average: $120-180/session
- Trauma-specialist premium practices: $190-225/session (Best Within You, etc.)
- Alpharetta/North Fulton: Limited high-end options, some clients drive to Atlanta

**Our Justification:**
1. **Length:** 55-minute sessions (not 45) or 90-minute intensives
2. **Depth:** Somatic work, IFS, nervous system regulation—not just talk therapy
3. **Credentials:** PhD + Masters-level clinician with specialized training
4. **Access:** Limited caseload (12 max) = availability + consistency
5. **Value:** No insurance hassles, no notes sent to third parties, full privacy

**Positioning Statement:**
"We're not the cheapest option. We're the right option if you want trauma work with someone who understands biology, honors your lived experience, and isn't juggling 25 other clients."

## Ideal Client Profile

### Primary Avatar: "The Justice-Oriented Healer"

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28-45
- Location: Alpharetta, Milton, Roswell, Johns Creek, Cumming, or willing to drive
- Income: Household $80K+ (can afford private pay or has HSA)
- Insurance: High deductible plan, out-of-network benefits, or self-pay

**Psychographics:**
- Values evidence-based approaches BUT skeptical of purely medicalized mental health
- Politically progressive, justice-oriented, likely follows activists/therapists on social media
- Has tried therapy before (maybe multiple times) and felt misunderstood or "psychoanalyzed"
- Likely experiences: attachment wounds, complex trauma, medical trauma, religious trauma
- May be: LGBTQ+, neurodivergent, chronically ill/disabled, or part of marginalized communities
- **Active on TikTok** — finds therapists there, skeptical of "therapy speak," wants real talk

**Pain Points:**
- Previous therapists didn't "get it" (minimized family trauma, pushed forgiveness)
- Feels like a burden in 45-minute sessions when they're just getting started
- Frustrated by the medical model (diagnosis-focused, medication-first)
- Wants somatic/body-based work but hasn't found practitioners who also do "real" therapy

**What They Need to Hear:**
- "Your trauma is not your fault"
- "The science supports this"
- "I won't rush you"
- "You don't have to educate me about [identity/systemic issue]"

### Secondary Avatars

**The Couples in Crisis:**
- High-conflict couples where one/both have trauma histories
- Mixed-neurotype couples (one ADHD/autistic, one neurotypical)
- LGBTQ+ couples dealing with family rejection or navigating identity
- Price point: Willing to pay premium for expertise in trauma-informed couples work

**The Chronic Overachiever:**
- Professionals (tech, healthcare, academia) who look "fine" but are barely holding on
- High-functioning anxiety, perfectionism, imposter syndrome
- Previous therapy felt like chatting with a friend—not deep enough
- Needs: someone who will challenge them, not just validate

## Operational Model

### Session Structure

**Standard Individual Session (55 min):**
- Check-in (5 min)
- Somatic grounding/practice (5-10 min)
- Therapeutic work (30-35 min)
- Integration/closing (5 min)
- Documentation (outside session time)

**Intensive Session (90 min):**
- For: EMDR, IFS deep dives, couples sessions, trauma processing
- Full somatic container + processing time
- Scheduled weekly or biweekly (not back-to-back)

### Caseload Management

**Nicole's Capacity:**
- Maximum 12 individual clients/week
- Mix: 8 standard sessions + 2 intensives + 2 couples
- OR: 6 standard + 3 intensives + 3 groups
- Groups: 1/week (90 min, 6-8 people)

**Scheduling Philosophy:**
- No back-to-back sessions (somatic work requires recovery)
- 15-min buffer between clients
- No sessions after 6 PM (burnout prevention)
- 4-day work week (Mon-Thu clinical, Fri admin/development)

### Office Space Requirements

**Specifications:**
- Location: Alpharetta/Milton/Roswell area (within 10 min of GA-400)
- Square footage: 1,200-1,800 sq ft
- Must have:
  - 2-3 clinical offices (soundproofed)
  - Group room (can fit 10 people for yoga)
  - Waiting area
  - ADA accessibility
  - LGBTQ+ affirming neighborhood/atmosphere
  - Parking

**Timeline:**
- Begin search: Month 9-10 (after license application)
- Secure space: Month 12-14
- Build-out if needed: Month 14-16
- Open doors: Month 16-18 (March 2027 target)

## Clinical Framework

### Theoretical Orientation

**Primary Modalities:**
- Internal Family Systems (IFS) — Parts work, Self-energy
- Somatic Experiencing — Nervous system regulation, body-based trauma processing
- CBT — Cognitive restructuring, practical skills
- EMDR — Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (future certification)
- Attachment Theory — Relationship patterns, developmental trauma

**Integrative Approach:**
- Biology-informed (polyvagal theory, neuroplasticity, epigenetics)
- Justice-oriented (anti-oppressive, systemic awareness)
- Trauma-informed (safety, trust, collaboration, empowerment)
- Somatic (body as source of wisdom, regulation, processing)

### Specialized Populations

**We Welcome:**
- LGBTQ+ individuals and couples
- Trauma survivors (complex, single-incident, developmental, medical)
- Neurodivergent clients (ADHD, autism, with trauma-informed lens)
- Healthcare workers, activists, therapists (vicarious trauma)
- Parents of neurodiverse children (nervous system regulation focus)

**We Refer Out:**
- Active eating disorders requiring higher level of care
- Active substance use disorders (not our specialty)
- Court-ordered therapy
- Crisis/safety issues requiring 24/7 support

## Brand Voice & Messaging Guidelines

### Tone Attributes

1. **Sharp, Not Mean** — Witty, sarcastic observations about therapy culture, but never at client's expense
2. **Scientifically Literate** — Can cite research, but explains it like a human
3. **Justice-Oriented** — Names systems, doesn't blame individuals
4. **Embodied** — Talks about bodies, nervous systems, somatic experience without being woo-woo
5. **Warmly Boundaried** — Professional but not clinical, personal but not oversharing

### Messaging Do's and Don'ts

✅ **DO:**
- Say "your trauma is not your fault"
- Acknowledge systemic factors (racism, capitalism, medical trauma)
- Explain the biology (polyvagal, attachment, neuroplasticity)
- Be funny about therapy culture, not people's pain
- Use "we" when talking about the work, "I" when sharing expertise

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use therapy jargon without explanation
- Promise cures or quick fixes
- Make medical claims
- Be saccharine or "good vibes only"
- Center the founder's story over the client's needs
