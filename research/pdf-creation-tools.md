# PDF & Digital Product Creation Tools

*Compiled March 8, 2026 — Ronda's capability research*

---

## For Workbooks & Lead Magnets

### **Canva** ⭐ (Top Pick for Beginners)
- **Best for:** Quick, beautiful workbooks and PDFs
- **Pros:** Drag-and-drop, templates, brand kit, easy to learn
- **Cons:** Less control over precise typography
- **Cost:** Free tier sufficient; Pro ~$13/month for brand features
- **Use case:** Lead magnets, simple workbooks, social graphics

### **Adobe InDesign** (Professional Option)
- **Best for:** Complex, multi-page workbooks with precise design
- **Pros:** Total control, professional output, industry standard
- **Cons:** Steep learning curve, subscription cost
- **Cost:** ~$23/month (Adobe Creative Cloud)
- **Use case:** 85,000+ word comprehensive workbook like "The Anxious Nervous System"

### **Google Docs + Export**
- **Best for:** Quick drafts, text-heavy content
- **Pros:** Free, collaborative, familiar
- **Cons:** Limited design control
- **Use case:** Drafting content before design phase

---

## For eBooks & Digital Courses

### **Designrr**
- **Best for:** Repurposing content into ebooks
- **Pros:** Import from blog posts, auto-formatting
- **Cost:** ~$29/month
- **Use case:** Turning blog content into lead magnets

### **Vellum**
- **Best for:** Polished EPUB exports
- **Pros:** Beautiful formatting, especially for fiction
- **Cons:** Mac only
- **Cost:** ~$250 one-time
- **Use case:** Professional ebook publishing

### **Kindle Create**
- **Best for:** Amazon KDP publishing
- **Pros:** Free, Amazon-optimized
- **Use case:** Publishing on Amazon Kindle store

## Workflow Recommendation

1. **Draft in Google Docs** (content creation)
2. **Design in Canva** (visual layout)
3. **Export as PDF** (delivery format)
4. **Sell via:** Gumroad, Podia, or own website
