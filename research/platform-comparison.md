## Detailed Platform Analysis

### 🥇 PAYHIP - Best for Starting Out

**Pricing:**
- Free plan: 5% transaction fee only
- Plus plan: $29/month, 2% transaction fee
- Pro plan: $99/month, 0% transaction fee

**Pros:**
- ✅ No monthly fee to start (perfect for testing)
- ✅ Built-in email marketing (newsletters, abandoned cart)
- ✅ PDF stamping (protects your workbooks from piracy)
- ✅ Simple setup (literally 30 minutes)
- ✅ Clean, professional checkout
- ✅ Handles VAT/tax automatically
- ✅ Good for EU customers (GDPR compliant)

**Cons:**
- ❌ Less customizable than Shopify
- ❌ No blog/content marketing features
- ❌ Smaller app ecosystem

**Best For:**
- Testing products before committing to monthly fees
- Therapists who want simplicity over complexity
- Starting with low upfront costs

**Therapy-Specific Features:**
- PDF stamping protects your intellectual property
- Can sell coaching/session bookings alongside products
- Clean, professional appearance (important for credibility)

---

### 🥈 GUMROAD - Best for Discoverability

**Pricing:**
- Free plan: 10% transaction fee
- Creator plan: $9/month + lower fees
- Premium features for higher volume

**Pros:**
- ✅ Gumroad Discover (marketplace brings you customers)
- ✅ Excellent for creators building audience
- ✅ Simple email marketing built-in
- ✅ Membership/subscription features
- ✅ Very easy to use
- ✅ Handles all tax compliance

**Cons:**
- ❌ HIGH fees (10% on free plan)
- ❌ Limited customization
- ❌ You don't own customer emails as easily

**Best For:**
- Creators who need marketplace exposure
- Building an audience from scratch
- Those who prioritize discoverability over cost

**The Math Problem:**
On a $47 workbook:
- Gumroad: $4.70 fee (10%)
- Payhip: $2.35 fee (5%)
- **You lose $2.35 more per sale on Gumroad**

At 100 sales/month: **$235/month more in fees**

**Verdict:** Only worth it if Gumroad Discover brings you significant traffic.

---

### 🥉 SHOPIFY - Best for Scaling

**Pricing:**
- Basic: $29/month + 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction
- Shopify: $79/month (lower rates)
- Advanced: $299/month

**Pros:**
- ✅ Full e-commerce platform
- ✅ Excellent marketing tools (abandoned cart, email, SEO)
- ✅ Huge app ecosystem (integrate anything)
- ✅ Professional, customizable storefront
- ✅ Best for long-term scaling
- ✅ Lower transaction fees than Gumroad/Payhip at volume
- ✅ Own your customer data completely

**Cons:**
- ❌ Monthly fee even with zero sales
- ❌ More complex setup
- ❌ Overkill for just selling a few workbooks
- ❌ Need to configure tax compliance

**Break-Even Analysis:**
- Shopify costs $29/month
- Saves you ~2-5% in fees vs. Payhip/Gumroad
- **You need ~$1,000/month in sales for Shopify to make sense financially**

**Best For:**
- Established businesses with consistent sales
- Those wanting full control and customization
- Scaling to multiple products, courses, memberships

**Therapy Business Path:**
Start with Payhip → Move to Shopify when you hit $1K/month consistently

### 4. PODIA - Best for Courses + Downloads

**Pricing:**
- Mover: $39/month (downloads, courses)
- Shaker: $89/month (adds webinars, affiliate)
- Earthquaker: $199/month (adds team)

**Pros:**
- ✅ Beautiful, simple interface
- ✅ Great for courses + digital downloads together
- ✅ Built-in email marketing (generous limits)
- ✅ Community features
- ✅ No transaction fees
- ✅ Good customer support

**Cons:**
- ❌ Higher monthly cost
- ❌ No marketplace/discoverability
- ❌ Limited customization

**Best For:**
- Those planning courses + workbooks together
- Coaches who want community features
- Those who hate technical setup

**Verdict:** Great platform but overkill if you're just selling PDF workbooks initially.

---

### 5. ETSY - Best for Marketplace Traffic

**Pricing:**
- Free to list
- $0.20 per listing
- 6.5% transaction fee
- 3% + 25¢ payment processing
- **Total: ~9.5% per sale**

**Pros:**
- ✅ Built-in traffic (millions of buyers)
- ✅ People search Etsy for workbooks/planners
- ✅ Easy setup
- ✅ Established trust
- ✅ Good for SEO

**Cons:**
- ❌ High fees
- ❌ Competing on price (race to bottom)
- ❌ Limited branding control
- ❌ Etsy owns the customer relationship
- ❌ Hard to build email list

**Best For:**
- Getting initial sales while building your own platform
- Testing product-market fit
- Products that fit Etsy's audience (planners, journals)

**Strategy:**
Use Etsy for discovery, but drive traffic to your own platform (Payhip/Shopify) for repeat sales.

---

### 6. YOUR WEBSITE (WordPress + Easy Digital Downloads)

**Pricing:**
- Hosting: $5-20/month
- Easy Digital Downloads: Free (or $99/year for pro)
- Payment processing: 2.9% + 30¢
- **Total: ~$10-30/month + standard processing**

**Pros:**
- ✅ Lowest long-term costs
- ✅ Full control over everything
- ✅ Own all customer data
- ✅ No platform risk
- ✅ Professional (yourname.com)

**Cons:**
- ❌ Technical setup required
- ❌ You handle security, updates, backups
- ❌ No built-in traffic
- ❌ Need to configure tax compliance

**Best For:**
- Tech-savvy therapists
- Those wanting maximum control
- Long-term cost savings

**Recommendation:**
Only go this route if you're comfortable with WordPress or have someone to help with tech.

## My Specific Recommendations for Fern & Feather

### PHASE 1: Launch & Test (Months 1-3)
**Platform: Payhip (Free Plan)**

**Why:**
- Zero upfront cost = lower risk
- Built-in email marketing (crucial for nurture sequences)
- PDF stamping protects your workbooks
- Simple enough that you won't get stuck on tech
- Can migrate to Shopify later without losing customers

**Setup:**
1. Create Payhip account (free)
2. Upload "The Embodied Parts Workbook" PDF
3. Set price at $47
4. Connect your domain (fernandfeather.com/products)
5. Set up email capture for lead magnet

**Marketing Strategy:**
- TikTok → Lead magnet (Payhip) → Email nurture → Product (Payhip)
- All-in-one platform keeps it simple

---

### PHASE 2: Growth ($1K+/month consistent)
**Platform: Shopify Basic ($29/month)**

**Why:**
- Lower fees at volume (you'll save money)
- Better SEO (blog integration)
- More professional appearance
- Better email marketing (Klaviyo integration)
- Can handle courses, memberships, physical products later

**Migration:**
- Export customers from Payhip
- Import to Shopify
- Set up 301 redirects
- Most customers won't even notice

---

### PHASE 3: Multi-Channel (Optional)
**Primary: Shopify**  
**Secondary: Etsy** (for discovery)

**Strategy:**
- List products on Etsy at slightly higher price (to cover fees)
- Drive Etsy traffic to your email list
- Main sales happen on your Shopify store
- Etsy = discovery, Shopify = retention
