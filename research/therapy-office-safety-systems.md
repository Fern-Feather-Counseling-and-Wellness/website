## The Problem

Therapists (especially women) working alone in private practice face unique safety risks:
- One-on-one sessions with potentially volatile clients
- Isolated office locations
- Limited ability to call for help without escalating situations
- Need for **discrete** activation (client shouldn't know help is being summoned)

## Option 1: Flic Smart Buttons (RECOMMENDED - Budget)

### What It Is
Small wireless smart buttons that can be programmed to send alerts via Bluetooth/WiFi.

### Pricing
- **Single button:** $35
- **3-pack:** $79.99 (~$27 each)
- **6-pack:** $129.99 (~$22 each) ← **Best value**
- **Hub required:** ~$50 (one-time)

### How It Works for Therapy Office
1. Place button **under desk** or **on side table** within easy reach
2. Program to send text/email to:
   - Other clinicians in the building
   - Front desk/admin
   - Emergency contact
3. **Silent activation** — no noise, no lights
4. Can also trigger phone call or notification

### Pros
- ✅ Very affordable (~$22/button)
- ✅ Completely silent/discrete
- ✅ No monthly fees (after initial purchase)
- ✅ Small, portable, can move between rooms
- ✅ Customizable (one press = text, double press = call, hold = 911)
- ✅ Works with existing WiFi

### Cons
- ❌ Limited range (Bluetooth ~150ft)
- ❌ Requires hub for full functionality
- ❌ No GPS tracking
- ❌ No direct 911 connection
- ❌ Battery-powered (needs occasional replacement)

### Best Use Case
Small practice with multiple rooms where alerts go to a central person (receptionist, other clinician, or admin).

## Option 2: Silent Beacon (RECOMMENDED - Individual Safety)

### What It Is
Wearable panic button with GPS, 2-way audio, and multiple alert modes.

### Pricing
- **Device:** ~$99-129
- **Monthly service:** $15-25/month
- **No contract options available**

### How It Works
1. Wear as pendant, clip to clothing, or keep in pocket
2. **Silent mode** — press button, device vibrates to confirm alert sent
3. Alerts go to:
   - Pre-set emergency contacts
   - 911 (optional)
   - Monitoring center
4. **2-way audio** — responders can hear what's happening
5. **GPS location** — responders know exactly where you are

### Pros
- ✅ Wearable (always with you)
- ✅ GPS tracking
- ✅ 2-way audio (responders can assess situation)
- ✅ Silent mode (client doesn't know)
- ✅ Works anywhere (not just office)
- ✅ No WiFi needed (cellular)
- ✅ Specifically marketed for healthcare/therapists

### Cons
- ❌ Monthly subscription required
- ❌ More expensive long-term
- ❌ Visible (if worn as pendant)
- ❌ Needs charging

### Best Use Case
Individual clinicians who want personal protection that travels with them — especially if seeing clients in different locations or doing home visits.

## Additional Safety Measures (Beyond Panic Buttons)

### Office Design
- **Position furniture** so you're closest to the exit
- **Remove heavy objects** that could be used as weapons
- **Ensure clear sightlines** to door
- **Install a door chime** so you know when someone enters

### Protocols
- **Safety screening** during initial consultation (history of violence, substance use)
- **Check-in system** — text buddy when session starts/ends
- **De-escalation training** for all clinicians
- **Clear policies** on terminating sessions for safety

### Technology
- **Video doorbell** to see who's arriving
- **Electronic locks** with remote unlock capability
- **Security cameras** in common areas (not therapy rooms)

## Recommended Setup for Fern & Feather

### Phase 1: Launch (Budget Option)
**Cost: ~$200 one-time**

1. **Flic 6-pack:** $130
   - One per therapy room (4 rooms)
   - One for reception/front desk
   - One spare
2. **Flic Hub:** $50
3. **Setup:** Program each button to text both you and Kiera when pressed

**How it works:**
- Clinician feels unsafe → presses button under desk
- You and Kiera get instant text: "ALERT: Room 2 — Check on clinician"
- Can have code word system ("Can you bring me the file?" = all clear)

### Phase 2: Enhanced Safety (Individual Protection)
**Cost: ~$100-130 per clinician + $15-25/month**

Add **Silent Beacon wearables** for clinicians who want them:
- Each clinician has personal device
- Can wear during sessions (discrete clip or pocket)
- GPS tracking if they do home visits
- Direct 911 option if needed

### Phase 3: Full System (If Scaling)
**Cost: Quote-based**

If you expand to 5+ clinicians with dedicated admin:
- Upgrade to **TeamAlert** or similar professional system
- Centralized monitoring
- Integration with building security

## Action Items

### Immediate (Pre-Launch)
1. **Order Flic 6-pack + hub** — test in your current space
2. **Create safety protocols** — screening, check-ins, de-escalation
3. **Design office layout** with safety in mind (exit access, furniture placement)

### Before Seeing Clients
4. **Program buttons** with emergency contacts
5. **Test system** — make sure alerts actually reach you
6. **Brief all clinicians** on how to use and when to use
7. **Post emergency numbers** in each room (discrete location)

### Ongoing
8. **Review safety protocols** quarterly
9. **Test panic buttons** monthly
10. **Debrief** any incidents or near-misses

---

## Key Takeaway

**You don't need an expensive system to be safe.** A $22 Flic button under the desk that texts your partner when pressed is 90% as effective as a $5,000 professional system. The most important safety measure is **having a plan** and **knowing you can get help without escalating the situation**.

Start simple. Upgrade if needed.
