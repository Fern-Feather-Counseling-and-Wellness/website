Insurance & Payment

Do you take insurance?

Nope — and that's by design, not accident.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: insurance companies exist to make money, not to support your wellbeing. When you let insurance into your therapy relationship, here's what actually happens:

You get a permanent diagnosis attached to your medical record — even if you're just going through a rough patch or working on personal growth. Insurance requires a disorder code to pay for anything. That label follows you forever.

They decide what "counts" as therapy — and spoiler: it's only crisis management, not actual thriving. Working on relationships? Understanding yourself better? Preventing burnout? Not "medically necessary" in their world.

They limit your care — some case manager who's never met you decides how many sessions you "need" based on a spreadsheet, not your actual life.

Your privacy disappears — insurance creates a permanent trail of your mental health history that future employers, insurers, and agencies can potentially access.

We refused to play that game.

By staying private-pay, your records stay actually private. You don't need a diagnostic label to get help. We decide together what you need — not some insurer looking at their bottom line.

If you have out-of-network benefits, we can provide a superbill (detailed receipt with diagnostic codes) to submit for partial reimbursement. Fair warning: submitting it means your insurance gets your diagnosis and treatment info. You're trading privacy for potential reimbursement — entirely your call, and we won't judge either way.

We also reserve sliding scale spots for those who need them. Therapy shouldn't be a luxury, but we believe that shouldn't come at the cost of selling your privacy to insurance companies.

Under the No Surprises Act, you have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate explaining how much your medical care will cost. We are happy to provide this upon request.
