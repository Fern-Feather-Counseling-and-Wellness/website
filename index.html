Common Questions

Everything you need to know about starting therapy with us.

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

If you're struggling with anxiety, trauma, relationship issues, or life transitions and your usual coping strategies aren't working, therapy can help. We offer a free 15-minute consultation to discuss your needs and see if we're a good fit. There's no pressure to commit — it's simply a chance to connect and explore.

What should I expect in the first session?

The first session is about getting to know you — your history, current challenges, and what you hope to achieve. We'll discuss our approach, answer questions, and start building a foundation for our work together. No pressure, no judgment. Just a conversation about where you are and where you'd like to go.

How long does therapy take?

It varies based on your goals and what you're working through. Some clients see significant improvement in 8-12 sessions; others prefer ongoing support over months or years. We'll check in regularly about your progress and adjust as needed. You're in the driver's seat.

Do you take insurance?

We're private pay only, which allows us to provide the highest quality, individualized care without insurance constraints. We can provide superbills for out-of-network reimbursement, and many clients use HSA/FSA funds. See our Rates page for detailed pricing.

What approaches do you use?

We're integrative therapists, which means we draw from multiple evidence-based approaches: Internal Family Systems (IFS), somatic therapy, EMDR, attachment-based therapy, CBT, and mindfulness. We'll tailor our approach to what works best for you. Learn more on our Services page.

Do you offer virtual sessions?

Yes! We offer secure, HIPAA-compliant telehealth sessions for clients throughout Georgia. Virtual therapy is just as effective as in-person for many issues, and it offers the convenience of accessing care from your own space.

What makes your practice holistic?

We believe healing happens on multiple levels — mind, body, and relationships. In addition to traditional talk therapy, we offer somatic approaches, trauma-informed yoga, and collaborate with massage therapists, nutritionists, and other wellness providers. We treat the whole person, not just the symptoms.

How do I get started?

Simply reach out via email or phone, or click the "Schedule Consult" button. We'll set up a free 15-minute consultation to discuss what you're looking for and how we can help. If we're a good fit, we'll schedule your first full session.
