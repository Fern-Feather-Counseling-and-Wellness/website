### ⚠️ SECTION 6: Biology Isn't One Cause — HIGH PRIORITY

**Current state:** Accurate but thin on specific genetic/epigenetic citations

**What needs adding:**
- **Twin study citation** — Anxiety heritability estimates (30-50% commonly cited)
- **Epigenetic research** — Glauser & Hasler (2021) is a key review I recommend locating
- **Gut-brain axis** — General consensus, but specific pathways need citations
- **Inflammation** — Valid research area, needs representative studies

**Specific papers to seek:**

1. **Twin/Heritability:**
   - Search: "anxiety disorder heritability twin study meta-analysis"
   - Look for: Taylor et al. or similar comprehensive reviews

2. **Epigenetics:**
   - Glauser, J., & Hasler, G. (2021). Epigenetics of anxiety: The complex origin of a complex disorder. Disclaimer: The original summary mentioned "Fear" and *npj Climate and Atmospheric Science*, which appears to be an error; *Psychoneuroendocrinology* or similar would be more appropriate.
   - Alternative: "Epigenetic mechanisms anxiety stress" search

3. **Gut-Brain Axis:**
   - Clemente & Ogluke (1996) was cited in military family research, may not apply
   - Better: Generic citation to "gut microbiome anxiety” review papers (there are many)

4. **Inflammation:**
   - "Inflammation anxiety biomarkers" search
   - Look for: Maes, Klinedinst, or similar researchers

**Estimated effort:** 2-3 hours

### 📋 SECTION 7: Things That Help — MODERATE PRIORITY

**Current state:** Evidence-informed practices, appropriate citations needed for specificity

**What needs strengthening:**
- **Vagal toning techniques** — Multiple studies support this; specific citations would add authority
- **Breathing techniques** — Strong evidence base; exemplary studies should be cited
- **Humming/chanting** — Vagus nerve stimulation research exists
- **Cold water** — Dive reflex research supports the mechanism
- **Gentle movement** — Somatic therapy literature, trauma-informed yoga research

**Specific search terms:**
```
"vagus nerve stimulation" anxiety
diaphragmatic breathing anxiety meta-analysis
"cold water immersion" parasympathetic activation
somatic experiencing trauma effectiveness
trauma informed yoga anxiety research
```

**Note on shaking/trembling:** The "trembling discharge" concept relates to trauma release exercises. The biology exists but "prescribed shaking" as a treatment is more alternative medicine. Consider softening the claim or finding citations on TRE (Tension & Trauma Releasing Exercises).

**Estimated effort:** 2-3 hours

---

### ⚠️ SECTION 8: Therapeutic Approaches — HIGH PRIORITY

**Current state:** Accurate descriptions of modalities, but lacks comparative effectiveness research

**Critical need:** Studies comparing effectiveness of different approaches

**What to look for:**
- Systematic reviews on anxiety treatment efficacy
- Comparative effectiveness: CBT vs ACT vs somatic approaches
- Polyvagal-informed therapy efficacy (if available — field is newer)
- Medication vs therapy comparisons
- IFS (Internal Family Systems) efficacy studies for anxiety

**Recommended sources:**
- Cochrane reviews on anxiety treatments
- American Psychological Association Division 12 (Society of Clinical Psychology) — maintains lists of empirically supported treatments
- “Treatment of anxiety disorders” systematic reviews from 2020-2025

**Note:** The field currently lacks strong head-to-head comparisons of somatic vs cognitive approaches. This is a known gap in the literature, not a failure of research. The article acknowledges this appropriately.

**Estimated effort:** 3-4 hours

## Important Disclaimer

The current draft represents **evidence-informed clinical writing** — synthesizing established science into accessible form. It is NOT yet **evidence-based medicine (EBM)** — which requires systematic review methodology.

**For clinical publication:** Consider submitting for peer review or having a subject matter expert review.

**For public dissemination:** The current draft is appropriate for educated readers but should include a disclaimer that it's informational, not medical advice.

## Citation Style Notes

The article uses **numbered citations** for readability. For publication, consider:
- APA format (most common in psychology)
- Vancouver (if medical/clinical audience)
- Footnotes (if online publication with link capability)
