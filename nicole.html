Hi, I'm Nicole

On the outside, it probably looks like you're managing everything. You're the one people count on, the one who keeps going, keeps giving, and somehow always figures it out. But inside, you've got parts of yourself that are exhausted, anxious, overwhelmed, or won't allow your body to slow down no matter how hard you try. I specialize in helping adults navigate anxiety, trauma, nervous system dysregulation, perfectionism, life transitions, and the unique challenges that often come with neurodivergence. Together, we'll make sense of these parts and what's happening beneath the surface so you can move from simply surviving to feeling more grounded, connected, and at home in yourself.

My Style 🤘

My style? Casual, warm, non-judgmental, and genuinely human. I'm the therapist who's often running in with the biggest Diet Coke I could find, probably apologizing because I cut it a little too close. I laugh easily, I swear like a sailor (when it's appropriate), and I don't believe therapy has to feel stiff or clinical to be deeply effective.

I care deeply about creating a space where everyone feels genuinely welcome—all identities, all backgrounds, all family structures, all ways of being. You belong here exactly as you are.

And while the atmosphere is relaxed, the work is anything but casual. My background in science and my love of understanding the brain and nervous system shape everything I do. I approach life from a secular, science-informed perspective. I'm open to exploring both conventional and complementary treatments when there is good evidence to support their effectiveness to tailor my approach to the unique person sitting across from me.

My Story

I'm a bit of an unusual therapist. Before I became a counselor, I spent years as a college professor with a PhD in Biology. I taught future healthcare providers about the nervous system, stress responses, and the intricate connections between brain and body. I still teach — I love it, and I love working with young adults who are navigating their own big questions about life.

All that scientific knowledge became deeply personal when I started to process my own trauma and anxiety — and when I learned that my child is neurodivergent. I knew exactly what was happening in the nervous system, but knowing wasn't enough. I wanted to understand my daughter, to understand myself, and what I discovered was that therapy is incredibly powerful.

That's what drew me to become a clinician. I went back to school for counseling, and now I help others the way therapy helped me — at the intersection of hard science and genuine compassion. I still teach healthcare professionals, because I believe the best therapists never stop learning. Whether you're an individual struggling with anxiety, a couple trying to reconnect, or a parent navigating the complexities of raising a neurodivergent kid, we'll use evidence-based approaches that actually work.

How I Work

Science-based, relationally-focused, and deeply human.

🧠 My Integrative Approach

I draw from multiple evidence-based modalities tailored to your needs — and as a trained scientist, research isn't just a buzzword for me. It's the foundation. These are just some of the approaches I use:

  • ✓ IFS (Internal Family Systems) — Understanding your inner parts
  • ✓ Somatic Therapies — Working with the body to heal the mind
  • ✓ ERP (Exposure & Response Prevention) — For OCD and anxiety
  • ✓ Attachment Theory — Understanding relationship patterns
  • ✓ Gottman Method — Research-based couples therapy
  • ✓ DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy) — Distress tolerance and emotional regulation
  • ✓ CBT & ACT — Practical tools for thoughts and behaviors

And more — we'll find what works for your unique brain and needs.

❤️ What to Expect

Our work together is:

  • ✓ Casual & warm — This isn't stuffy clinical theater. I'm real, I'm human, and yes, I swear sometimes. Come as you are.
  • ✓ Evidence-based first — As a trained scientist, research is the foundation of everything we do. Warmth matters — but it's always backed by approaches that actually work.
  • ✓ Non-judgmental — Whatever you're dealing with, I've heard it. No judgment, ever.
  • ✓ Collaborative — You're in charge of your healing. We go at your pace.
  • ✓ Trauma-informed — Safety and consent always come first.
  • ✓ All are welcome — All identities, all backgrounds, all relationship structures, all family types. You belong here exactly as you are.
  • ✓ Funny & human — We can laugh. We can be real. Therapy doesn't have to be somber to be effective.
